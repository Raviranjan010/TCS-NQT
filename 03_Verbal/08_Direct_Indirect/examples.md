# Direct & Indirect Speech — Worked Examples

This file contains 5 worked examples of Direct to Indirect (Reported) Speech conversions. Each example includes a step-by-step breakdown of tense shifts, pronoun changes, and time/place marker updates.

---

## Direct to Indirect Conversion Table

| Q# | Sentence Type | Direct Speech | Reporting Verb | Pronoun Shift (SON Rule) | Tense Backshift | Place/Time Marker Shift | Indirect Speech (Correct Answer) |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **1** | Assertive | The developer said, "I **am writing** a new script **today**." | said (Past) | I $\rightarrow$ he | Present Continuous $\rightarrow$ Past Continuous | today $\rightarrow$ that day | The developer said that he **was writing** a new script **that day**. |
| **2** | Assertive | She said to me, "We **have completed** the design." | said to $\rightarrow$ told | We $\rightarrow$ they | Present Perfect $\rightarrow$ Past Perfect | None | She told me that they **had completed** the design. |
| **3** | Interrogative | The lead engineer asked, "Are **you** coming **tomorrow**?" | asked (Past) | you $\rightarrow$ I/he/they (contextual) | Present Continuous $\rightarrow$ Past Continuous | tomorrow $\rightarrow$ the next day | The lead engineer asked if I **was coming the next day**. |
| **4** | Interrogative (Wh-) | He asked, "**Where** **did** you buy this laptop?" | asked (Past) | you $\rightarrow$ I | Simple Past $\rightarrow$ Past Perfect | this $\rightarrow$ that | He asked where I **had bought that** laptop. |
| **5** | Imperative | The director said, "**Please** submit the reports." | said $\rightarrow$ requested | None (implied you) | Infinitive (to + V1) applied | None | The director requested me **to submit** the reports. |

## Detailed Step-by-Step Explanations

### Example 1 (Assertive Sentence with Time Shift)
*   **Direct:** The developer said, "I **am writing** a new script **today**."
*   **Analysis:**
    1.  **Reporting Verb:** "said" is in the past tense, so we must backshift the tenses and time markers in the quotation.
    2.  **Pronoun Shift:** The first-person pronoun "**I**" refers to "The developer" (third person singular, male/female). We shift it to "**he**" (or "she").
    3.  **Tense Backshift:** The Present Continuous "**am writing**" shifts to the Past Continuous "**was writing**".
    4.  **Time Marker Shift:** The time word "**today**" shifts to "**that day**".
*   **Indirect:** The developer said that he **was writing** a new script **that day**.

### Example 2 (Assertive Sentence with "said to")
*   **Direct:** She said to me, "We **have completed** the design."
*   **Analysis:**
    1.  **Reporting Verb:** "said to" followed by an object ("me") changes to "**told**".
    2.  **Pronoun Shift:** The first-person plural "**We**" shifts to the third-person plural "**they**" (representing the group she belongs to).
    3.  **Tense Backshift:** The Present Perfect "**have completed**" shifts to the Past Perfect "**had completed**".
*   **Indirect:** She told me that they **had completed** the design.

### Example 3 (Interrogative Sentence - Yes/No Question)
*   **Direct:** The lead engineer asked, "Are **you** coming **tomorrow**?"
*   **Analysis:**
    1.  **Sentence Type:** This is a Yes/No question, so we use the conjunction "**if**" or "**whether**" to connect the clauses. The reporting verb "**asked**" remains.
    2.  **Pronoun Shift:** The second-person pronoun "**you**" refers to the listener (assumed first-person "**I**" here).
    3.  **Tense Backshift:** The interrogative clause structure changes to assertive, and the tense shifts from Present Continuous ("Are you coming") to Past Continuous ("**I was coming**").
    4.  **Time Marker Shift:** The word "**tomorrow**" shifts to "**the next day**" or "the following day".
*   **Indirect:** The lead engineer asked if I **was coming the next day**.

### Example 4 (Interrogative Sentence - Wh- Question)
*   **Direct:** He asked, "**Where** **did** you buy this laptop?"
*   **Analysis:**
    1.  **Sentence Type:** This is a "Wh-" question, so the question word "**where**" acts as the connector. We do not use "that" or "if".
    2.  **Pronoun Shift:** "**you**" shifts to "**I**".
    3.  **Tense Backshift:** The question is in the Simple Past ("did buy"), which shifts to the Past Perfect "**had bought**".
    4.  **Place Marker Shift:** "**this**" shifts to "**that**".
*   **Indirect:** He asked where I **had bought that** laptop.

### Example 5 (Imperative Sentence - Request)
*   **Direct:** The director said, "**Please** submit the reports."
*   **Analysis:**
    1.  **Sentence Type:** The word "Please" identifies this as a request. We change the reporting verb "said" to "**requested**".
    2.  **Infinitive Link:** Connect the reported clause using an infinitive: "**to**" + base verb ("**submit**").
*   **Indirect:** The director requested me **to submit** the reports.
