# Active & Passive Voice — Worked Examples

This file contains 5 worked examples of Active and Passive Voice conversions. Each example features a detailed analysis of the subject, verb, and object, along with a step-by-step breakdown of how the sentence shifts using the standard tense conversion rules.

---

## Conversion Breakdown Table

| Q# | Tense | Active Voice | Subject (S) | Verb (V) | Object (O) | Passive Structure Applied | Passive Voice (Correct Answer) |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **1** | Simple Present | The manager conducts the weekly meeting. | The manager | conducts | the weekly meeting | O + is/am/are + V3 + by S | The weekly meeting **is conducted** by the manager. |
| **2** | Present Continuous | The developer is writing the database script. | The developer | is writing | the database script | O + is/am/are + being + V3 + by S | The database script **is being written** by the developer. |
| **3** | Simple Past | The committee approved the budget proposal. | The committee | approved | the budget proposal | O + was/were + V3 + by S | The budget proposal **was approved** by the committee. |
| **4** | Present Perfect | The QA team has tested the new release. | The QA team | has tested | the new release | O + has/have + been + V3 + by S | The new release **has been tested** by the QA team. |
| **5** | Imperative | Close the terminal window. | (You - implied) | Close | the terminal window | Let + O + be + V3 | **Let** the terminal window **be closed**. |

## Detailed Step-by-Step Explanations

### Example 1 (Simple Present Tense)
*   **Active:** "The manager conducts the weekly meeting."
*   **Analysis:**
    1.  **Identify Subject & Object:** The subject is "The manager" (singular), and the object is "the weekly meeting" (singular).
    2.  **Identify Tense:** The verb "conducts" is in the Simple Present tense.
    3.  **Apply Rule:** In the passive voice, the auxiliary verb for a singular object in the Simple Present is "**is**", followed by the past participle (V3) of "conduct" $\rightarrow$ "**conducted**".
*   **Passive:** "The weekly meeting **is conducted** by the manager."

### Example 2 (Present Continuous Tense)
*   **Active:** "The developer is writing the database script."
*   **Analysis:**
    1.  **Identify Subject & Object:** Subject is "The developer", object is "the database script".
    2.  **Identify Tense:** The verb "is writing" is in the Present Continuous tense.
    3.  **Apply Rule:** The continuous aspect requires adding "**being**" after the auxiliary verb. Since "the database script" is singular, we use "**is being**" + V3 of write $\rightarrow$ "**written**".
*   **Passive:** "The database script **is being written** by the developer."

### Example 3 (Simple Past Tense)
*   **Active:** "The committee approved the budget proposal."
*   **Analysis:**
    1.  **Identify Subject & Object:** Subject is "The committee", object is "the budget proposal".
    2.  **Identify Tense:** The verb "approved" is in the Simple Past tense.
    3.  **Apply Rule:** For a singular object in the Simple Past, we use the auxiliary "**was**" + V3 of approve $\rightarrow$ "**approved**".
*   **Passive:** "The budget proposal **was approved** by the committee."

### Example 4 (Present Perfect Tense)
*   **Active:** "The QA team has tested the new release."
*   **Analysis:**
    1.  **Identify Subject & Object:** Subject is "The QA team", object is "the new release".
    2.  **Identify Tense:** "has tested" is in the Present Perfect tense.
    3.  **Apply Rule:** The perfect aspect requires adding "**been**" after the auxiliary verb. Since "the new release" is singular, we use "**has been**" + V3 of test $\rightarrow$ "**tested**".
*   **Passive:** "The new release **has been tested** by the QA team."

### Example 5 (Imperative Sentence - Order/Command)
*   **Active:** "Close the terminal window."
*   **Analysis:**
    1.  **Identify Structure:** This is an imperative sentence expressing a command, with the subject "you" implied.
    2.  **Apply Rule:** The formula for an imperative command in the passive voice is: `Let + Object + be + Past Participle (V3)`.
    3.  **Application:** The object is "the terminal window", and the verb is "close" (V3 is "closed").
*   **Passive:** "**Let** the terminal window **be closed**."
