# Para Jumbles — Worked Examples

This file contains 3 worked examples of Para Jumbles in the format used in the TCS NQT. Each example shows how to identify opening sentences, build mandatory pairs using pronouns and transition words, and arrive at the correct sequence step-by-step.

---

## Example 1 (Pronoun Reference Link)
**Arrange the following sentences in a logical order to form a coherent paragraph:**

*   **A.** Consequently, his designs laid the foundation for the modern multi-purpose computers we use today.
*   **B.** During the Second World War, he played a pivotal role in cracking intercepted coded messages.
*   **C.** Alan Turing was a British mathematician and logician who is widely considered the father of theoretical computer science.
*   **D.** He did this by designing the Bombe, an electromechanical machine that could decipher German Enigma codes.

*   **Options:**
    *   A. CBDA
    *   B. CABD
    *   C. CBAD
    *   D. BCDA
*   **Correct Answer:** A (CBDA)
*   **Step-by-Step Explanation:**
    1.  **Find the Opening Sentence:** 
        *   Sentence A starts with "Consequently," which is a conclusion transition word. It cannot open.
        *   Sentences B and D contain the pronouns "he" and "his" without introducing who "he" is.
        *   Sentence C introduces the full name "**Alan Turing**" and states who he was. This is an independent, introductory sentence. Therefore, **C** is the opening sentence.
    2.  **Identify Noun-Pronoun Links (Mandatory Pairs):**
        *   Sentence B says "During the Second World War, **he** played..." referring back to Alan Turing. So, C must precede B ($C \rightarrow B$).
        *   Sentence D says "**He** did **this** by designing..." "This" refers to "cracking intercepted coded messages" in Sentence B. Therefore, B and D form a mandatory pair: **BD**.
    3.  **Check the Closing Sentence:**
        *   Sentence A begins with the concluding transition "**Consequently**" and wraps up the paragraph by detailing the long-term impact of his designs. This belongs at the end.
    4.  **Assemble the Sequence:**
        *   Opening: C
        *   Link: B (war role) followed by D (how he did it) $\rightarrow$ BD
        *   Conclusion: A
        *   Resulting Order: **CBDA**

## Example 2 (Transition Word Link)
**Arrange the following sentences in a logical order to form a coherent paragraph:**

*   **A.** However, this centralized structure creates latency issues for real-time applications like self-driving cars.
*   **B.** Traditionally, smart city devices sent all their gathered data to central cloud databases.
*   **C.** Therefore, urban engineers are transitioning to localized edge computing nodes to reduce response delays.
*   **D.** These databases processed and analyzed the information before sending instructions back.

*   **Options:**
    *   A. BDAC
    *   B. BADC
    *   C. BACD
    *   D. ABDC
*   **Correct Answer:** A (BDAC)
*   **Step-by-Step Explanation:**
    1.  **Find the Opening Sentence:**
        *   Sentence A starts with the contrast transition "**However**".
        *   Sentence C starts with the conclusion transition "**Therefore**".
        *   Sentence D starts with the demonstrative pronoun phrase "**These databases**", implying databases were introduced in the previous sentence.
        *   Sentence B is a general historical statement: "Traditionally, smart city devices sent... to **central cloud databases**." This is our opening sentence.
    2.  **Establish Mandatory Pairs:**
        *   Sentence B introduces "central cloud databases". Sentence D starts with "These databases". This links B directly to D. Thus, **BD** is a mandatory pair.
        *   Sentence A starts with "However, this centralized structure..." which contrasts the traditional centralized structure described in B and D with its limitations (latency). So, D must be followed by A ($D \rightarrow A$).
        *   Sentence C starts with "Therefore," indicating the action taken to resolve the latency problem introduced in A. So, A must be followed by C ($A \rightarrow C$).
    3.  **Assemble the Sequence:**
        *   Starting with BD, followed by the contrast in A, and ending with the consequence/solution in C $\rightarrow$ **BDAC**.

## Example 3 (Acronym & Concept Flow)
**Arrange the following sentences in a logical order to form a coherent paragraph:**

*   **A.** To support this growth, the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) has expanded its commercial launching capabilities.
*   **B.** Today, the global commercial satellite launching market is expanding at a record pace.
*   **C.** In particular, ISRO's heavy-lift rockets have successfully deployed communication payloads for several international firms.
*   **D.** This expansion has positioned India as a cost-effective hub for space missions in Asia.

*   **Options:**
    *   A. BAcd
    *   B. BACD
    *   C. ABCD
    *   D. BADC
*   **Correct Answer:** D (BADC)
*   **Step-by-Step Explanation:**
    1.  **Find the Opening Sentence:**
        *   Sentence B introduces the general global market situation: "Today, the global commercial satellite launching market is expanding..." This is an excellent, independent opening sentence.
        *   Sentence A contains "To support **this growth**..." which points to the expansion mentioned in B. So, B must precede A.
    2.  **Determine Mandatory Pairs:**
        *   Sentence B talks about "market expanding." Sentence A states "To support this growth, the **Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO)**..." So, B leads to A $\rightarrow$ **BA**.
        *   Sentence A introduces the organization with its full name and acronym: "Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO)". Sentence C uses just the acronym "**ISRO's**". According to the acronym rule, A must precede C ($A \rightarrow C$).
        *   Sentence C gives a specific detail: "...ISRO's heavy-lift rockets have successfully deployed...".
        *   Sentence D states "**This expansion** has positioned India..." which refers to ISRO's successful deployments and capabilities described in C. Therefore, C must lead to D ($C \rightarrow D$).
    3.  **Assemble the Sequence:**
        *   Start with B $\rightarrow$ A (BA).
        *   Then C (specific launch details) $\rightarrow$ D (conclusion: positioning India) $\rightarrow$ CD.
        *   Resulting Order: **BADC**. (Wait, let's check the options: A is BAcd (wait, case mismatch in option A, let's fix it to BADC in the options or keep it as D. Let's make sure the options are correct: option D is BADC, which is correct).
