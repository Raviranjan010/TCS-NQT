# Sentence Improvement — Worked Examples

This file contains 5 worked examples of Sentence Improvement in the exact format used in the TCS NQT. Each question features a sentence with an underlined phrase that may be grammatically incorrect, followed by improvement options and a detailed explanation of the grammatical rules applied.

---

## Example 1 (Dangling Modifiers)
**Improve the underlined part of the sentence. If the sentence is correct, select "No improvement required".**

"<u>Having completed the coding phase</u>, the application was submitted by the developers for testing."

*   **Options:**
    *   A. Having completed the coding phase, the developers submitted the application
    *   B. After completing the coding phase, the application was submitted
    *   C. On completion of the coding phase, the application was submitted by the developers
    *   D. No improvement required
*   **Correct Answer:** A
*   **Explanation:**
    *   **Rule:** A participial phrase at the beginning of a sentence must modify the subject of the main clause. In the original sentence, "Having completed the coding phase" modifies "the application" (which cannot code). The subject of the main clause must be the logical actor: "the developers."
    *   **Option Evaluation:** Option A changes the passive clause so that "the developers" becomes the subject, resolving the dangling modifier.
    *   **Corrected Sentence:** "**Having completed the coding phase, the developers submitted the application** for testing."

## Example 2 (Parallel Structure)
**Improve the underlined part of the sentence. If the sentence is correct, select "No improvement required".**

"The consultant's duties include analyzing business workflows, writing reports, and <u>to present recommendations to the stakeholders</u>."

*   **Options:**
    *   A. to present recommendations for the stakeholders
    *   B. presenting recommendations to the stakeholders
    *   C. presentation of recommendations to stakeholders
    *   D. No improvement required
*   **Correct Answer:** B
*   **Explanation:**
    *   **Rule:** Parallelism dictates that elements in a series must have the same grammatical form. Here, the first two items are gerund phrases: *analyzing business workflows* and *writing reports*. The third item must also be a gerund phrase to maintain parallel structure.
    *   **Option Evaluation:** Option B converts the infinitive phrase "to present..." into the gerund phrase "**presenting**...", matching the preceding terms.
    *   **Corrected Sentence:** "The consultant's duties include analyzing business workflows, writing reports, and **presenting recommendations to the stakeholders**."

## Example 3 (Redundancy)
**Improve the underlined part of the sentence. If the sentence is correct, select "No improvement required".**

"Due to a technical glitch, the system administrator had to <u>revert back to the previous version</u> of the database."

*   **Options:**
    *   A. revert to the previous version
    *   B. revert back again to the previous version
    *   C. reverse back to the previous version
    *   D. No improvement required
*   **Correct Answer:** A
*   **Explanation:**
    *   **Rule:** Redundancy occurs when words with duplicate meanings are used together. The verb "revert" means to return to a previous state, practice, or topic. Adding the word "back" is redundant.
    *   **Option Evaluation:** Option A removes the word "back", leaving the sentence concise and grammatically correct.
    *   **Corrected Sentence:** "Due to a technical glitch, the system administrator had to **revert to the previous version** of the database."

## Example 4 (Pronoun-Antecedent Agreement)
**Improve the underlined part of the sentence. If the sentence is correct, select "No improvement required".**

"Each of the project coordinators must submit <u>their status report</u> by Friday evening."

*   **Options:**
    *   A. there status report
    *   B. his or her status report
    *   C. their status reports
    *   D. No improvement required
*   **Correct Answer:** B
*   **Explanation:**
    *   **Rule:** Singular indefinite pronouns like *each*, *every*, *everyone*, *someone*, and *nobody* require singular verbs and singular possessive pronouns (*his*, *her*, *his or her*) for formal agreement, despite the plural noun in the prepositional phrase ("of the project coordinators").
    *   **Option Evaluation:** "Each" is the singular subject, so the possessive pronoun must be singular: "**his or her**".
    *   **Corrected Sentence:** "Each of the project coordinators must submit **his or her status report** by Friday evening."

## Example 5 (Tense Consistency)
**Improve the underlined part of the sentence. If the sentence is correct, select "No improvement required".**

"The senior engineer <u>had worked in the firm since five years</u> when the organization decided to upgrade its core technology stack."

*   **Options:**
    *   A. has worked in the firm since five years
    *   B. had been working in the firm for five years
    *   C. was working in the firm for five years
    *   D. No improvement required
*   **Correct Answer:** B
*   **Explanation:**
    *   **Rule:** When describing a continuous action that was ongoing in the past up to a certain point, use the Past Perfect Continuous tense (*had been working*). Additionally, the preposition "for" is used to show a duration of time ("five years"), not "since" (which marks a starting point).
    *   **Option Evaluation:** Option B correctly combines the past perfect continuous tense with the preposition "for" to represent the ongoing five-year period of work leading up to the technology upgrade.
    *   **Corrected Sentence:** "The senior engineer **had been working in the firm for five years** when the organization decided to upgrade its core technology stack."
