# Data Interpretation - Concept Guide

## 1. Introduction to Data Interpretation (DI)
Data Interpretation tests your ability to extract meaningful insights from visual charts (tables, bar graphs, line charts, and pie charts).

### Core Mathematical Calculations in DI:
1.  **Percentage Growth:**
    $$\text{Growth \%} = \frac{\text{New Value} - \text{Old Value}}{\text{Old Value}} \times 100$$
2.  **Ratio comparisons:** Simplifying $\frac{X}{Y}$ for multiple periods.

### Immediate Worked Example:
Sales of a company rose from ₹40 Crore in 2024 to ₹50 Crore in 2025. Find the percentage growth.
*   **Step 1:** Difference $= 50 - 40 = 10$.
*   **Step 2:** Apply formula:
    $$\text{Growth} = \frac{10}{40} \times 100 = 25\%$$

## 2. Pie Chart Angle Calculations
A pie chart represents a whole ($100\%$) distributed over $360^\circ$.

### Derivation of Degree to Percent conversion:
$$100\% = 360^\circ \implies 1\% = 3.6^\circ$$
$$\text{Angle in Degrees} = \text{Percentage Value} \times 3.6$$

### Immediate Worked Example:
If food expenses account for $20\%$ of a family budget, find the corresponding sector angle.
*   **Apply conversion:**
    $$\text{Angle} = 20 \times 3.6 = 72^\circ$$
