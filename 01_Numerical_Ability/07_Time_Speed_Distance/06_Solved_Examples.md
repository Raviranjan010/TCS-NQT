# Time Speed Distance - Solved Examples

## 🟢 Easy-Level Examples
### Example 1. Simple relative speed
*   **Question:** A thief is spotted by a cop at 200m. The thief runs at 10 km/hr, cop chases at 11 km/hr. Find the distance thief ran before being caught.
*   **Solution:**
    *   Relative speed $= 11 - 10 = 1$ km/hr $= 5/18$ m/s.
    *   Time to catch $= 200 / (5/18) = 720$ seconds.
    *   Thief's distance $= 10 \times (5/18) \times 720 = 2000$ meters.

## 🟡 Medium-Level Examples
### Example 2. Average Speed with uneven segments
*   **Question:** A car covers 1/3 of the distance at 20 km/hr, 1/3 at 30 km/hr, and 1/3 at 60 km/hr. Find the average speed.
*   **Solution:** Let total distance be 3d.
    *   Total Time $= \frac{d}{20} + \frac{d}{30} + \frac{d}{60} = d\left(\frac{3+2+1}{60}\right) = \frac{6d}{60} = \frac{d}{10}$.
    *   Average Speed $= 3d / (d/10) = 30$ km/hr.

## 🔴 Hard-Level Examples
### Example 3. Escalator problem
*   **Question:** A man walks up a moving escalator in 30 seconds. If he walks twice as fast, it takes him 20 seconds. How long would it take if he just stood on the escalator?
*   **Solution:** Let escalator steps be $E$. Speed of escalator $= v_e$, walking speed $= v_w$.
    *   $E = 30(v_w + v_e)$
    *   $E = 20(2v_w + v_e) \implies 30v_w + 30v_e = 40v_w + 20v_e \implies 10v_e = 10v_w \implies v_e = v_w$.
    *   So, $E = 30(v_e + v_e) = 60v_e$.
    *   Standing time $= E / v_e = 60$ seconds.
