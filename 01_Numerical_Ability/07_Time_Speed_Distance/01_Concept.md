# Time Speed Distance - Concept Guide

## 1. Core Speed Formula
Speed ($S$) is the rate at which distance ($D$) is covered over time ($T$).
$$S = \frac{D}{T}$$

### Unit Conversion Derivation:
*   Convert km/hr to m/s:
    $$1\text{ km/hr} = \frac{1000\text{ m}}{3600\text{ s}} = \frac{5}{18}\text{ m/s}$$
*   Convert m/s to km/hr:
    $$1\text{ m/s} = \frac{18}{5}\text{ km/hr}$$

### Immediate Worked Example:
A car travels at $72$ km/hr. Find its speed in m/s.
*   **Step 1:** Multiply by $5/18$:
    $$72 \times \frac{5}{18} = 4 \times 5 = 20\text{ m/s}$$

## 2. Average Speed
Average speed is defined as the total distance divided by the total time.

### Derivation for Equal Distances:
Let a journey be split into two equal distances $d$, covered at speeds $x$ and $y$.
*   Time $T_1 = \frac{d}{x}$
*   Time $T_2 = \frac{d}{y}$
*   $$\text{Average Speed} = \frac{\text{Total Distance}}{\text{Total Time}} = \frac{2d}{\frac{d}{x} + \frac{d}{y}} = \frac{2}{\frac{x+y}{xy}} = \frac{2xy}{x+y}$$

### Immediate Worked Example:
A boy goes to school at 20 km/hr and returns at 30 km/hr. Find his average speed.
*   **Step 1:** Apply formula:
    $$\text{Average Speed} = \frac{2 \times 20 \times 30}{20 + 30} = \frac{1200}{50} = 24\text{ km/hr}$$
