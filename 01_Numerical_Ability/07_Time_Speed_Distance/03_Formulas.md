# Time Speed Distance - Formulas & Derivations

### 1. Train Crossing a Pole/Platform
*   **Crossing a Pole:** $\text{Time} = \frac{L_{train}}{S_{train}}$
    *   *Derivation:* The pole has negligible width, so distance to cross is the length of the train.
*   **Crossing a Platform/Bridge:** $\text{Time} = \frac{L_{train} + L_{platform}}{S_{train}}$
    *   *Worked Example:* A 200m long train travels at 36 km/hr (10 m/s). Find time to cross a 300m bridge.
        $$\text{Time} = \frac{200 + 300}{10} = 50\text{ seconds}$$

### 2. Boats and Streams
*   Downstream Speed: $D = U + V$
*   Upstream Speed: $U_{up} = U - V$
    (where $U = \text{speed of boat in still water}$, $V = \text{speed of stream}$)
    *   *Derivation of boat speed:* $U = \frac{D + U_{up}}{2}$
