# Simple and Compound Interest - Practice Advanced

**Q1.** A person invests ₹12,000 in two schemes at SI. Scheme A offers $8\%$ p.a. and Scheme B offers $10\%$ p.a. If the total interest at the end of 2 years is ₹2,160, find the amount invested in Scheme A.
> 🎯 Hint: Use the weighted average/alligation method on interest rates.
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>
*   **Approach:** Alligation.
*   **Step-by-step:** Overall interest per year $= 2160 / 2 = ₹1,080$.
    $$\text{Overall Rate} = \frac{1080}{12000} \times 100 = 9\%$$
    Ratio of investment in A and B (rates 8% and 10% with mean 9%):
    $$\text{Ratio} = |10 - 9| : |8 - 9| = 1 : 1$$
    Investment in A $= 1/2 \times 12000 = ₹6,000$.
*   **Answer:** ₹6,000
*   **Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐☆ | **Target Time:** 75s
</details>
