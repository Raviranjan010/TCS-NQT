# Simple and Compound Interest - Interview Questions

**Q1.** Explain the visual difference between the growth curves of Simple Interest and Compound Interest.
*   **Answer:** Simple Interest follows a linear growth pattern ($Y = mX + C$), representing an arithmetic progression. Compound Interest follows an exponential growth curve ($Y = a(b)^X$), representing a geometric progression where the interest of previous periods becomes part of the base for subsequent calculations.
