# Simple and Compound Interest - Formulas & Derivations

### 1. Difference between CI and SI for 2 Years
$$\text{Diff}_2 = P\left(\frac{R}{100}\right)^2$$
*   **Derivation:**
    $$\text{SI for 2 years} = \frac{2PR}{100}$$
    $$\text{CI for 2 years} = P\left(1+\frac{R}{100}\right)^2 - P = P\left(\frac{2R}{100} + \frac{R^2}{10000}\right)$$
    $$\text{Difference} = \text{CI} - \text{SI} = P\left(\frac{R}{100}\right)^2$$
*   **Worked Example:** Principal ₹5,000, Rate $10\%$, Time 2 years.
    $$\text{Diff}_2 = 5000 \times \left(\frac{10}{100}\right)^2 = 5000 \times 0.01 = ₹50$$

### 2. Difference between CI and SI for 3 Years
$$\text{Diff}_3 = P\left(\frac{R}{100}\right)^2 \left(3 + \frac{R}{100}\right)$$
*   **Worked Example:** Principal ₹10,000, Rate $10\%$, Time 3 years.
    $$\text{Diff}_3 = 10000 \times (0.1)^2 \times (3 + 0.1) = 10000 \times 0.01 \times 3.1 = ₹310$$
