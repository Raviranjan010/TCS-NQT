# Simple and Compound Interest - Past Year Questions (PYQs)

### PYQ 1. Growth multiplier at CI
*   **Question:** A sum of money becomes 3 times itself in 4 years at compound interest. In how many years will it become 27 times itself?
*   **Pattern ID:** SICI_GROWTH_PYQ
*   **Approach:** $3^1$ times in 4 years $\implies 27 = 3^3$ times in $3 \times 4 = 12$ years.
*   **Solution:** Since compounding is exponential, $A = P(1+i)^T$. $3P = P(1+i)^4 \implies (1+i)^4 = 3$. For $27P$, we need $(1+i)^T = 27 = 3^3 = ((1+i)^4)^3 = (1+i)^{12} \implies T = 12$.
*   **Shortcut:** Power multiplier: $27 = 3^3 \implies \text{Time} = 3 \times 4 = 12$ years.
*   **Variation & Trap:** If the question asked at SI, the formula is $A = P(1 + RT/100)$, which is linear.
