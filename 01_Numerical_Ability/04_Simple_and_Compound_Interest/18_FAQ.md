# Simple and Compound Interest - FAQ

**Q1. Can we apply successive percentage formula to find compound interest?**
*   **Answer:** Yes, CI is mathematically equivalent to successive percentage increases. For example, a 2-year CI at 10% is equivalent to two successive increases of 10%, giving $10 + 10 + 1 = 21\%$.

**Q2. What is the difference between simple interest and compound interest?**
*   **Answer:** Simple interest is calculated only on the original principal, whereas compound interest is calculated on the principal plus the accumulated interest of previous periods.

**Q3. How do we calculate effective interest rate?**
*   **Answer:** For compounding cycle of $n$ times a year, $\text{Effective Rate} = \left(1 + \frac{R}{100n}\right)^n - 1$ as a percentage.

**Q4. What is the Rule of 72?**
*   **Answer:** A quick shortcut to estimate doubling time at compound interest: $\text{Years to double} \approx 72 / R$.
