# Time and Work - FAQ

**Q1. How does the LCM method work?**
*   **Answer:** Assume the total work is the LCM of the times taken by individuals. Efficiency is then calculated as $\text{Total Work} / \text{Time}$, resulting in easy-to-use integer rates.

**Q2. What is negative efficiency?**
*   **Answer:** The rate of work of an agent who destroys the work, like an emptying pipe draining a reservoir.

**Q3. How to solve men-women group equivalence questions?**
*   **Answer:** Establish an efficiency ratio between one man and one woman (e.g., $1M = 2W$) using the given equations, convert all entities into a single unit, and use the $M_1 D_1 = M_2 D_2$ formula.

**Q4. What happens when someone leaves the work in between?**
*   **Answer:** Use the equation: $\sum (\text{Individual Efficiency} \times \text{Days Worked}) = \text{Total Work}$.
