# Time and Work - Concept Guide

## 1. Core Rule: Work & Days
Work is the product of rate (efficiency) and time. If a person completes a task in $D$ days, their rate is $\frac{1}{D}$ of the task per day.

### Derivation of Combined Work:
If A takes $x$ days and B takes $y$ days:
*   A's 1-day rate $= \frac{1}{x}$
*   B's 1-day rate $= \frac{1}{y}$
*   Combined 1-day rate $= \frac{1}{x} + \frac{1}{y} = \frac{x+y}{xy}$
*   Total days $T = \frac{1}{\text{Combined Rate}} = \frac{xy}{x+y}$

### Immediate Worked Example:
A can build a wall in 10 days and B can do it in 15 days. How long will they take together?
*   **Step 1:** Apply formula:
    $$T = \frac{10 \times 15}{10 + 15} = \frac{150}{25} = 6\text{ days}$$

---

## 2. LCM Method (Fastest Approach)
Instead of dealing with fractions, assume the total work is the LCM of the individual days.

### Immediate Worked Example:
A takes 12 days, B takes 18 days. Find their combined time.
*   **Step 1:** Assume total work $= \text{LCM}(12, 18) = 36$ units.
*   **Step 2:** Find efficiencies (work units per day):
    $$\text{Efficiency of A} = 36 / 12 = 3\text{ units/day}$$
    $$\text{Efficiency of B} = 36 / 18 = 2\text{ units/day}$$
*   **Step 3:** Combined efficiency $= 3 + 2 = 5$ units/day.
*   **Step 4:** Time taken $= 36 / 5 = 7.2$ days.
