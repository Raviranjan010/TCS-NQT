# Time and Work - Formulas & Derivations

### 1. The Men-Days-Hours Formula
If $M_1$ men work $H_1$ hours per day for $D_1$ days to do $W_1$ work, and $M_2$ men work $H_2$ hours per day for $D_2$ days to do $W_2$ work:
$$\frac{M_1 D_1 H_1}{W_1} = \frac{M_2 D_2 H_2}{W_2}$$
*   **Derivation:**
    $$\text{Work} \propto \text{Men} \times \text{Days} \times \text{Hours} \implies W = k \cdot M \cdot D \cdot H \implies \frac{M \cdot D \cdot H}{W} = \frac{1}{k}$$
*   **Worked Example:** If 15 men working 8 hours a day can complete a work in 20 days, how many days will 20 men working 6 hours a day take?
    $$\frac{15 \times 20 \times 8}{1} = \frac{20 \times D_2 \times 6}{1} \implies 2400 = 120 D_2 \implies D_2 = 20\text{ days}$$
