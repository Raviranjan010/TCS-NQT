# Probability - FAQ

**Q1. What is the difference between independent and mutually exclusive events?**
*   **Answer:** Independent events concern the occurrence of one not affecting the other (e.g., throwing two dice). Mutually exclusive events cannot happen simultaneously (e.g., pulling a card that is both a King and an Ace).

**Q2. What is Bayes' Theorem?**
*   **Answer:** A mathematical formula for determining conditional probability of an event, based on prior knowledge of conditions that might be related to the event.

**Q3. How to calculate "at least one" probability?**
*   **Answer:** Compute the probability of the event not occurring at all, and subtract it from 1: $1 - P(\text{None})$.

**Q4. What is the probability of a leap year having 53 Sundays?**
*   **Answer:** $2/7$ (since there are 2 extra days which can form 7 pairs, 2 of which contain Sunday).
