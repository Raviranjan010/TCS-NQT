# Probability - Formulas & Derivations

### 1. Conditional Probability
$$P(A|B) = \frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)}$$
*   **Derivation:** The probability of A occurring, given that B has already occurred, restricts the sample space to B. Within B, only outcomes in $A \cap B$ are favorable.
*   **Worked Example:** In a class, $40\%$ study Math and $25\%$ study both Math and Science. Find the probability that a student studies Science, given they study Math.
    $$P(\text{Science} | \text{Math}) = \frac{P(\text{Science} \cap \text{Math})}{P(\text{Math})} = \frac{0.25}{0.40} = 0.625$$
