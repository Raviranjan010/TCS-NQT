# Ratio and Proportion - Practice Advanced

**Q1.** A, B, and C started a business. A invested 1/3 of the total capital, B invested as much as A and C together. If the annual profit was ₹7,200, find C's share.
> 🎯 Hint: Let total capital be 6 units to avoid fractions.
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>
*   **Approach:** Ratio balancing.
*   **Step-by-step:** Let total capital $= 6$.
    *   A's share $= 1/3 \times 6 = 2$.
    *   B's share $= \text{A} + \text{C} \implies B = 2 + C$.
    *   Since $A + B + C = 6 \implies 2 + (2+C) + C = 6 \implies 2C = 2 \implies C = 1$.
    *   Then $B = 3$.
    *   Ratio A:B:C $= 2:3:1$.
    *   C's share $= 1/6 \times 7200 = ₹1,200$.
*   **Answer:** ₹1,200
*   **Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐☆ | **Target Time:** 60s
</details>
