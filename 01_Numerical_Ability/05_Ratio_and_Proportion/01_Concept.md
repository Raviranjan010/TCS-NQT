# Ratio and Proportion - Concept Guide

## 1. Understanding Ratios
A ratio compares two quantities of the same unit by division.

### Properties of Ratios:
If we multiply or divide both terms of a ratio by a non-zero number, the value remains unchanged.
$$\frac{a}{b} = \frac{ak}{bk}$$

### Immediate Worked Example:
Divide ₹1,200 between A and B in the ratio $2:3$.
*   **Step 1:** Add ratio parts: $2 + 3 = 5$ parts.
*   **Step 2:** Find value of 1 part: $1200 / 5 = ₹240$.
*   **Step 3:** Allocate: A gets $2 \times 240 = ₹480$, B gets $3 \times 240 = ₹720$.

## 2. Proportion & Componendo-Dividendo
A proportion is an equation stating that two ratios are equal: $a:b = c:d \iff \frac{a}{b} = \frac{c}{d}$.

### Derivation of Componendo & Dividendo (C&D):
If $\frac{a}{b} = \frac{c}{d}$:
1.  Add 1 on both sides:
    $$\frac{a}{b} + 1 = \frac{c}{d} + 1 \implies \frac{a+b}{b} = \frac{c+d}{d} \quad \text{(Componendo)}$$
2.  Subtract 1 from both sides:
    $$\frac{a}{b} - 1 = \frac{c}{d} - 1 \implies \frac{a-b}{b} = \frac{c-d}{d} \quad \text{(Dividendo)}$$
3.  Divide Componendo by Dividendo:
    $$\frac{a+b}{a-b} = \frac{c+d}{c-d} \quad \text{(Componendo and Dividendo)}$$

### Immediate Worked Example:
If $\frac{x+2}{x-2} = \frac{5}{3}$, find $x$.
*   Apply C&D:
    $$\frac{(x+2) + (x-2)}{(x+2) - (x-2)} = \frac{5+3}{5-3} \implies \frac{2x}{4} = \frac{8}{2} \implies \frac{x}{2} = 4 \implies x = 8$$
