# Percentages: Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

This FAQ answers common queries about percentage calculations, bases, and successive changes.

---

## 💬 Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1: Why can't I add successive percentage discounts directly?
Successive discounts are applied to a decreasing base value, not the original value.
- Adding them directly assumes both are calculated on the original price.
- **Example**: A $20\%$ discount followed by a $10\%$ discount yields a net discount of $28\%$, not $30\%$.

---

### Q2: What is the "base value" in a percentage calculation?
The base value is the initial or reference value that the change is calculated against.
- It always goes in the denominator of the change fraction.
- In profit and loss, the Cost Price (CP) serves as the base value.

---

### Q3: How do I convert a fraction to a percentage quickly?
Multiply the fraction by $100$ and add the $\%$ symbol.
- **Example**:
  $$\frac{3}{5} \times 100 = 60\%$$

---

### Q4: When should I use the product constancy rule?
Use this rule when the product of two variables is constant, such as Price $\times$ Consumption $=$ Expenditure.
- If one variable increases by $a/b$, the other must decrease by $\frac{a}{a+b}$ to keep the product constant.

---

### Q5: How do I calculate a percentage change between two values?
Subtract the old value from the new value, divide by the old value, and multiply by 100.
- **Formula**:
  $$\text{Change \%} = \frac{\text{New} - \text{Old}}{\text{Old}} \times 100$$
- A positive result indicates an increase, while a negative result indicates a decrease.
