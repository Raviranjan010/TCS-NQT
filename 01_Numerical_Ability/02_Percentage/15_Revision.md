# Percentage Revision Guide

---

### ⚡ 1-Minute Revision
*   **Fraction conversion is key**: Speed up math by converting percentages to fractions (e.g., $25\% = 1/4$, $16.67\% = 1/6$).
*   **Never add percentages directly**: Successive changes require compound formulas or multipliers (use $x + y + xy/100$).
*   **Product constancy jumps**: If A rises by $1/x$, B falls by $1/(x+1)$ to keep their product constant.
*   **Equal percentage swings**: A price change of $+P\%$ followed by $-P\%$ always leads to a net decrease of $(P/10)^2\%$.
*   **Identify the base**: In "A is what % more than B", B is the denominator. In "B is what % less than A", A is the denominator.

---

### ⏱ 5-Minute Revision
Review these core formulas and their direct applications:

1.  **Successive Change Formula**:
    $$\text{Net} = x + y + \frac{xy}{100}\%$$
    *   *Example*: $+20\%$ and $-10\% \implies 20 - 10 + \frac{20 \times (-10)}{100} = +8\%$ (increase).
2.  **Product Constancy Base Adjustment**:
    $$\text{Increase } = \frac{1}{x} \implies \text{Decrease } = \frac{1}{x+1}$$
    *   *Example*: Price of sugar rises by $25\%$ ($\frac{1}{4}$ increase) $\implies$ consumption must drop by $\frac{1}{4+1} = \frac{1}{5} = 20\%$ to keep expenditure constant.
3.  **Equal Percentage Swings**:
    $$\text{Loss} = \left(\frac{P}{10}\right)^2\%$$
    *   *Example*: A price rises by $30\%$ and then drops by $30\%$. Net change $= -(3)^2 = -9\%$ (decrease).
4.  **Commutative Property**:
    $$A\% \text{ of } B = B\% \text{ of } A$$
    *   *Example*: $16\%$ of $50 = 50\%$ of $16 = 8$.

### 📖 15-Minute Revision
Review this conceptual recap and attempt the 3 quick-test questions below.

#### Concept Recap:
Percentages are fractions with a base of 100. In exam questions, look for shifts in bases. If you spend $20\%$ of salary on food, your base for the next calculation is either the original $100\%$ (if no "remaining" keyword) or the remainder $80\%$ (if "remaining" is present). For elections, ensure you subtract invalid/illegal votes first to find the valid vote base.

#### Quick Test Questions:

**Q1.** A town's population increases by $10\%$ in Year 1 and decreases by $10\%$ in Year 2. If the current population is $9,900$, what was it 2 years ago?
*   *Solution*: Net change of $+10\%$ and $-10\%$ is a net $1\%$ decrease. Thus $99\%$ of original population $= 9,900 \implies \text{Original} = 10,000$.

**Q2.** If A is $50\%$ more than B, by what percentage is B less than A?
*   *Solution*: $50\%$ increase $= +1/2$. The equivalent decrease is $-1/(2+1) = -1/3 = 33.33\%$.

**Q3.** An item's price is marked up by $30\%$ and then sold at a $10\%$ discount. What is the net profit percentage?
*   *Solution*: $x = +30, y = -10 \implies 30 - 10 + \frac{30 \times (-10)}{100} = 20 - 3 = +17\%$.

### 🌙 Night-Before-Exam Revision
*   **Formula Checks**: Ensure you memorize the fraction conversion scale (especially $14.28\% = 1/7$ and $11.11\% = 1/9$).
*   **Boilerplate Math**: Successive discounts are always subtraction: $d_1 + d_2 - \frac{d_1 d_2}{100}$.
*   **Top 3 Traps to Avoid**:
    1.  Adding successive percentages directly (e.g. $+15\%$ and $+10\% \neq 25\%$).
    2.  Applying discounts to Cost Price instead of Marked Price.
    3.  Typing percentage symbols (`%`) into numerical input blanks on the TCS NQT system (enter numbers only).

### 🏃 Exam-Hall Revision (30 seconds)
Use this single comparison table for rapid reference right before entering the exam:

| Wording in Question | Mathematical Relation | Shortcut |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **"A is X% more than B"** | $A = B \times (1 + X/100)$ | B is less than A by $\frac{X}{100+X} \times 100\%$ |
| **"Successive change of X% and Y%"** | $\text{Net} = X + Y + XY/100$ | Use multiplying factors for tricky fractions. |
| **"Price rises... keep cost constant"** | $P \times C = \text{Constant}$ | $+1/x$ price change $\implies -1/(x+1)$ consumption change |
| **"Winner won by margin of V"** | $W\% - L\% = V$ | Margin $= (2W - 100)\%$ of total valid votes |
