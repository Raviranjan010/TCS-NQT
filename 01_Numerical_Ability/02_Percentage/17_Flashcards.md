# Percentage - Flashcards

Use this table to review key percentage concepts and fraction conversions for active recall.

| # | Front (Question) | Back (Answer) | Memory Trick |
|---|-----------------|---------------|--------------|
| 1 | What is the fraction equivalent of $16.67\%$? | **$\frac{1}{6}$** | $16.67$ starts with **1** and **6** $\rightarrow$ $\frac{1}{6}$ |
| 2 | What is the commutative rule of percentages? | **$A\%$ of $B = B\%$ of $A$** | "Swap the percentage sign to make calculations easier" |
| 3 | What is the net formula for two successive changes? | **$x + y + \frac{xy}{100}$** | "Sum the changes, then add their product over 100" |
| 4 | If variable A increases by $\frac{1}{x}$, what is the balance decrease for B? | **$\frac{1}{x+1}$** | "Increase = add to denominator ($x \rightarrow x+1$)" |
| 5 | What is the net change for equal increase and decrease of $P\%$? | **Depreciation of $\left(\frac{P}{10}\right)^2\%$** | "Always a loss; square the tens place digit" |
| 6 | What is the fraction equivalent of $8.33\%$? | **$\frac{1}{12}$** | $8.33\%$ is exactly half of $16.67\%$ ($\frac{1}{6} \div 2 = \frac{1}{12}$) |
| 7 | What does "increase to $120\%$" mean? | **An increase of $20\%$** | "TO means final state, BY means change" |
| 8 | What is the fraction equivalent of $14.28\%$? | **$\frac{1}{7}$** | $14$ is double of $7$ $\rightarrow$ $\frac{1}{7}$ |
| 9 | If a variable decreases by $\frac{1}{x}$, what is the balance increase? | **$\frac{1}{x-1}$** | "Decrease = subtract from denominator ($x \rightarrow x-1$)" |
| 10 | What is the net discount for successive discounts of $A\%$ and $B\%$? | **$A + B - \frac{AB}{100}$** | "Sum of discounts minus their product over 100" |
