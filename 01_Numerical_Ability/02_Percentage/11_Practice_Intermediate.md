## 📝 Practice Booklet

### Q1. Multi-step salary spend
**Question**: A man spends $20\%$ of his income on house rent, $15\%$ of the remaining on children's education, and $30\%$ of the balance on food. If he saves $5712$ at the end of the month, find his total income. (FIB)
- **Hint**: Let total income be $X$. Successive multipliers are $0.80$, $0.85$, and $0.70$.
- **Detailed Solution**:
  - Let Income $= X$.
  - After rent, remaining $= 0.80X$.
  - After education, remaining $= 0.80X \times 0.85 = 0.68X$.
  - After food, remaining $= 0.68X \times 0.70 = 0.476X$.
  - Equation: $0.476X = 5712 \implies X = \frac{5712}{0.476} = 12000$.
- **Shortcut**:
  $$X = \frac{5712}{0.8 \times 0.85 \times 0.7} = \frac{5712}{0.476} = 12000$$
- **Common Mistake**: Adding the percentage spent values directly ($20\% + 15\% + 30\% = 65\%$).

### Q2. Price-consumption budget increase
**Question**: Price of petrol rises by $25\%$. By what percentage must a driver reduce consumption so that expenditure increases by only $10\%$? (MCQ)
- **Options**: A. $12\%$, B. $15\%$, C. $10\%$, D. $8\%$
- **Hint**: Target expenditure multiplier $= 1.10$, price multiplier $= 1.25$.
- **Detailed Solution**:
  - Let initial cost be $100$ and consumption be $100 \implies$ budget $= 10000$.
  - New price $= 125$. Target budget $= 11000$.
  - New consumption $C = 11000 / 125 = 88$.
  - Reduction $= 100 - 88 = 12\%$.
- **Shortcut**:
  $$\text{Consumption Factor} = \frac{1.10}{1.25} = 0.88 \implies 12\% \text{ reduction}$$
- **Common Mistake**: Calculating difference as $25\% - 10\% = 15\%$.

### Q3. Successive rectangular dimensions shift
**Question**: Length of a rectangle increases by $30\%$ and breadth decreases by $20\%$. Find net change in area. (MCQ)
- **Options**: A. $4\%$ increase, B. $4\%$ decrease, C. $10\%$ increase, D. $6\%$ increase
- **Hint**: Use the successive percentage change formula with positive and negative signs.
- **Detailed Solution**:
  - $x = +30\%$, $y = -20\%$.
  - Net Change $= 30 - 20 + \frac{30 \times (-20)}{100} = 10 - 6 = 4\%$.
  - Positive result indicates an increase.
- **Shortcut**: Multiplier: $1.30 \times 0.80 = 1.04 \implies 4\%$ increase.
- **Common Mistake**: Stating there is a $10\%$ change ($30 - 20 = 10\%$).

### Q4. Passing limits and score margins
**Question**: A student secures $30\%$ marks and fails by 10 marks. Another student scores $40\%$ marks and gets 15 marks more than passing marks. Find passing marks. (FIB)
- **Hint**: The difference in percentage marks equals the sum of failed and extra marks.
- **Detailed Solution**:
  - Difference in marks $= 40\% - 30\% = 10\%$.
  - Marks gap $= 15 - (-10) = 25$ marks.
  - $10\% \text{ of Max Marks} = 25 \implies \text{Max Marks} = 250$.
  - Passing Marks $= 30\%$ of $250 + 10 = 75 + 10 = 85$.
- **Shortcut**: $10\% \rightarrow 25 \implies 30\% \rightarrow 75 \implies \text{Pass} = 75 + 10 = 85$.
- **Common Mistake**: Subtracting the marks margin instead of adding them to find the total gap.

### Q5. Fraction numerator/denominator shifts
**Question**: If numerator of a fraction increases by $150\%$ and denominator increases by $300\%$, the fraction becomes $5/18$. Find original fraction. (MCQ)
- **Options**: A. $4/9$, B. $5/9$, C. $2/3$, D. $4/5$
- **Hint**: Let fraction be $x/y$. Numerator becomes $2.5x$, denominator becomes $4y$.
- **Detailed Solution**:
  - $\frac{x \times 2.50}{y \times 4.00} = 5/18$.
  - $\frac{x}{y} \times \frac{25}{40} = 5/18 \implies \frac{x}{y} \times 5/8 = 5/18 \implies \frac{x}{y} = \frac{8}{18} = 4/9$.
- **Shortcut**:
  $$\frac{x}{y} = \frac{5}{18} \times \frac{400\%}{250\%} = \frac{5}{18} \times \frac{8}{5} = 4/9$$
- **Common Mistake**: Using $150$ and $300$ as factors instead of adding them to $100\%$.

### Q6. Double category population shift
**Question**: In a school, $60\%$ of students are boys. If $20\%$ of boys and $30\%$ of girls are scholarship holders, find percentage of students who do not hold a scholarship. (FIB)
- **Hint**: Calculate the non-scholarship percentages for boys ($80\%$) and girls ($70\%$).
- **Detailed Solution**:
  - Let total students be $100 \implies$ Boys $= 60$, Girls $= 40$.
  - Non-scholarship boys $= 60 \times 0.80 = 48$.
  - Non-scholarship girls $= 40 \times 0.70 = 28$.
  - Total non-scholarship $= 48 + 28 = 76$.
- **Shortcut**:
  $$\text{Weighted Average} = 0.6 \times 80\% + 0.4 \times 70\% = 48\% + 28\% = 76\%$$
- **Common Mistake**: Averaging scholarship rates directly.

### Q7. Income tax brackets and net income
**Question**: If income tax is increased by $19\%$, net income is reduced by $1\%$. Find rate of income tax. (FIB)
- **Hint**: The absolute increase in tax equals the absolute decrease in net income.
- **Detailed Solution**:
  - $19\% \text{ of Tax} = 1\% \text{ of Net Income} \implies \text{Tax} / \text{Net Income} = 1/19$.
  - Total Income $= \text{Tax} + \text{Net Income} = 1 + 19 = 20$.
  - Tax Rate $= \text{Tax} / \text{Total Income} \times 100 = 1/20 \times 100 = 5\%$.
- **Shortcut**:
  $$\text{Tax Rate} = \frac{\text{Net Income Change \%}}{\text{Tax Change \%} + \text{Net Income Change \%}} \times 100 = \frac{1}{19+1} \times 100 = 5\%$$
- **Common Mistake**: Stating the tax rate is $1\%$.

### Q8. Mixture water addition
**Question**: A $30\text{ L}$ mixture of milk and water contains $10\%$ water. How much water must be added to make it a $20\%$ water mixture? (FIB)
- **Hint**: Solute (milk) quantity remains constant at $90\%$ initially.
- **Detailed Solution**:
  - Initial milk volume $= 90\%$ of $30 = 27\text{ L}$.
  - In the new mixture, milk will be $80\%$.
  - New total volume $V = 27 / 0.80 = 33.75\text{ L}$.
  - Water added $= 33.75 - 30 = 3.75\text{ L}$.
- **Shortcut**:
  $$\text{Added Qty} = \text{Initial Volume} \times \frac{\text{Diff in \%}}{100 - \text{Target \%}} = 30 \times \frac{10}{80} = 3.75\text{ L}$$
- **Common Mistake**: Calculating $10\%$ of $30$ and adding it directly.

### Q9. Successive discount equivalence
**Question**: Find the single discount equivalent to successive discounts of $20\%$ and $15\%$. (MCQ)
- **Options**: A. $35\%$, B. $32\%$, C. $30\%$, D. $28\%$
- **Hint**: Successive reduction formula: $d_1 + d_2 - \frac{d_1 d_2}{100}$.
- **Detailed Solution**:
  - Net discount $= 20 + 15 - \frac{300}{100} = 35 - 3 = 32\%$.
- **Shortcut**: Multiplier product: $0.80 \times 0.85 = 0.68 \implies 1 - 0.68 = 32\%$ discount.
- **Common Mistake**: Stating discount is $35\%$.

### Q10. Voter lists and turnout
**Question**: In an election, $8\%$ voters did not cast votes. Winner got $48\%$ of total votes on list and won by $1100$ votes. Find total voters. (FIB)
- **Hint**: Winner got $48\%$ of total votes. Loser got $\text{Total} - \text{did not cast} - \text{winner}$.
- **Detailed Solution**:
  - Total list $= 100\%$. Cast votes $= 92\%$.
  - Winner got $48\% \implies$ Loser got $92\% - 48\% = 44\%$ of total.
  - Difference $= 48\% - 44\% = 4\% = 1100$.
  - Total list $= 1100 / 0.04 = 27500$.
- **Shortcut**: $4\% \rightarrow 1100 \implies 100\% \rightarrow 27500$.
- **Common Mistake**: Assuming loser got $52\% - 8\%$.

### Q11. Population compound decline
**Question**: Population of a town decreases by $10\%$ in first year and increases by $10\%$ in second year. Find net change. (MCQ)
- **Options**: A. No change, B. $1\%$ decrease, C. $1\%$ increase, D. $2\%$ decrease
- **Hint**: Use successive change formula with signs.
- **Detailed Solution**:
  - $-10 + 10 - \frac{100}{100} = -1\%$.
- **Shortcut**: $0.90 \times 1.10 = 0.99 \implies 1\%$ decrease.
- **Common Mistake**: Assuming the changes cancel out (no change).

### Q12. Multi-product sales markup
**Question**: Shopkeeper marks up item by $40\%$ and gives $20\%$ discount. If CP is $1500$, find profit. (FIB)
- **Hint**: Profit percentage is successive markup and discount.
- **Detailed Solution**:
  - Profit $\% = 40 - 20 - 8 = 12\%$.
  - Profit value $= 1500 \times 0.12 = 180$.
- **Shortcut**: $1.40 \times 0.80 = 1.12 \implies 12\% \text{ profit} \implies 1500 \times 0.12 = 180$.
- **Common Mistake**: Calculating profit on marked price instead of cost price.

### Q13. Candidate score margins
**Question**: Vikram needs $35\%$ marks to pass. He scores $160$ and fails by 15 marks. Find maximum marks. (FIB)
- **Hint**: Passing marks $= 160 + 15 = 175$.
- **Detailed Solution**:
  - $35\%$ of max marks $= 175$.
  - Max Marks $= 175 / 0.35 = 500$.
- **Shortcut**: $35\% \rightarrow 175 \implies 1\% \rightarrow 5 \implies 100\% \rightarrow 500$.
- **Common Mistake**: Failing by 15 means subtracting 15.

### Q14. Monthly savings ratios
**Question**: A family spends $60\%$ of income. Income increases by $15\%$, and savings remain same. Find percentage increase in spending. (MCQ)
- **Options**: A. $25\%$, B. $15\%$, C. $20\%$, D. $10\%$
- **Hint**: Let initial income be $100 \implies$ spend $= 60$, savings $= 40$.
- **Detailed Solution**:
  - Initial: Income $= 100$, Spend $= 60$, Savings $= 40$.
  - New: Income $= 115$. Savings remain $40 \implies$ new spend $= 115 - 40 = 75$.
  - Increase in spend $= 75 - 60 = 15 \implies$ percentage increase $= 15/60 \times 100 = 25\%$.
- **Shortcut**: Change in savings is $0\%$.
  $$1 \times 15\% = 0.6 \times x + 0.4 \times 0 \implies 15 = 0.6x \implies x = 25\%$$
- **Common Mistake**: Stating spending increases by $15\%$.

### Q15. Fresh fruit dry weight
**Question**: Fresh grapes contain $80\%$ water, dry raisins contain $10\%$ water. Find dry weight from $90\text{ kg}$ fresh grapes. (FIB)
- **Hint**: Pulp weight is constant at $20\%$ in fresh.
- **Detailed Solution**:
  - Pulp weight $= 90 \times 0.20 = 18\text{ kg}$.
  - Raisins contain $10\%$ water $\implies 90\%$ pulp.
  - Let raisins weight be $R \implies 0.90R = 18 \implies R = 20\text{ kg}$.
- **Shortcut**: $90 \times \frac{20}{90} = 20\text{ kg}$.
- **Common Mistake**: Subtracting water weights directly.

### Q16. Income bracket allocations
**Question**: Salary of A is $30\%$ more than salary of B. By what percentage is B's salary less than A's? (MCQ)
- **Options**: A. $23.08\%$, B. $30\%$, C. $25\%$, D. $27\%$
- **Hint**: Let B's salary be $100 \implies$ A's salary $= 130$.
- **Detailed Solution**:
  - B is less than A by $30$ on a base of $130$.
  - Less percentage $= 30/130 \times 100 = 300/13 = 23.08\%$.
- **Shortcut**: Increase of $3/10 \implies$ Decrease of $\frac{3}{10+3} = 3/13 = 23.08\%$.
- **Common Mistake**: Stating B's salary is $30\%$ less than A's.

### Q17. Sequential percentage depreciation
**Question**: Machine value depreciates by $10\%$ annually. If its present value is $8100$, find value 2 years ago. (FIB)
- **Hint**: Let value 2 years ago be $X \implies X \times 0.90 \times 0.90 = 8100$.
- **Detailed Solution**:
  - $0.81X = 8100 \implies X = 10000$.
- **Shortcut**: $10\%$ depreciation twice $= 19\%$ drop $\implies 81\% \rightarrow 8100 \implies X = 10000$.
- **Common Mistake**: Calculating $10\%$ increase on current value twice.

### Q18. Student ratio brackets
**Question**: In a class, $40\%$ are girls. If $60\%$ of boys pass and $80\%$ of girls pass, find class pass percentage. (FIB)
- **Hint**: Weighted average of pass percentages.
- **Detailed Solution**:
  - Let total students be $100 \implies$ Girls $= 40$, Boys $= 60$.
  - Boys pass $= 60 \times 0.60 = 36$.
  - Girls pass $= 40 \times 0.80 = 32$.
  - Total pass $= 36 + 32 = 68\%$.
- **Shortcut**: $0.6 \times 60\% + 0.4 \times 80\% = 36\% + 32\% = 68\%$.
- **Common Mistake**: Averaging pass rates directly ($\frac{60+80}{2} = 70\%$).

### Q19. Product markup and net margins
**Question**: Trader marks up item by $20\%$ and gives $10\%$ discount. Find profit or loss percentage. (MCQ)
- **Options**: A. $8\%$ profit, B. $10\%$ profit, C. $8\%$ loss, D. $2\%$ profit
- **Hint**: Successive markup and discount: $+20$ and $-10$.
- **Detailed Solution**:
  - Net Change $= 20 - 10 - \frac{200}{100} = 10 - 2 = 8\%$.
- **Shortcut**: $1.20 \times 0.90 = 1.08 \implies 8\%$ profit.
- **Common Mistake**: Stating there is a $10\%$ profit.

### Q20. Sales tax changes
**Question**: Sales tax on an item drops from $8\%$ to $6\%$. How much does a buyer save on purchase of $5000$? (FIB)
- **Hint**: Savings equals the difference in tax rates applied to purchase amount.
- **Detailed Solution**:
  - Difference in tax rate $= 8\% - 6\% = 2\%$.
  - Savings $= 2\%$ of $5000 = 100$.
- **Shortcut**: $5000 \times 0.02 = 100$.
- **Common Mistake**: Calculating individual taxes and subtracting (takes longer).
