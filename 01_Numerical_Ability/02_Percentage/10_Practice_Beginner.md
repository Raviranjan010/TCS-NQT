# Percentage - Practice Beginner

---

**Q1.** If A's salary is $25\%$ more than B's salary, by what percentage is B's salary less than A's salary?

> 🎯 Hint: Find the fraction equivalent of the increase and adjust the base.

<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>

**Approach:** Base Adjustment / Product Constancy Formula

**Step-by-step:**
Let B's salary be 100.
A's salary = 125.
Difference = 25.
Percentage less = (Difference / A) * 100
Percentage less = (25 / 125) * 100 = 20%

**Answer:** **20%**

**Shortcut:**
An increase of 25% is +1/4. The equivalent decrease is -1/(4+1) = -1/5 = 20%.

**Common Mistake:**
Assuming that if A is 25% more, then B must be 25% less.

**Difficulty:** ⭐☆☆☆☆ | **Target Time:** 30 seconds

</details>

**Q2.** A student scored 32% marks in an exam and failed by 16 marks. If the passing percentage is 40%, find the maximum marks of the exam.

> 🎯 Hint: The difference in percentage points equals the difference in actual marks.

<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>

**Approach:** Difference in Percentage Points

**Step-by-step:**
Passing Percentage = 40%
Scored Percentage = 32%
Difference in Percentage = 40% - 32% = 8%
Marks needed to pass = 16
Therefore, 8% of Maximum Marks = 16
Maximum Marks = (16 / 8) * 100 = 200

**Answer:** **200**

**Shortcut:**
8% corresponds to 16. So 1% corresponds to 2. Hence, 100% corresponds to 200.

**Common Mistake:**
Adding the scored percentage and fail marks directly (e.g., 32 + 16 = 48%).

**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐☆☆☆ | **Target Time:** 45 seconds

</details>

**Q3.** The price of sugar rises by 20%. By what percentage must a householder reduce consumption so as not to increase the expenditure on sugar?

> 🎯 Hint: Apply the product constancy fraction rule for an increase of +1/5.

<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>

**Approach:** Product Constancy / Base Adjustment

**Step-by-step:**
Price increase = 20% = +1/5.
Let original price be 5, new price is 6.
To keep expenditure same, consumption must change from 6 to 5.
Reduction in consumption = 1 / 6
Percentage reduction = (1 / 6) * 100 = 16.67%

**Answer:** **16.67%**

**Shortcut:**
If variable increases by 1/x (1/5), it must decrease by 1/(x+1) = 1/6 = 16.67%.

**Common Mistake:**
Subtracting 20% directly from consumption, resulting in a wrong answer of 20%.

**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐☆☆☆ | **Target Time:** 40 seconds

</details>

**Q4.** A price of a mobile phone is increased by 20% and then decreased by 10%. Find the net percentage change in its price.

> 🎯 Hint: Apply the successive change formula with x = +20 and y = -10.

<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>

**Approach:** Successive Percentage Change Formula

**Step-by-step:**
Let x = +20
Let y = -10
Net Change = x + y + (xy / 100)
Net Change = 20 - 10 + (20 * -10 / 100)
Net Change = 10 - 2 = 8%

**Answer:** **8%**

**Shortcut:**
Multiplying factors: 1.20 * 0.90 = 1.08 -> 8% net increase.

**Common Mistake:**
Adding the values directly: 20% - 10% = 10% increase.

**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐☆☆☆ | **Target Time:** 35 seconds

</details>

**Q5.** A book's price is increased by 20% and then decreased by 20%. Find the net percentage change.

> 🎯 Hint: For equal increase and decrease of x%, there is always a net decrease of x^2/100%.

<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>

**Approach:** Standard Equal Successive Change Formula

**Step-by-step:**
Let x = +20, y = -20
Net Change = 20 - 20 + (20 * -20 / 100)
Net Change = 0 - 400 / 100
Net Change = -4%

**Answer:** **4% decrease**

**Shortcut:**
Loss % = (x / 10)^2 = (20 / 10)^2 = 2^2 = 4% decrease.

**Common Mistake:**
Thinking the price remains unchanged (0% net change).

**Difficulty:** ⭐☆☆☆☆ | **Target Time:** 20 seconds

</details>
