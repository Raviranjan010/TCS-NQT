# Averages - Concept Guide

## 1. Mathematical Average (Arithmetic Mean)
An average is a single value that represents the central value of a set of data.
$$\text{Average} = \frac{\text{Sum of all terms}}{\text{Number of terms}}$$

### Derivation of Sum of Terms:
Multiplying both sides of the definition by the number of terms ($N$):
$$\text{Sum} = \text{Average} \times N$$

### Immediate Worked Example:
Find the average of $15, 20, 25, 30, 35$.
*   **Step 1:** Sum the terms: $15 + 20 + 25 + 30 + 35 = 125$.
*   **Step 2:** Count $= 5$.
*   **Step 3:** Calculate: $125 / 5 = 25$.

---

## 2. Weighted Average
When combining groups with different counts and averages.

### Derivation of Weighted Average:
Let Group 1 have $n_1$ terms with average $A_1$ (Sum $= n_1 A_1$) and Group 2 have $n_2$ terms with average $A_2$ (Sum $= n_2 A_2$).
$$\text{Weighted Average} = \frac{\text{Total Sum}}{\text{Total Count}} = \frac{n_1 A_1 + n_2 A_2}{n_1 + n_2}$$

### Immediate Worked Example:
A class has 20 boys (average age 12) and 30 girls (average age 10). Find the average age of the whole class.
*   **Step 1:** Apply formula:
    $$\text{Average} = \frac{20(12) + 30(10)}{20 + 30} = \frac{240 + 300}{50} = \frac{540}{50} = 10.8\text{ years}$$
