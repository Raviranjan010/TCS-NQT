# TCS NQT 2026 — Verbal Ability Part 1
**Prep Focus: Sentence Completion & Grammar Rules (20 Questions, ~25s per question)**

In the actual exam, Sentence Completion questions appear one by one with a tight 25-second limit per question. You cannot go back to previous questions. Speed and reflex are key.

---

## ⚡ 25-Second Sentence Completion Strategies

1. **The Blank Clue**: Read the sentence quickly to identify whether the blank requires a positive or negative word. Look for transitional words like *but*, *although*, *yet*, *however* (which indicate contrast) or *and*, *moreover*, *furthermore* (which indicate agreement).
2. **Grammar Elimination**: Check the parts of speech required (e.g., does the blank need a noun, verb, adjective, or adverb?). Eliminate options that violate basic agreement rules.
3. **Collocation Checking**: Many blanks are based on prepositions that pair with specific verbs (e.g., *refrain from*, *indifferent to*, *adapt to*, *comply with*). Look for these prepositions immediately following the blank.
4. **Don't Read the Options First**: Read the sentence first, guess a word in your head, then match it with the options. Reading all options first for every question will run down your 25 seconds.

## 📘 Essential Grammar Rules Tested by TCS

### 1. Subject-Verb Agreement
* **Rule**: Singular subjects take singular verbs; plural subjects take plural verbs.
* **Tricky Cases**:
  * *Either/Or* and *Neither/Nor*: The verb agrees with the **closer subject**.
    * *Example*: Neither the manager nor the **employees are** coming.
    * *Example*: Neither the employees nor the **manager is** coming.
  * *Along with, together with, as well as, accompanied by*: The verb agrees with the **first subject**.
    * *Example*: The **CEO**, along with his board members, **is** attending the meeting.
  * *Each, Everyone, Someone, Nobody*: These are always **singular**.
    * *Example*: Each of the candidates **is** expected to arrive on time.

### 2. Parallelism
* **Rule**: Sentences must maintain structural balance. Items in a list or comparison must be of the same grammatical form.
* *Incorrect*: He likes running, swimming, and **to ride** a bike.
* *Correct*: He likes running, swimming, and **riding** a bike.

### 3. Prepositions & Phrasal Verbs
Memorize these high-frequency pairings:
* **Abstain from** / **Refrain from** (doing something)
* **Accused of** (not for)
* **Comply with** (not to)
* **Cope with** (not cope up with)
* **Deprive of**
* **Indifferent to**
* **Prefer X to Y** (not prefer X over Y)

### 4. Tenses & Conditionals
* **Zero Conditional**: If + Simple Present $\to$ Simple Present (Universal facts).
  * *Example*: If you heat ice, it melts.
* **First Conditional**: If + Simple Present $\to$ Will + Verb (Real/possible future).
  * *Example*: If it rains, we will cancel the match.
* **Second Conditional**: If + Simple Past $\to$ Would + Verb (Imaginary/hypothetical).
  * *Example*: If I were the CEO, I would increase salaries.
* **Third Conditional**: If + Past Perfect $\to$ Would have + Past Participle (Past regret).
  * *Example*: If we had prepared well, we would have cleared the exam.

## 📝 Practice Drill & Solved PYQs

### 1. Prepositional Agreement
> [!TIP]
> **TCS NQT PYQ 1**: The new employee was not accustomed _______ such a high-pressure work culture, but he managed to adapt.
> * **Options**: (A) to, (B) with, (C) for, (D) in
> * **Solution**: The adjective *accustomed* is always followed by the preposition *to*.
> * **Answer**: `(A) to`

---

### 2. Subject-Verb Agreement
> [!TIP]
> **TCS NQT PYQ 2**: Every one of the new software programs _______ been tested for bugs before release.
> * **Options**: (A) have, (B) has, (C) are, (D) were
> * **Solution**: The subject is "Every one", which is singular. Therefore, it requires a singular verb. The present perfect form is "has been tested".
> * **Answer**: `(B) has`

---

### 3. Sentence Correction / Parallelism
> [!TIP]
> **TCS NQT PYQ 3**: Choose the grammatically correct sentence:
> * **Options**:
>   * (A) She not only speaks French fluently but also she knows German.
>   * (B) She speaks not only French fluently but also German.
>   * (C) She speaks French fluently and also knowing German.
>   * (D) Not only does she speak French fluently, but also German.
> * **Solution**: Under the rule of parallelism, "not only [Noun/Noun phrase] but also [Noun/Noun phrase]" is balanced. In option B, "not only French... but also German" correctly balances the two nouns representing languages.
> * **Answer**: `(B) She speaks not only French fluently but also German.`

---

### 4. Double Blanks (Vocabulary Context)
> [!TIP]
> **TCS NQT PYQ 4**: Although the team faced several _______ during the initial phase, their _______ efforts eventually led to a successful product launch.
> * **Options**:
>   * (A) setbacks, relentless
>   * (B) triumphs, half-hearted
>   * (C) delays, sporadic
>   * (D) challenges, sluggish
> * **Solution**: The transitional word "Although" indicates contrast. The first blank should be something negative (setbacks/delays/challenges) and the second blank must be something positive that leads to success (relentless). Relentless means persistent and determined.
> * **Answer**: `(A) setbacks, relentless`
