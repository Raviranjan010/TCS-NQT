## 2. Grammar Rules That Appear in Sentence Completion

Ensure you understand these grammar rules and traps to avoid selecting incorrect synonyms.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Subject-Verb Agreement Trap**  
> Collective nouns (*team*, *jury*, *committee*) take singular verbs when acting as a unified unit, but plural verbs when acting as individuals.  
> * *The jury **was** unanimous.* (Singular)  
> * *The jury **were** divided in their opinions.* (Plural)

> [!WARNING]
> **Preposition Choice Trap**  
> Always use **different from**, never use *different than*.  
> * *Correct*: This C++ compiler is different **from** the standard one.  
> * *Incorrect*: This C++ compiler is different *than* the standard one.

> [!NOTE]
> **Articles Phonetic Rule**  
> Use **an** before vowel sounds, even if the letter is a consonant (e.g. *an SQL developer*, *an hour*, *an MP*). Use **a** before consonant sounds (e.g. *a union*, *a university*, *a one-time setup*).

# ⚠️ Top 5 Spelling Mistakes Engineers Make

Spelling errors lead to zero marks on NQT sentence completion tasks.

1. **Occurred** (Incorrect: *Ocured*, *Occured*) — Must have double **c** and double **r**.
2. **Preceding** (Incorrect: *Preceeding*) — Single **e** in the second syllable.
3. **Obsolete** (Incorrect: *Obsolate*) — Ends in "**lete**".
4. **Incompatible** (Incorrect: *Incompatable*) — Ends in "**ible**".
5. **Simultaneously** (Incorrect: *Simultaniously*) — Ends in "**eously**".
