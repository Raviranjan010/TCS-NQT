Q: What is the divisibility rule for 11?
A: The absolute difference between the sum of digits at odd positions and even positions is 0 or a multiple of 11.

Q: What is the number of factors of 100?
A: $100 = 2^2 \times 5^2 \rightarrow (2+1)(2+1) = 9$ factors.

Q: What is the cyclicity cycle length of digit 2?
A: 4 ($2, 4, 8, 6 \dots$).

Q: How do you find the LCM of fractions?
A: $\frac{\text{LCM of Numerators}}{\text{HCF of Denominators}}$.

Q: What is the number of trailing zeros in $25!$?
A: $\lfloor 25/5 \rfloor + \lfloor 25/25 \rfloor = 5 + 1 = 6$ zeros.

Q: Are 8 and 15 coprime?
A: Yes ($\text{HCF}(8, 15) = 1$).

Q: What is the product of LCM and HCF of two numbers $A$ and $B$?
A: $A \times B$.

Q: What is the hexadecimal value of binary `1111`?
A: `F`.

Q: How many prime numbers exist between 1 and 50?
A: 15.

Q: What is the remainder when $18^{10}$ is divided by 17?
A: $18 \equiv 1 \pmod{17} \rightarrow 1^{10} \equiv 1 \pmod{17}$. Remainder is **1**.

Q: Every perfect square has an even number of factors.
A: False (they always have an odd number of factors).

Q: The number 1 is a prime number.
A: False.

Q: If a number is divisible by 3 and 4, it is also divisible by 12.
A: True.

Q: The binary number `101` is equivalent to decimal 5.
A: True.

Q: What is the formula/rule for Harmonic Mean?
A: $$v_{\text{avg}} = \frac{2 v_1 v_2}{v_1 + v_2}$$

Q: What is the formula/rule for Arithmetic Mean?
A: $$v_{\text{avg}} = \frac{v_1 \cdot t + v_2 \cdot t}{2t} = \frac{v_1 + v_2}{2}$$
