# Verbal Ability — Section Cheatsheet

## Reading Comprehension
*   **What it is:** Read the passage rapidly (in 30-45 seconds) to get a high-level overview.
*   **How to do it:** Focus on the title, the first paragraph, the first sentence of each middle paragraph, and the final paragraph.
*   **Purpose:** Understand the **tone**, **topic**, and **structure** of the passage.

## Error Detection
*   **Intervening Phrases:** Do not let phrases starting with "along with," "as well as," "together with," or "in addition to" confuse you about the subject's number.
    *   *Incorrect:* The captain, along with his crew members, **were** praised.
    *   *Correct:* The captain, along with his crew members, **was** praised (Subject is singular: *captain*).
*   **Either/Or and Neither/Nor:** When subjects are joined by "either... or" or "neither... nor", the verb agrees with the **closest** subject.
    *   *Incorrect:* Neither the manager nor the employees **was** present.
    *   *Correct:* Neither the manager nor the employees **were** present (Closest subject is plural: *employees*).
*   **Collective Nouns:** Nouns like "committee," "jury," or "team" take a singular verb when acting as a unified unit, but a plural verb if the members are acting individually.
    *   *Incorrect:* The jury **were** unanimous in its decision.
    *   *Correct:* The jury **was** unanimous in its decision.

## Fill in the Blanks
*   **Question:** Although the CEO's speech was ________, it failed to ________ the disgruntled shareholders.
    *   *Options:*
        *   A. tedious, annoy
        *   B. eloquent, appease
        *   C. verbose, irritate
        *   D. brief, concern
    *   *Analysis:*
        1.  "Although" indicates contrast. The first blank describes the speech positively (or neutrally) if the second blank represents a failure of a positive action.
        2.  The phrase "failed to [blank] the disgruntled shareholders" implies we wanted to satisfy or calm them down. So blank 2 should mean "pacify" or "please".
        3.  Let's look at the second words in the options: *annoy* (negative), *appease* (positive - means pacify), *irritate* (negative), *concern* (neutral).
        4.  *Appease* fits blank 2 perfectly. Let's check word 1: *eloquent* (positive - means fluent speaker).
        5.  "Although the speech was eloquent, it failed to appease..." makes perfect logical sense.
    *   *Answer:* **B**

## Synonyms & Antonyms
*   **Rule:** Do not jump to the most common definition of the word. A word can have multiple meanings based on its part of speech and surrounding words.
*   **Bene** (good/well): *Benevolent*, *beneficial*.
*   **Mal** (bad/evil): *Malevolent*, *malicious*, *malignant*.

## Sentence Improvement
*   **Incorrect:** <u>Walking down the street</u>, the library came into view.
    *   *Analysis:* The underlined modifier is misplaced. As written, it implies the *library* was walking down the street.
*   **Correct / Improvement:** **As I was walking down the street**, the library came into view. / Walking down the street, **I saw** the library.
*   **Incorrect:** He likes <u>swimming, hiking, and to run</u>.
    *   *Analysis:* The underlined list contains two gerunds (*swimming*, *hiking*) and one infinitive (*to run*). They must be parallel.

## Para Jumbles
*   **Keywords:** Look for sentences starting with "Thus," "Consequently," "Therefore," "In conclusion," or "Finally."
*   **Content:** It represents a wrap-up or future outlook rather than introducing new details.

## Active & Passive Voice
*   **Order/Command structure:**
    *   *Active:* Write the letter.
    *   *Passive (Formula: Let + Object + be + V3):* **Let** the letter **be written**.
*   **Request structure:**
    *   *Active:* Please sit down.
    *   *Passive:* You are requested to sit down.

| Tense | Active Structure | Passive Structure | Active Example | Passive Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Simple Present** | S + V1 (s/es) + O | O + is/am/are + V3 + by S | He writes a letter. | A letter **is written** by him. |
| **Present Continuous** | S + is/am/are + V1-ing + O | O + is/am/are + **being** + V3 + by S | He is writing a letter. | A letter **is being written** by him. |
| **Present Perfect** | S + has/have + V3 + O | O + has/have + **been** + V3 + by S | He has written a letter. | A letter **has been written** by him. |
| **Simple Past** | S + V2 + O | O + was/were + V3 + by S | He wrote a letter. | A letter **was written** by him. |
| **Past Continuous** | S + was/were + V1-ing + O | O + was/were + **being** + V3 + by S | He was writing a letter. | A letter **was being written** by him. |
| **Past Perfect** | S + had + V3 + O | O + had + **been** + V3 + by S | He had written a letter. | A letter **had been written** by him. |
| **Simple Future** | S + will/shall + V1 + O | O + will/shall + **be** + V3 + by S | He will write a letter. | A letter **will be written** by him. |
| **Future Perfect** | S + will have + V3 + O | O + will have + **been** + V3 + by S | He will have written a letter. | A letter **will have been written** by him. |

## Direct & Indirect Speech
*   **S**ubject (First person pronouns: *I, we*) changes according to the **Subject** of the reporting verb.
*   **O**bject (Second person pronouns: *you*) changes according to the **Object** of the reporting verb.
*   **N**o change for Third person pronouns (*he, she, it, they*).

| Direct Tense | Indirect Tense | Direct Example | Indirect Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Simple Present** | Simple Past | She said, "I **write** a letter." | She said that she **wrote** a letter. |
| **Present Continuous** | Past Continuous | She said, "I **am writing** a letter." | She said that she **was writing** a letter. |
| **Present Perfect** | Past Perfect | She said, "I **have written** a letter." | She said that she **had written** a letter. |
| **Simple Past** | Past Perfect | She said, "I **wrote** a letter." | She said that she **had written** a letter. |
| **Past Continuous** | Past Perfect Continuous | She said, "I **was writing** a letter." | She said that she **had been writing** a letter. |
| **Simple Future (will)** | Conditional (would) | She said, "I **will write** a letter." | She said that she **would write** a letter. |

## Vocabulary
*   **Meaning:** A departure from what is normal, usual, or expected.
*   **Synonym/Antonym:** Deviation / Normality
*   **Usage:** The sudden system crash was a temporary software aberration.
