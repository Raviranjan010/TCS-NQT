# Time, Speed & Distance — Worked Examples

## Example 1 — Basic Speed Calculation

**Problem:** A car travels 240 km in 4 hours. Find its speed in m/s.

**Solution:**
1. Speed = Distance / Time = 240 / 4 = 60 km/h
2. Convert to m/s: 60 × (5/18) = 300/18 = **16.67 m/s**

---

## Example 2 — Train Crossing a Platform

**Problem:** A train 150m long crosses a platform 250m long in 20 seconds. Find speed in km/h.

**Solution:**
1. Total distance = 150 + 250 = 400 m
2. Speed = Distance / Time = 400 / 20 = 20 m/s
3. Convert: 20 × (18/5) = **72 km/h**

---

## Example 3 — Two Trains in Opposite Directions

**Problem:** Train A (150m, 60 km/h) and Train B (200m, 90 km/h) move in opposite directions. Find time to cross each other.

**Solution:**
1. Relative Speed = 60 + 90 = 150 km/h = 150 × (5/18) = 41.67 m/s
2. Total Length = 150 + 200 = 350 m
3. Time = 350 / 41.67 = **8.4 seconds**

---

## Example 4 — Boats & Streams

**Problem:** A boat goes 30 km downstream in 2 hours and 20 km upstream in 4 hours. Find the boat's speed in still water.

**Solution:**
1. Downstream speed = 30/2 = 15 km/h
2. Upstream speed = 20/4 = 5 km/h
3. Still water speed = (15 + 5)/2 = **10 km/h**
4. Stream speed = (15 - 5)/2 = 5 km/h

---

## Example 5 — Average Speed (Round Trip)

**Problem:** Ravi travels from city A to B at 60 km/h and returns at 40 km/h. Find average speed.

**Solution:**
1. Use harmonic mean (equal distances):
$$\text{Avg Speed} = \frac{2 \times 60 \times 40}{60 + 40} = \frac{4800}{100} = \textbf{48 km/h}$$

**Common mistake:** Do NOT take (60+40)/2 = 50. That's wrong!
