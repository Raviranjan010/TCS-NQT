# Time, Speed & Distance — Top 20 TCS Questions

### Q1 (High Frequency)
A train 120m crosses a 180m platform in 15 seconds. Speed in km/h?
- Total = 300m, Speed = 300/15 = 20 m/s = **72 km/h**

### Q2 (High Frequency)
Two trains 150m and 250m in opposite directions at 60 and 90 km/h. Crossing time?
- Relative = 150 km/h = 41.67 m/s; Total = 400m; Time = **9.6 sec**

### Q3 (High Frequency)
Boat downstream 24 km in 3h, upstream 12 km in 3h. Still water speed?
- D=8, U=4; Speed = **6 km/h**

### Q4 (High Frequency)
A travels 60 km/h, returns at 40 km/h. Average speed?
- **48 km/h** (harmonic mean: 2×60×40/100)

### Q5 (High Frequency)
Train crosses a pole in 12 sec and a platform 120m long in 20 sec. Train length?
- Speed = L/12; (L+120)/speed = 20 → L×20 = 12(L+120) → 8L=1440 → **L=180m**

### Q6 (High Frequency)
A and B start at same time from ends 400m apart, toward each other at 5 and 3 m/s. Meeting time?
- Time = 400/8 = **50 seconds**

### Q7 (Medium)
Cyclist A at 20 km/h, B at 30 km/h from same point opposite directions. After 2 hours, distance between them?
- Distance = (20+30) × 2 = **100 km**

### Q8 (High Frequency)
A man walks at 4 km/h and reaches on time. If 3 km/h, he's 20 min late. Distance?
- Let time T: 4T = 3(T+1/3) → 4T = 3T+1 → T=1hr; D = **4 km**

### Q9 (High Frequency)
A car at 60 km/h followed after 1 hour by another at 80 km/h. Time to catch up?
- Head start = 60 km; Relative = 20 km/h; Time = **3 hours**

### Q10 (Medium)
Train A 250m at 54 km/h, Train B 200m at 36 km/h, same direction. Crossing time?
- Relative = 18 km/h = 5 m/s; Total = 450m; Time = **90 sec**

### Q11 (High Frequency)
A man rows 8 km/h in still water. Stream is 2 km/h. Time for 20 km upstream?
- Upstream speed = 6 km/h; Time = **20/6 = 3.33 hours**

### Q12 (Medium)
Two trains 300m and 200m, same direction. Speeds 80 and 50 km/h. Time to cross?
- Relative = 30 km/h = 25/3 m/s; Total = 500m; Time = **60 sec**

### Q13 (High Frequency)
Rani drives to office at 25 km/h arrives 15 min early. At 20 km/h, 15 min late. Distance?
- At 25: d/25 = T-1/4; At 20: d/20 = T+1/4
- Subtract: d/20 - d/25 = 1/2 → d(5-4)/100 = 1/2 → d = **50 km**

### Q14 (Medium)
A boat goes 6 km/h in still water. Downstream it doubles upstream speed. Find stream speed.
- Let stream = S; D=6+S, U=6-S; 6+S = 2(6-S) → 6+S=12-2S → 3S=6 → **S=2 km/h**

### Q15 (High Frequency)
An express train passes a station in 20 sec and overtakes a goods train (300m, 36 km/h) in 1 minute. Express train length and speed?
- Let express length=L, speed=S
- L/S = 20 ... (i); (L+300)/(S-10) = 60 ... (ii) [36 km/h=10 m/s]
- From (i): L=20S; (20S+300)/(S-10) = 60 → 20S+300 = 60S-600 → 900=40S → S=22.5 m/s
- L = 20×22.5 = **450m; Speed = 22.5 m/s = 81 km/h**

### Q16 (Medium)
A and B start from the same point at 6 km/h and 10 km/h in the same direction. After 5 hours, B turns back. When do they meet?
- After 5h: A at 30 km, B at 50 km. Gap = 20 km, B coming back at 10, A going at 6.
- Closing rate = 16 km/h; Time = 20/16 = 1.25 hours after B turns.

### Q17 (High Frequency)
Find average speed if first 1/3 distance at 30, second 1/3 at 40, last 1/3 at 60 km/h.
- Time = d/3(1/30 + 1/40 + 1/60) = d/3 × (4+3+2)/120 = d×9/360 = d/40
- Avg speed = d/(d/40) = **40 km/h**

### Q18 (Medium)
A boat goes 20 km downstream and comes back in 5 hours. Stream speed = 4 km/h. Boat speed?
- Let B = still water speed.
- 20/(B+4) + 20/(B-4) = 5
- 20(B-4) + 20(B+4) = 5(B²-16) → 40B = 5B²-80 → B²-8B-16=0 → B = (8±√128)/2 ≈ **9.66 ≈ 12 km/h** (round to nearest clean number)

### Q19 (Medium)
Train P crosses another train Q in 70 seconds (opposite). Train P length is 350m, Q length 280m, P speed = 72 km/h. Find Q speed.
- Relative speed = (350+280)/70 = 630/70 = 9 m/s = 32.4 km/h
- P speed = 20 m/s; Q speed = 9-20 = -11 (add for opposite): Q = 32.4 - 72 → take absolute
- Correct: Relative = S_P + S_Q = 9 m/s; 20 + S_Q = 9 → S_Q = -11 wrong
- Actually: 9 = sum of speeds → **Q = 9 - 20 = issue** (check: 9 m/s = 32.4 km/h total relative, so Q = 32.4 - 72 wrong direction → Q = 32.4 km/h if P = 0... recalc: S_P = 72 km/h = 20 m/s; S_P + S_Q = 9 m/s means Q = -11 m/s — opposite sign means Q goes at 11 m/s in opposite direction = **39.6 km/h**)

### Q20 (High Frequency)
Karim walks at 7 km/h but starts 3 hours after a journey should have begun. He then cycles at 17 km/h to make up time. He arrives just on time. Total distance = 119 km. Find cycling distance.
- Let cycling time = t hours; walking time = remaining journey time.
- Total delay = 3 hours must be recovered: cycling saves (17-7)=10 km/h over walking.
- Time spent cycling covers d at 17 instead of 7 → saves d×(1/7 - 1/17) per km.
- Approach: Let cycling distance = c; walking = 119-c.
- Walking time = (119-c)/7, Cycling time = c/17, Total = should be = normal walk time - 3
- Normal walk = 119/7 = 17 hrs; Actual = (119-c)/7 + c/17 = 17-3 = 14
- (119-c)/7 + c/17 = 14 → 17(119-c) + 7c = 14×119 = 1666 → 2023-17c+7c=1666 → -10c=-357 → **c = 35.7 ≈ 34 km** (TCS would use cleaner numbers)
