### Q1
A sum of money invested at compound interest doubles itself in 5 years. In how many years will it become 8 times itself at the same rate?

#### Solution
1.  Let the initial principal be $P$.
2.  It doubles in 5 years $ightarrow P ightarrow 2P$ in 5 years.
3.  Since compounding is exponential:
    *   $2P ightarrow 4P$ in another 5 years (total 10 years).
    *   $4P ightarrow 8P$ in another 5 years (total 15 years).
4.  It will become 8 times itself in **15 years**.

### Q6
If the simple interest on a sum of money for 2 years at $5\%$ is 80 Rupees, find the compound interest on the same sum at the same rate for the same time.

#### Solution
1.  Time = 2 years. Rate = $5\%$.
2.  Simple interest per year = $80 / 2 = 40$ Rupees.
3.  For Compound Interest, Year 2 interest earns compound interest on Year 1 interest:
    $$	ext{Additional Interest} = 5\% 	ext{ of } 40 = 2 	ext{ Rupees}$$
4.  Total CI = $80 + 2 = 82$ Rupees.
