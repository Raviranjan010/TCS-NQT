## Part 2 — 5 True/False Statements

### 1. Statement
Simple interest earned is identical in value for every single year of the loan term.
*   **Answer:** **TRUE**
*   **Explanation:** Simple interest is calculated strictly on the original Principal ($P$), which is constant.

### 2. Statement
Compound Interest is always less than Simple Interest for any period of time.
*   **Answer:** **FALSE**
*   **Explanation:** Compound interest is greater than Simple Interest for any period $T > 1$ year (and equal at exactly 1 year).

### 3. Statement
Quarterly compounding compounding events result in higher interest yield than annual compounding.
*   **Answer:** **TRUE**
*   **Explanation:** More frequent compounding periods increase the compounding effect, raising the effective interest rate.

### 4. Statement
In a leap year, day-based simple interest must be calculated dividing by 366.
*   **Answer:** **TRUE**
*   **Explanation:** Leap years contain exactly 366 days.

### 5. Statement
The annual installment in a compound interest loan is simply the total accumulated debt divided by the number of years.
*   **Answer:** **FALSE**
*   **Explanation:** CI installments are determined by present-value discounting, not direct linear division.\n
