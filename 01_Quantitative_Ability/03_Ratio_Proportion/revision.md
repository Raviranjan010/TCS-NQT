## 1. Fundamentals

### Ratios & Proportions
*   **Properties:** A ratio remains unchanged if both numerator and denominator are multiplied by the same non-zero number.
*   **Proportions:** $a:b :: c:d \implies ad = bc$.
*   **Mean Proportional:** $b = \sqrt{ac}$.
*   **Third Proportional:** $c = b^2 / a$.
*   **Fourth Proportional:** $d = bc / a$.

---

## 2. Partnership Profits
In business partnerships, profit is shared based on the product of investment and active time:
$$\text{Profit Ratio} = (I_A \times T_A) : (I_B \times T_B) : (I_C \times T_C)$$
*   **Active vs. Sleeping Partner:** Active partner's salary must be deducted from the total profit **first** before sharing the remainder.

---

## 3. Alligation Cross Rule
Used to find the ratio in which two ingredients must be mixed to obtain a target mean value:
$$\frac{\text{Cheaper Quantity}}{\text{Dearer Quantity}} = \frac{D - M}{M - C}$$
*   Make sure all three values ($C$, $D$, and $M$) are of the **same property** and unit (e.g., all Cost Prices).

---

## 4. Dilution & Replacements
For removing $x$ liters from a vessel of volume $V$ containing pure liquid and replacing with water:
$$\text{Remaining Liquid} = V \times \left(1 - \frac{x}{V}\right)^n$$
*   where $n$ is the number of times the process is performed.
*   **Water Volume** = Total Volume ($V$) - Remaining Liquid.
