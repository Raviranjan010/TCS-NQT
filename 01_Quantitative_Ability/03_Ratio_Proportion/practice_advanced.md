### Q1
A vessel is filled with liquid, 3 parts of which are water and 5 parts syrup. How much of the mixture must be drawn off and replaced with water so that the mixture may be half water and half syrup?

#### Standard Method
1.  Let the initial volume of the mixture be 8 units. Water = 3, Syrup = 5.
2.  Let $x$ units of mixture be drawn off.
    *   Water removed = $\frac{3}{8}x$.
    *   Syrup removed = $\frac{5}{8}x$.
3.  $x$ units of water are added back.
4.  Final syrup volume = $5 - \frac{5}{8}x$.
5.  We want final syrup to be half of the total volume (which remains 8 units):
    $$5 - \frac{5}{8}x = 4 \implies \frac{5}{8}x = 1 \implies x = \frac{8}{5} \text{ units}$$
6.  Fraction of mixture drawn off = $\frac{x}{8} = \frac{8/5}{8} = \frac{1}{5}$.

#### Alternate Fast Method
*   Since syrup is not added, its quantity decreases purely by dilution.
*   Initial syrup fraction = $\frac{5}{8}$. Target syrup fraction = $\frac{1}{2} = \frac{4}{8}$.
*   Fractional decrease in syrup concentration = $\frac{5/8 - 4/8}{5/8} = \frac{1/8}{5/8} = \frac{1}{5}$.
*   Since dilution is uniform, the fraction of mixture drawn off is exactly equal to the concentration decrease fraction, which is $\frac{1}{5}$.

### Q2
A, B, and C enter into a partnership. A invests 3 times as much as B, and B invests two-thirds of what C invests. At the end of the year, B receives 4,000 Rupees as profit share. Find the total profit.

#### Standard Method
1.  Let C's investment be $3x$. B's investment = $2x$. A's investment = $6x$.
2.  Investment ratio $A:B:C = 6:2:3$.
3.  Since duration is equal, profit ratio = $6:2:3$.
4.  B's profit share = $4000 = \frac{2}{11} \times \text{Total Profit}$.
5.  $$\text{Total Profit} = \frac{4000 \times 11}{2} = 22,000 \text{ Rupees}$$

#### Alternate Fast Method
*   Profit shares: A = 6 units, B = 2 units, C = 3 units. Total = 11 units.
*   Since 2 units = 4000 Rupees, 1 unit = 2000 Rupees.
*   Total Profit (11 units) = $11 \times 2000 = 22,000$ Rupees.

### Q3
A can contains a mixture of two liquids A and B in the ratio $7:5$. When 9 liters of mixture are drawn off and the can is filled with B, the ratio of A and B becomes $7:9$. How many liters of liquid A was contained by the can initially?

#### Standard Method
1.  Let the initial volumes of A and B be $7x$ and $5x$. Total = $12x$.
2.  In 9 liters of mixture:
    *   Liquid A removed = $\frac{7}{12} \times 9 = 5.25$ liters.
    *   Liquid B removed = $\frac{5}{12} \times 9 = 3.75$ liters.
3.  After adding 9 liters of B, new ratio is:
    $$\frac{7x - 5.25}{5x - 3.75 + 9} = \frac{7}{9} \implies \frac{7x - 5.25}{5x + 5.25} = \frac{7}{9}$$
4.  Divide both numerators by 7:
    $$\frac{x - 0.75}{5x + 5.25} = \frac{1}{9} \implies 9x - 6.75 = 5x + 5.25 \implies 4x = 12 \implies x = 3$$
5.  Initial liquid A = $7x = 21$ liters.

#### Alternate Fast Method
*   Notice that the amount of liquid A changes only due to removal, but B is added.
*   However, let's look at the ratios before and after:
    *   Before replacement: $A:B = 7:5$
    *   After replacement: $A:B = 7:9$
*   Since A's parts are same (7), the change in B's parts ($9 - 5 = 4$ parts) must correspond to the addition of B.
*   Wait! The total volume before drawing off is $12x$. After drawing off 9 liters, the remaining volume is $12x - 9$. In this remaining volume, the ratio is still $7:5$ (total 12 parts).
*   When we add 9 liters of B, the total volume becomes $12x$ again, and the ratio is $7:9$ (total 16 parts).
*   Since A is constant in the remaining mixture and the final mixture:
    *   Remaining: $A:B = 7:5$ (total 12 parts)
    *   Final: $A:B = 7:9$ (total 16 parts)
    *   Change in B = 4 parts = 9 liters $\implies$ 1 part = $2.25$ liters.
    *   A's share = 7 parts = $7 \times 2.25 = 15.75$ liters in the *remaining* mixture.
    *   Initial A = A in final mixture = $\frac{7}{16} \times \text{Total}$.
    *   Since 16 parts is the final mixture, final total volume = $16 \times 2.25 = 36$ liters.
    *   Initial A = $\frac{7}{12} \times 36 = 21$ liters.

### Q4
An amount of 735 Rupees is divided among A, B, and C such that if their shares are decreased by 25 Rupees, 15 Rupees, and 20 Rupees respectively, their remaining shares will be in the ratio $1:3:2$. What is the original share of C?

#### Standard Method
1.  Let the remaining shares be $x$, $3x$, $2x$.
2.  Original shares: A = $x + 25$, B = $3x + 15$, C = $2x + 20$.
3.  Sum of original shares = 735:
    $$(x + 25) + (3x + 15) + (2x + 20) = 735 \implies 6x + 60 = 735 \implies 6x = 675 \implies x = 112.5$$
4.  C's share = $2x + 20 = 2(112.5) + 20 = 245$ Rupees.

#### Alternate Fast Method
*   Total reduction = $25 + 15 + 20 = 60$ Rupees.
*   Remaining total sum = $735 - 60 = 675$ Rupees.
*   Sum of remaining ratio terms = $1 + 3 + 2 = 6$.
*   Remaining share of C = $\frac{2}{6} \times 675 = 225$ Rupees.
*   C's original share = $225 + 20 = 245$ Rupees.

### Q5
A gold bar is broken into three parts whose weights are in the ratio $3:4:5$. The value of a gold bar is directly proportional to the square of its weight. If the total loss incurred due to breaking is 23,500 Rupees, find the value of the unbroken gold bar.

#### Standard Method
1.  Let weights of the broken parts be $3x$, $4x$, $5x$. Unbroken weight = $12x$.
2.  Value of unbroken gold = $k(12x)^2 = 144k x^2$.
3.  Value of broken parts = $k(3x)^2 + k(4x)^2 + k(5x)^2 = k x^2(9 + 16 + 25) = 50k x^2$.
4.  Loss = $\text{Unbroken Value} - \text{Broken Value} = 144k x^2 - 50k x^2 = 94k x^2$.
5.  Given Loss = 23,500 Rupees:
    $$94k x^2 = 23,500 \implies k x^2 = \frac{23,500}{94} = 250$$
6.  Value of unbroken gold = $144 \times 250 = 36,000$ Rupees.

#### Alternate Fast Method
*   Weight parts = 3, 4, 5. Total = 12.
*   Initial Value = $12^2 = 144$ units.
*   Broken Value = $3^2 + 4^2 + 5^2 = 9 + 16 + 25 = 50$ units.
*   Loss = $144 - 50 = 94$ units = 23,500 Rupees $\implies$ 1 unit = 250 Rupees.
*   Initial Value = $144 \times 250 = 36,000$ Rupees.

### Q6
The incomes of A, B, and C are in the ratio $7:9:12$ and their expenditures are in the ratio $8:9:15$. If A saves $\frac{1}{4}$ of his income, find the ratio of their savings.

#### Standard Method
1.  Let incomes be $7x, 9x, 12x$ and expenditures be $8y, 9y, 15y$.
2.  A's savings = $\text{Income} - \text{Expenditure} = 7x - 8y = \frac{1}{4}(7x)$.
3.  $$7x - 8y = 1.75x \implies 5.25x = 8y \implies \frac{x}{y} = \frac{8}{5.25} = \frac{32}{21}$$
4.  Let $x = 32$ and $y = 21$.
    *   Incomes: A = 224, B = 288, C = 384.
    *   Expenditures: A = 168, B = 189, C = 315.
    *   Savings: A = $224 - 168 = 56$, B = $288 - 189 = 99$, C = $384 - 315 = 69$.
5.  Savings Ratio = $56 : 99 : 69$.

#### Alternate Fast Method
*   Savings = Income - Expenditure.
*   Since A's savings is $25\%$ of income, A's expenditure is $75\%$ of income:
    $$8y = \frac{3}{4}(7x) \implies y = \frac{21}{32}x$$
*   Write savings ratio directly as:
    $$(7 - 8 \times \frac{21}{32}) : (9 - 9 \times \frac{21}{32}) : (12 - 15 \times \frac{21}{32})$$
    Multiply by 32 to eliminate fraction:
    $$(224 - 168) : (288 - 189) : (384 - 315) = 56 : 99 : 69$$

### Q7
Two vessels A and B contain milk and water mixed in the ratio $8:5$ and $5:2$ respectively. In what ratio must these mixtures be mixed to get a new mixture containing milk and water in the ratio $9:4$?

#### Standard Method
1.  Milk fraction in A = $\frac{8}{13}$.
2.  Milk fraction in B = $\frac{5}{7}$.
3.  Target milk fraction = $\frac{9}{13}$.
4.  Apply Alligation:
    $$\text{Ratio} = \frac{5/7 - 9/13}{9/13 - 8/13} = \frac{(65 - 63)/91}{1/13} = \frac{2/91}{1/13} = \frac{2 \times 13}{91} = \frac{26}{91} = \frac{2}{7}$$
5.  Ratio is **2 : 7**.

#### Alternate Fast Method
*   Alligation on water fraction:
    *   A's water = $5/13$. B's water = $2/7$. Mean water = $4/13$.
    *   Ratio = $\frac{4/13 - 5/13}{2/7 - 4/13}$ (take absolute values)
    *   Ratio = $\frac{1/13}{|26-28|/91} = \frac{1/13}{2/91} = \frac{91}{26} = \frac{7}{2}$ (dearer to cheaper, so cheaper to dearer = $2:7$).

### Q8
A, B, and C enter into a partnership. A invests one-third of the capital for one-third of the time, B invests one-sixth of the capital for one-sixth of the time, and C invests the rest of the capital for the whole time. If the total profit is 46,000 Rupees, find C's share.

#### Standard Method
1.  Let total capital be $6$ units and total time be $6$ months.
2.  A's investment = $2$ units for $2$ months.
3.  B's investment = $1$ unit for $1$ month.
4.  C's investment = $6 - (2 + 1) = 3$ units for $6$ months (whole time).
5.  Profit sharing ratio:
    $$P_A : P_B : P_C = (2 \times 2) : (1 \times 1) : (3 \times 6) = 4 : 1 : 18$$
6.  C's share = $\frac{18}{23} \times 46,000 = 18 \times 2,000 = 36,000$ Rupees.

#### Alternate Fast Method
*   Shares: A = $1/9$, B = $1/36$, C = $1/2 \times 1 = 1/2$ (capital = $1 - 1/3 - 1/6 = 1/2$).
*   LCM of 9, 36, 2 is 36.
*   Ratio = $4 : 1 : 18$.
*   Total units = 23. C's share = $18/23 \times 46,000 = 36,000$ Rupees.

### Q9
If $a:b = c:d$, show that $\frac{ma + nc}{mb + nd} = \frac{a}{b}$.

#### Standard Method
1.  Let $\frac{a}{b} = \frac{c}{d} = k \implies a = bk, c = dk$.
2.  $$\text{Expression} = \frac{m(bk) + n(dk)}{mb + nd} = \frac{k(mb + nd)}{mb + nd} = k = \frac{a}{b}$$

#### Alternate Fast Method
*   Since $\frac{a}{b} = \frac{c}{d}$, any linear combination of numerators divided by the same combination of denominators equals the original ratio:
    $$\frac{ma + nc}{mb + nd} = \frac{a}{b}$$

### Q10
A mixture of 100 liters of milk and water contains $10\%$ water. How much water must be added to make it $20\%$ water in the new mixture?

#### Standard Method
1.  Water = 10 liters, Milk = 90 liters.
2.  Let water added be $x$ liters.
    $$\frac{10 + x}{100 + x} = \frac{20}{100} = \frac{1}{5} \implies 5(10 + x) = 100 + x \implies 50 + 5x = 100 + x \implies 4x = 50 \implies x = 12.5 \text{ liters}$$

#### Alternate Fast Method
*   Since milk volume is constant:
    *   Initial milk = $90\%$ of 100 = 90 liters.
    *   Final milk must be $80\%$ of the new total volume $V$.
    *   $$0.80V = 90 \implies V = \frac{90}{0.80} = 112.5 \text{ liters}$$
*   Water added = $112.5 - 100 = 12.5$ liters.
