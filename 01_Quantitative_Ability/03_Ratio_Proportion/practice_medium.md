### Q1
A sum of money is divided among A, B, C, and D in the ratio $3:4:9:10$. If the share of C is 2,580 Rupees more than the share of B, what is the total share of A and D together?

#### Solution
1.  Let the shares of A, B, C, D be $3x$, $4x$, $9x$, $10x$.
2.  We are given that:
    $$C - B = 2580 \implies 9x - 4x = 2580 \implies 5x = 2580 \implies x = 516$$
3.  Total share of A and D together = $3x + 10x = 13x$.
4.  $$\text{Value} = 13 \times 516 = 6708 \text{ Rupees}$$

### Q2
A bag contains 50p, 25p, and 10p coins in the ratio $5:9:4$ amounting to 206 Rupees. Find the individual number of coins of each denomination.

#### Solution
1.  Express denominations in Rupees: $0.50$, $0.25$, $0.10$.
2.  Let coin counts be $5x$, $9x$, $4x$.
3.  Value equation:
    $$(5x \times 0.50) + (9x \times 0.25) + (4x \times 0.10) = 206$$
    $$2.5x + 2.25x + 0.40x = 206$$
    $$5.15x = 206 \implies x = \frac{206}{5.15} = 40$$
4.  Coin counts:
    *   50p coins = $5 \times 40 = 200$
    *   25p coins = $9 \times 40 = 360$
    *   10p coins = $4 \times 40 = 160$

### Q3
The ratio of incomes of two persons is $9:7$ and the ratio of their expenditures is $4:3$. If each of them saves 200 Rupees per month, find their monthly incomes.

#### Solution
1.  Let incomes be $9x$ and $7x$, and expenditures be $4y$ and $3y$.
2.  Equations:
    $$9x - 4y = 200 \implies \times 3 \implies 27x - 12y = 600$$
    $$7x - 3y = 200 \implies \times 4 \implies 28x - 12y = 800$$
3.  Subtract the first from the second:
    $$x = 200$$
4.  Incomes:
    *   Person 1 Income = $9 \times 200 = 1800$ Rupees
    *   Person 2 Income = $7 \times 200 = 1400$ Rupees

### Q4
In a mixture of 60 liters, the ratio of milk and water is $2:1$. If this ratio is to be $1:2$, what quantity of water should be further added?

#### Solution
1.  Initial volume = 60 liters.
2.  Milk = $\frac{2}{3} \times 60 = 40$ liters. Water = 20 liters.
3.  Let the water added be $x$ liters. New milk to water ratio must be $1:2$:
    $$\frac{40}{20 + x} = \frac{1}{2}$$
4.  $$80 = 20 + x \implies x = 60 \text{ liters}$$

### Q5
A and B enter into a partnership. A invests 16,000 Rupees for 8 months and B remains in the business for 4 months. If B's share of profit is $\frac{2}{7}$ of the total profit, how much money did B invest?

#### Solution
1.  Let B's investment be $I_B$.
2.  If B's profit is $\frac{2}{7}$ of the total, A's profit is $\frac{5}{7}$ of the total.
3.  Profit ratio $P_A : P_B = 5:2$.
4.  $$P_A : P_B = (I_A \times T_A) : (I_B \times T_B)$$
    $$\frac{5}{2} = \frac{16000 \times 8}{I_B \times 4} = \frac{32000}{I_B}$$
5.  $$5I_B = 64000 \implies I_B = 12,800 \text{ Rupees}$$

### Q6
In what ratio must tea worth 62 Rupees per kg be mixed with tea worth 72 Rupees per kg so that the mixture is worth 64.50 Rupees per kg?

#### Solution
1.  Cheaper cost ($C$) = 62. Dearer cost ($D$) = 72. Mean cost ($M$) = 64.50.
2.  Apply Alligation Rule:
    $$\frac{q_c}{q_d} = \frac{D - M}{M - C} = \frac{72 - 64.50}{64.50 - 62} = \frac{7.5}{2.5} = \frac{3}{1}$$
3.  Ratio is **3 : 1**.

### Q7
Three containers have their volumes in the ratio $3:4:5$. They are full of mixtures of milk and water in the ratios $4:1$, $3:1$, and $5:2$ respectively. If contents of all three are poured into a single vessel, find the ratio of milk and water in it.

#### Solution
1.  Let the total volumes of the containers be 15, 20, and 25 units respectively (maintaining $3:4:5$ ratio).
2.  Container 1 (Ratio 4:1): Milk = $12$, Water = $3$.
3.  Container 2 (Ratio 3:1): Milk = $15$, Water = $5$.
4.  Container 3 (Ratio 5:2): Milk = $\frac{5}{7} \times 25 = \frac{125}{7}$, Water = $\frac{2}{7} \times 25 = \frac{50}{7}$.
5.  Total Milk = $12 + 15 + \frac{125}{7} = 27 + 17.86 = 44.86$ or $\frac{189 + 125}{7} = \frac{314}{7}$.
6.  Total Water = $3 + 5 + \frac{50}{7} = 8 + \frac{50}{7} = \frac{106}{7}$.
7.  Ratio of Milk to Water = $\frac{314}{7} : \frac{106}{7} = 314 : 106 = 157 : 53$.

### Q8
A sum of 1,300 Rupees is divided among A, B, C, and D such that:
$$\frac{\text{A's share}}{\text{B's share}} = \frac{\text{B's share}}{\text{C's share}} = \frac{\text{C's share}}{\text{D's share}} = \frac{2}{3}$$
Find A's share.

#### Solution
1.  Let $D = x$.
2.  $C = \frac{2}{3}D = \frac{2}{3}x$.
3.  $B = \frac{2}{3}C = \frac{4}{9}x$.
4.  $A = \frac{2}{3}B = \frac{8}{27}x$.
5.  Total sum:
    $$A + B + C + D = x\left(\frac{8}{27} + \frac{4}{9} + \frac{2}{3} + 1\right) = 1300$$
    $$x\left(\frac{8 + 12 + 18 + 27}{27}\right) = 1300 \implies x\left(\frac{65}{27}\right) = 1300$$
6.  $$x = \frac{1300 \times 27}{65} = 20 \times 27 = 540$$
7.  A's share = $\frac{8}{27} \times 540 = 8 \times 20 = 160$ Rupees.

### Q9
If $0.75 : x :: 5 : 8$, find the value of $x$.

#### Solution
1.  $$\frac{0.75}{x} = \frac{5}{8}$$
2.  $$5x = 0.75 \times 8 = 6 \implies x = \frac{6}{5} = 1.2$$

### Q10
A, B, and C invest capital to start a business. A invests 3 times as much as B, and B invests $\frac{2}{3}$ of what C invests. Find the ratio of their investments.

#### Solution
1.  Let C's investment be $3x$.
2.  B's investment = $\frac{2}{3} \times 3x = 2x$.
3.  A's investment = $3 \times B = 3 \times 2x = 6x$.
4.  Ratio $A:B:C = 6x : 2x : 3x = 6:2:3$.

### Q11
If 10 men can build a wall of 100 meters in 5 days, how many men are needed to build a wall of 200 meters in 10 days?

#### Solution
1.  Standard work relation: $\frac{M_1 D_1}{W_1} = \frac{M_2 D_2}{W_2}$.
2.  $$\frac{10 \times 5}{100} = \frac{M_2 \times 10}{200}$$
    $$\frac{50}{100} = \frac{10 M_2}{200} \implies \frac{1}{2} = \frac{M_2}{20} \implies M_2 = 10 \text{ men}$$

### Q12
Two numbers are in the ratio $3:4$. If 6 is added to each, the ratio becomes $4:5$. Find the numbers.

#### Solution
1.  Let the numbers be $3x$ and $4x$.
2.  $$\frac{3x + 6}{4x + 6} = \frac{4}{5}$$
3.  $$5(3x + 6) = 4(4x + 6) \implies 15x + 30 = 16x + 24 \implies x = 6$$
4.  Numbers are $3(6) = 18$ and $4(6) = 24$.

### Q13
A mixture of 20 liters contains milk and water in the ratio $3:1$. How many liters of milk must be added so that the resulting ratio is $4:1$?

#### Solution
1.  Total volume = 20 liters.
2.  Milk = $15$ liters, Water = $5$ liters.
3.  Let milk added be $x$ liters.
    $$\frac{15 + x}{5} = \frac{4}{1} \implies 15 + x = 20 \implies x = 5 \text{ liters}$$

### Q14
The profit of a business is divided between two partners A and B in the ratio $3:2$. If A invested 6000 Rupees, how much did B invest if both invested for the same duration?

#### Solution
1.  Since duration is equal, profit ratio equals investment ratio: $I_A : I_B = P_A : P_B$.
2.  $$\frac{6000}{I_B} = \frac{3}{2} \implies 3I_B = 12000 \implies I_B = 4000 \text{ Rupees}$$

### Q15
Find the compound ratio of $1:2$, $3:4$, and $5:6$.

#### Solution
1.  $$\text{Compound Ratio} = \frac{1}{2} \times \frac{3}{4} \times \frac{5}{6} = \frac{15}{48} = \frac{5}{16} \rightarrow 5:16$$
