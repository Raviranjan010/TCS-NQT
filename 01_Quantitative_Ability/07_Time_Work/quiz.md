## Part 1 — 10 Rapid Q&A Pairs

### Q1
If A completes a task in 8 days and B in 12 days, what is the LCM to assume for work?
*   **Answer:** 24 units
*   **Explanation:** LCM of 8 and 12 is 24.

### Q2
What is B's efficiency if B takes 12 days and total work is 24 units?
*   **Answer:** 2 units/day
*   **Explanation:** $24 / 12 = 2$.

### Q3
If A and B have efficiencies of 3 and 2 units/day respectively, how long to complete 20 units together?
*   **Answer:** 4 days
*   **Explanation:** Combined efficiency $= 3 + 2 = 5 \implies 	ext{Time} = 20 / 5 = 4$ days.

### Q4
If 6 men do a work in 10 days, how long do 10 men take?
*   **Answer:** 6 days
*   **Explanation:** $6 	imes 10 = 10 	imes D_2 \implies D_2 = 6$.

### Q5
An inlet fills in 3 hours, leak empties in 6 hours. Find net time to fill.
*   **Answer:** 6 hours
*   **Explanation:** $	ext{Net Rate} = rac{1}{3} - rac{1}{6} = rac{1}{6} \implies 6$ hours.

### Q6
If efficiency of A:B is 3:4, what is the ratio of time taken by them?
*   **Answer:** $4:3$
*   **Explanation:** Time and efficiency are inversely proportional.

### Q7
If A works 2 days and B works 3 days, and they have equal efficiency, what is their wage ratio?
*   **Answer:** $2:3$
*   **Explanation:** Wages split in ratio of work done $= 1(2) : 1(3) = 2:3$.

### Q8
If A can do a work in 5 days, what fraction is done in 2 days?
*   **Answer:** $2/5$
*   **Explanation:** Work done $= 2 	imes (1/5) = 2/5$.

### Q9
If A is twice as efficient as B, and they take 6 days together, find B's alone time.
*   **Answer:** 18 days
*   **Explanation:** Let B rate $= x$, A rate $= 2x$. Combined rate $= 3x = 1/6 \implies x = 1/18 \implies B$ takes 18 days.

### Q10
If 5 men build a shed in 8 days, how many men do it in 10 days?
*   **Answer:** 4 men
*   **Explanation:** $5 	imes 8 = M_2 	imes 10 \implies M_2 = 4$.

## Part 2 — 5 True/False Statements

### 1. Statement
The time taken to complete a work is directly proportional to the worker's efficiency.
*   **Answer:** **FALSE**
*   **Explanation:** Time is inversely proportional to efficiency (higher efficiency takes less time).

### 2. Statement
In pipes and cisterns, leak rates are treated as negative work rates.
*   **Answer:** **TRUE**
*   **Explanation:** Leaks remove water, opposing the positive work of inlets.

### 3. Statement
When workers work together for different durations, wages must be shared in the ratio of their efficiencies.
*   **Answer:** **FALSE**
*   **Explanation:** Wages must be shared in the ratio of the actual work done by each (Efficiency $	imes$ Days).

### 4. Statement
In alternating days work starting with A, the remaining work is always completed by B.
*   **Answer:** **FALSE**
*   **Explanation:** The remaining work is completed by whichever worker's turn it is on that specific day.
