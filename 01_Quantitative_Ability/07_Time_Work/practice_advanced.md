### Q1
A tank has a leak which can empty it in 8 hours. An inlet pipe fills water at the rate of 6 liters per minute. When the tank is full, the inlet is opened, and due to the leak, the tank is emptied in 12 hours. Find the capacity of the tank.

#### Standard Method
1.  Let the leak rate be $L$ (empties in 8 hours $ightarrow -1/8$ tank/hour).
2.  Let the net rate (with leak and inlet $I$) empty the tank in 12 hours $ightarrow -1/12$ tank/hour.
    $$I - L = -rac{1}{12} \implies I - rac{1}{8} = -rac{1}{12}$$
    $$I = rac{1}{8} - rac{1}{12} = rac{3 - 2}{24} = rac{1}{24} 	ext{ tank/hour}$$
3.  Inlet fills the tank in 24 hours.
4.  Inlet flow rate = 6 liters/minute $= 6 	imes 60 = 360$ liters/hour.
5.  Capacity = Flow rate $	imes$ filling time $= 360 	imes 24 = 8,640$ liters.

#### Alternate Fast Method
*   Let Capacity = LCM of 8 and 12 = 24 units.
*   Leak rate = -3 units/hour. Net rate = -2 units/hour.
*   Inlet rate = $-2 - (-3) = +1$ unit/hour.
*   Time taken by inlet to fill = $24 / 1 = 24$ hours.
*   Capacity $= 24 	ext{ hours} 	imes 60 	ext{ min/hour} 	imes 6 	ext{ L/min} = 8,640$ liters.

### Q2
A, B, and C can complete a work in 10, 20, and 30 days respectively. They work on alternate days starting with A, followed by B, then C. On which day will the work be completed?

#### Standard Method
1.  Assume Total Work = LCM of 10, 20, and 30 = 60 units.
2.  Efficiencies: A = 6, B = 3, C = 2 units/day.
3.  Define the 3-day cycle:
    *   Day 1 (A) = 6 units
    *   Day 2 (B) = 3 units
    *   Day 3 (C) = 2 units
    *   Total in 3 days = 11 units.
4.  Divide total work: $60 / 11 = 5$ cycles.
    *   Work in 5 cycles (15 days) $= 5 	imes 11 = 55$ units.
    *   Remaining work $= 60 - 55 = 5$ units.
5.  Day 16 (A's turn): A's efficiency is 6 units/day. Since remaining work is 5 units, A completes it in $5/6$ of the day.
6.  The work is completed on the **16th day**.

#### Alternate Fast Method
*   Cycles: $55$ units in 15 days.
*   Remaining 5 units allocated to A (who can do 6). Work ends on day 16.

### Q3
A and B can do a work in 12 days, B and C in 15 days, and C and A in 20 days. In how many days can A, B, and C complete the work working together?

#### Standard Method
1.  Daily rates:
    *   $A + B = 1/12$
    *   $B + C = 1/15$
    *   $C + A = 1/20$
2.  Add the three equations:
    $$2(A + B + C) = rac{1}{12} + rac{1}{15} + rac{1}{20}$$
    $$2(A + B + C) = rac{5 + 4 + 3}{60} = rac{12}{60} = rac{1}{5}$$
    $$A + B + C = rac{1}{10}$$
3.  Together they take **10 days**.

#### Alternate Fast Method
*   LCM of 12, 15, 20 = 60 units.
*   Efficiencies: $A+B=5, B+C=4, C+A=3$. Sum $= 12$.
*   Since each is counted twice, total efficiency of A, B, C $= 12 / 2 = 6$ units/day.
*   Time $= 60 / 6 = 10$ days.

### Q4
4 men and 6 women can complete a work in 8 days, while 3 men and 7 women can complete it in 10 days. In how many days will 10 women complete it?

#### Standard Method
1.  Let daily rate of 1 man be $M$ and 1 woman be $W$.
    $$8(4M + 6W) = 1 \implies 32M + 48W = 1$$
    $$10(3M + 7W) = 1 \implies 30M + 70W = 1$$
2.  Solve for $M$ and $W$:
    *   Multiply (1) by 30 and (2) by 32:
        $$960M + 1440W = 30$$
        $$960M + 2240W = 32$$
    *   Subtract: $800W = 2 \implies W = rac{1}{400}$.
3.  One woman takes 400 days.
4.  10 women take $400 / 10 = 40$ days.

#### Alternate Fast Method
*   Total work: $8(4M + 6W) = 10(3M + 7W) \implies 32M + 48W = 30M + 70W \implies 2M = 22W \implies 1M = 11W$.
*   Substitute $1M = 11W$ into first equation:
    *   $4(11W) + 6W = 50W$ complete in 8 days.
    *   Therefore, 10 women take $50 	imes 8 / 10 = 40$ days.

### Q5
A can do a work in 25 days and B in 20 days. They work together for 5 days and then A leaves. In how many days will B finish the remaining work?

#### Standard Method
1.  LCM of 25 and 20 = 100 units.
2.  Efficiencies: A = 4, B = 5.
3.  Work in 5 days $= 5 	imes 9 = 45$ units. Remaining $= 55$ units.
4.  B finishes in $55 / 5 = 11$ days.

#### Alternate Fast Method
*   Remaining work $= 100 - 45 = 55$. B's efficiency $= 5 \implies 11$ days.

### Q6
Two pipes A and B can fill a tank in 24 minutes and 32 minutes respectively. If both pipes are opened together, after how many minutes should B be closed so that the tank is full in 18 minutes?

#### Standard Method
1.  LCM of 24 and 32 = 96 units.
2.  Rates: A = 4 units/min, B = 3 units/min.
3.  Let B be closed after $x$ minutes.
    *   A works for all 18 minutes $ightarrow 18 	imes 4 = 72$ units.
    *   B works for $x$ minutes $ightarrow 3x$ units.
4.  $72 + 3x = 96 \implies 3x = 24 \implies x = 8$ minutes.

#### Alternate Fast Method
*   Work done by A in 18 mins $= 72$ units.
*   Remaining $96 - 72 = 24$ units completed by B $ightarrow 24 / 3 = 8$ minutes.

### Q8
A, B, and C can do a work in 10, 12, and 15 days respectively. They started together, but A left after 2 days and B left 3 days before completion. Find total days.

#### Standard Method
1.  Let total days be $x$.
2.  $A(2) + B(x-3) + C(x) = 60 \implies 12 + 5x - 15 + 4x = 60 \implies 9x = 63 \implies x = 7$ days.

### Q9
Three pipes A, B, and C can fill a tank in 6 hours. After working together for 2 hours, C is closed and A and B can fill it in 7 hours. Find time taken by C alone.

#### Standard Method
1.  $(A+B+C) = 1/6$. Work in 2 hours $= 2/6 = 1/3$. Remaining $= 2/3$.
2.  A and B fill $2/3$ in 7 hours $\implies (A+B) 	ext{ rate} = rac{2}{3 	imes 7} = rac{2}{21}$.
3.  C rate $= (A+B+C) - (A+B) = rac{1}{6} - rac{2}{21} = rac{7 - 4}{42} = rac{3}{42} = rac{1}{14}$.
4.  C alone takes **14 hours**.
