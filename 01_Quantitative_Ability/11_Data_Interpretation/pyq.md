# Data Interpretation — Previous Year Style Questions

## DI Set 1 — Sales Data Table (TCS 2023 Style)

| Month | North | South | East | West |
|-------|-------|-------|------|------|
| Jan   | 120   | 80    | 60   | 100  |
| Feb   | 140   | 90    | 70   | 110  |
| Mar   | 130   | 100   | 80   | 120  |
| Apr   | 160   | 110   | 90   | 130  |

Units: Thousands

### PYQ1
Total sales in January?
- 120+80+60+100 = **360 (thousands)**

### PYQ2
Which region had highest total sales?
- North: 550; South: 380; East: 300; West: 460; **North is highest**

### PYQ3
% increase in East sales from Jan to Apr?
- (90-60)/60 × 100 = **50%**

### PYQ4
Average monthly sales of South?
- (80+90+100+110)/4 = 380/4 = **95 thousand**

### PYQ5
Ratio of North to West in February?
- 140:110 = **14:11**

## DI Set 2 — Pie Chart (TCS 2022 Style)

Budget allocation (Total: 60 lakhs):
- Salaries: 40%
- Infrastructure: 25%
- Marketing: 20%
- Research: 10%
- Miscellaneous: 5%

### PYQ6
Amount on Salaries?
- 40% of 60 = **24 lakhs**

### PYQ7
Amount on Infrastructure and Marketing combined?
- 45% of 60 = **27 lakhs**

### PYQ8
How much more is Salary than Research?
- (40-10)% of 60 = 30% of 60 = **18 lakhs**

### PYQ9
If Miscellaneous increases by 20%, new Misc amount?
- 5% of 60 = 3; 3 × 1.2 = **3.6 lakhs**

### PYQ10
Ratio of Marketing to Miscellaneous?
- 20:5 = **4:1**

## DI Set 3 — Bar Graph (TCS 2021 Style)

Students in 5 courses (in hundreds): A=12, B=8, C=15, D=10, E=5

### PYQ11
Total students in all courses?
- (12+8+15+10+5) × 100 = **5000 students**

### PYQ12
Which course has fewest students?
- **E (500)**

### PYQ13
A's students as % of total?
- 1200/5000 × 100 = **24%**

### PYQ14
Ratio of C to D students?
- 1500:1000 = **3:2**

### PYQ15
How many more students in A than in B?
- (12-8) × 100 = **400**
