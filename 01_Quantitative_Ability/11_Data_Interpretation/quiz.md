# Data Interpretation — Rapid Quiz

## Use This Table

| Product | Q1 | Q2 | Q3 | Q4 |
|---------|----|----|----|----|
| A       | 20 | 30 | 25 | 35 |
| B       | 15 | 20 | 30 | 25 |
| C       | 25 | 25 | 20 | 30 |
| Total   | 60 | 75 | 75 | 90 |

(Values in units)

## Q&A

Q1: Which product sold most in Q2?
A1: A (30 units)

Q2: Total annual sales of Product B?
A2: 15+20+30+25 = 90

Q3: % change in total sales from Q1 to Q4?
A3: (90-60)/60 × 100 = 50%

Q4: Ratio of A to B in Q3?
A4: 25:30 = 5:6

Q5: Average quarterly sales of C?
A5: (25+25+20+30)/4 = 100/4 = 25

Q6: Which quarter had highest overall sales?
A6: Q3 and Q4 both = 75; Q4 = 90 → Q4

Q7: Product A's Q4 sales as % of total Q4?
A7: 35/90 × 100 = 38.9%

Q8: How much more did A sell than C in Q4?
A8: 35-30 = 5 units

Q9: Total sales across all products and quarters?
A9: 60+75+75+90 = 300 units

Q10: Which product had the most consistent quarterly sales?
A10: C (25, 25, 20, 30 — smallest range)

## True/False

1. % change uses new value as base → **FALSE** (uses old value)
2. "By how much" means subtract the values → **TRUE**
3. Pie chart sector of 90° = 25% → **TRUE** (90/360 = 0.25)
4. For average, count the total number of items including totals row → **FALSE**
5. Scale units must always be checked before reading any value → **TRUE**
