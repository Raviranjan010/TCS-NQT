### Q2
A shopkeeper sells a table at a $10\%$ profit. If he had bought it at $10\%$ less cost and sold it for 10 Rupees more, he would have gained $25\%$. Find the Cost Price of the table.

#### Standard Method
1.  Let the original Cost Price be $C$.
2.  Original SP = $1.10C$.
3.  New CP = $0.90C$.
4.  New SP = $1.10C + 10$.
5.  New profit is $25\%$ on new CP:
    $$1.10C + 10 = 0.90C 	imes 1.25$$
    $$1.10C + 10 = 1.125C \implies 0.025C = 10 \implies C = rac{10}{0.025} = 400 	ext{ Rupees}$$

#### Alternate Fast Method
*   Let CP = 100 units. Original SP = 110 units.
*   New CP = 90 units.
*   New SP for $25\%$ gain = $90 	imes 1.25 = 112.5$ units.
*   Difference in SP = $112.5 - 110 = 2.5$ units = 10 Rupees.
*   CP (100 units) = $10 	imes rac{100}{2.5} = 400$ Rupees.
