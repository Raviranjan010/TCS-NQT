# Average — Rapid Quiz (10 Q&As + 5 True/False)

Test your conceptual understanding and speed with this quick self-assessment.

## Part 1 — 10 Rapid Q&A Pairs

### Q1
What is the average of the first 5 natural numbers?
*   **Answer:** 3
*   **Explanation:** $\frac{1 + 2 + 3 + 4 + 5}{5} = 3$ (or middle term of 1, 2, 3, 4, 5).

### Q2
A car travels at 30 km/h and returns at 60 km/h. Find the average speed.
*   **Answer:** 40 km/h
*   **Explanation:** $\frac{2(30)(60)}{30 + 60} = \frac{3600}{90} = 40 \text{ km/h}$.

### Q3
If the average of 5 numbers is 20, what is their sum?
*   **Answer:** 100
*   **Explanation:** $\text{Sum} = \text{Average} \times \text{Count} = 20 \times 5 = 100$.

### Q4
The average age of 10 students is 15. If a teacher of 37 years joins, what is the new average?
*   **Answer:** 17 years
*   **Explanation:** New sum = $150 + 37 = 187$. New average = $187 / 11 = 17$.

### Q5
What is the average of consecutive numbers from 1 to 9?
*   **Answer:** 5
*   **Explanation:** $\frac{1 + 9}{2} = 5$.

### Q6
The average weight of 5 people is 50 kg. If one person of 50 kg leaves, what is the new average?
*   **Answer:** 50 kg
*   **Explanation:** Removing a term equal to the average does not change the average.

### Q7
If each of 10 observations is increased by 5, how does the average change?
*   **Answer:** Increases by 5
*   **Explanation:** Adding $k$ to every element increases the mean by $k$.

### Q8
Find the average of first 3 multiples of 4.
*   **Answer:** 8
*   **Explanation:** Multiples are 4, 8, 12. Average is middle term = 8.

### Q9
If the average of $A$ and $B$ is 10, and $B$ and $C$ is 15, find $C - A$.
*   **Answer:** 10
*   **Explanation:** $A+B = 20$, $B+C = 30$. Subtracting gives $C-A = 10$.

### Q10
A cyclist travels 1 hour at 10 km/h and 1 hour at 20 km/h. Find the average speed.
*   **Answer:** 15 km/h
*   **Explanation:** Since time is equal, average speed is arithmetic mean: $(10 + 20) / 2 = 15$.

## Part 2 — 5 True/False Statements

### 1. Statement
The average speed for a round trip is always equal to the arithmetic mean of the two speeds.
*   **Answer:** **FALSE**
*   **Explanation:** For equal distance segments (like a round trip), the average speed is the Harmonic Mean, which is always less than the arithmetic mean (unless the two speeds are equal).

### 2. Statement
The net sum of deviations of all observations from their actual mean is always zero.
*   **Answer:** **TRUE**
*   **Explanation:** $\sum (X_i - A) = \sum X_i - N \cdot A = S - S = 0$.

### 3. Statement
If the average of a group increases when a new member joins, the new member's weight must be greater than the group's old average.
*   **Answer:** **TRUE**
*   **Explanation:** Adding a term larger than the mean pulls the mean upward.

### 4. Statement
The average of the first $n$ odd natural numbers is always $n$.
*   **Answer:** **TRUE**
*   **Explanation:** The sum of first $n$ odd numbers is $n^2$, so the average is $n^2 / n = n$.

### 5. Statement
In a weighted average, simplifying the ratios of group sizes changes the final average.
*   **Answer:** **FALSE**
*   **Explanation:** Ratios represent the proportional weights, which yields the exact same average value while reducing calculations.
