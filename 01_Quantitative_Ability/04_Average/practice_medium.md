### Q1
The average salary of all the workers in a workshop is 8,000 Rupees. The average salary of 7 technicians is 12,000 Rupees, and the average salary of the rest is 6,000 Rupees. Find the total number of workers in the workshop.

#### Solution
1.  Let the total number of workers be $x$. The rest of the workers = $x - 7$.
2.  Total salary equation:
    $$12000 \times 7 + 6000 \times (x - 7) = 8000 \times x$$
    $$84000 + 6000x - 42000 = 8000x$$
    $$42000 = 2000x \implies x = 21$$
3.  The total number of workers is **21**.

### Q2
The average age of a husband and his wife was 23 years at the time of their marriage 5 years ago. The average age of the husband, wife, and a child born during the interval is 20 years now. Find the present age of the child.

#### Solution
1.  Sum of husband's and wife's ages 5 years ago = $23 \times 2 = 46$ years.
2.  Sum of their present ages (since each aged 5 years) = $46 + (2 \times 5) = 56$ years.
3.  Sum of present ages of husband, wife, and child = $20 \times 3 = 60$ years.
4.  Present age of child = $60 - 56 = 4$ years.

### Q3
The average weight of 8 persons increases by 2.5 kg when a new person comes in place of one of them weighing 65 kg. Find the weight of the new person.

#### Solution
1.  Apply the substitution deviation formula:
    $$\text{New Weight} = \text{Replaced Weight} + N \times (\text{Average Change})$$
2.  Here, $N = 8$, replaced weight = $65$ kg, change = $+2.5$ kg.
3.  $$\text{New Weight} = 65 + 8 \times 2.5 = 65 + 20 = 85 \text{ kg}$$

### Q4
A batsman makes a score of 87 runs in the 17th inning and thus increases his average by 3 runs. Find his average after the 17th inning.

#### Solution
1.  Let the average before the 17th inning (i.e., for 16 innings) be $A$.
2.  Total runs after 17 innings = $16A + 87$.
3.  New average = $A + 3$.
4.  $$17(A + 3) = 16A + 87$$
    $$17A + 51 = 16A + 87 \implies A = 36$$
5.  Average after the 17th inning = $A + 3 = 39$.

### Q5
The average of 5 consecutive odd numbers is 25. What is the product of the smallest and largest of these numbers?

#### Solution
1.  Since the numbers are consecutive odds, the average is the middle (3rd) term: 25.
2.  The series of 5 odd numbers is: $21, 23, 25, 27, 29$.
3.  Smallest = 21, Largest = 29.
4.  $$\text{Product} = 21 \times 29 = 609$$

### Q6
The average of 11 results is 50. If the average of the first six results is 49 and that of the last six is 52, find the sixth result.

#### Solution
1.  Sum of all 11 results = $11 \times 50 = 550$.
2.  Sum of first 6 results = $6 \times 49 = 294$.
3.  Sum of last 6 results = $6 \times 52 = 312$.
4.  The 6th result is included in both groups, so:
    $$\text{6th Result} = (\text{Sum}_1 + \text{Sum}_2) - \text{Total Sum} = (294 + 312) - 550 = 606 - 550 = 56$$

### Q7
An aeroplane flies along the four sides of a square field at speeds of 200, 400, 600, and 800 km/h. Find the average speed of the plane around the field.

#### Solution
1.  Let the side of the square field be $D$ km.
2.  Total distance = $4D$.
3.  Total time = $\frac{D}{200} + \frac{D}{400} + \frac{D}{600} + \frac{D}{800} = D\left(\frac{12 + 6 + 4 + 3}{2400}\right) = \frac{25D}{2400}$.
4.  $$\text{Average Speed} = \frac{\text{Total Distance}}{\text{Total Time}} = \frac{4D}{\frac{25D}{2400}} = \frac{4 \times 2400}{25} = 4 \times 96 = 384 \text{ km/h}$$

### Q8
Out of 9 persons, 8 persons spent 30 Rupees each for their meals. The 9th person spent 20 Rupees more than the average expenditure of all the nine. Find the total money spent by all of them.

#### Solution
1.  Let the average expenditure of all 9 persons be $A$.
2.  Total expenditure = $9A$.
3.  Expenditure of the 9th person = $A + 20$.
4.  Equation:
    $$8 \times 30 + (A + 20) = 9A$$
    $$240 + A + 20 = 9A \implies 260 = 8A \implies A = 32.5 \text{ Rupees}$$
5.  Total money spent = $9 \times 32.5 = 292.5 \text{ Rupees}$.

### Q9
The average of 5 numbers is 27. If one number is excluded, the average becomes 25. Find the excluded number.

#### Solution
1.  Initial Sum = $5 \times 27 = 135$.
2.  New Sum (with 4 numbers) = $4 \times 25 = 100$.
3.  Excluded number = $135 - 100 = 35$.

### Q10
The average temperature for Monday, Tuesday, and Wednesday was 40 °C. The average for Tuesday, Wednesday, and Thursday was 41 °C. If Thursday's temperature was 42 °C, find Monday's temperature.

#### Solution
1.  $M + T + W = 3 \times 40 = 120$.
2.  $T + W + Th = 3 \times 41 = 123$.
3.  Subtract equation (1) from (2):
    $$Th - M = 123 - 120 = 3$$
4.  Since $Th = 42$:
    $$42 - M = 3 \implies M = 39 \text{ °C}$$

### Q11
A library has an average of 510 visitors on Sundays and 240 on other days. Find the average number of visitors per day in a month of 30 days starting with a Sunday.

#### Solution
1.  Since the month has 30 days and starts with a Sunday:
    *   Number of Sundays = 5.
    *   Other days = 25.
2.  Combined average = $\frac{5 \times 510 + 25 \times 240}{30} = \frac{2550 + 6000}{30} = \frac{8550}{30} = 285$ visitors.

### Q12
In an exam, the average marks of 40 students is 72. Later, it was discovered that two marks, 64 and 62, were misread as 84 and 42. Find the correct average.

#### Solution
1.  Original Sum of marks = $40 \times 72 = 2880$.
2.  Difference in sum = $(\text{Correct marks}) - (\text{Incorrect marks}) = (64 + 62) - (84 + 42) = 126 - 126 = 0$.
3.  Since the difference is 0, the correct average remains **72**.

### Q13
Find the average of the first 20 multiples of 7.

#### Solution
1.  The multiples are $7, 14, 21, \dots, 140$.
2.  Since this is an Arithmetic Progression:
    $$\text{Average} = \frac{\text{First Term} + \text{Last Term}}{2} = \frac{7 + 140}{2} = 73.5$$

### Q14
The average weight of a class of 30 students is 45 kg. If the teacher's weight is included, the average increases by 0.5 kg. Find the teacher's weight.

#### Solution
1.  $$\text{Teacher's Weight} = \text{New Average} + N \times (\text{Average Increase})$$
    $$\text{Teacher's Weight} = 45.5 + 30 \times 0.5 = 45.5 + 15 = 60.5 \text{ kg}$$

### Q15
The average age of 8 men is increased by 2 years when two of them whose ages are 21 years and 23 years are replaced by two new men. Find the average age of the two new men.

#### Solution
1.  Let the sum of ages of two new men be $S$.
2.  $$S = (\text{Ages of replaced men}) + N \times (\text{Average Increase})$$
    $$S = (21 + 23) + 8 \times 2 = 44 + 16 = 60 \text{ years}$$
3.  Average age of two new men = $60 / 2 = 30$ years.
