# Average — Top 20 Most Repeated TCS-Style Questions

*   **TCS NQT Target:** Complete coverage of core exam variations.
*   **Marking Scheme:** +1.0 for Correct, -0.33 for Incorrect (Objective).

### Q1
The average age of 36 students in a group is 14 years. When the teacher's age is included, the average increases by 1 year. Find the teacher's age.
*   **Difficulty:** Easy | **Expected Time:** 45s | **TCS Frequency:** High
*   **Solution:**
    *   $$\text{Teacher's Age} = \text{New Average} + N \times (\text{Average Increase}) = 15 + 36 \times 1 = 51 \text{ years}$$.

---

### Q2
A batsman in his 17th inning makes a score of 85 runs and thereby increases his average by 3 runs. What is his average after the 17th inning?
*   **Difficulty:** Medium | **Expected Time:** 90s | **TCS Frequency:** High
*   **Solution:**
    *   Let the average before the 17th inning be $A$.
    *   $17(A+3) = 16A + 85 \implies 17A + 51 = 16A + 85 \implies A = 34$.
    *   New Average = $34 + 3 = 37$ runs.

### Q3
The average weight of 8 men increases by 1.5 kg when a new man replaces one weighing 65 kg. Find the weight of the new man.
*   **Difficulty:** Easy | **Expected Time:** 30s | **TCS Frequency:** High
*   **Solution:**
    *   $$\text{New Weight} = 65 + 8 \times 1.5 = 65 + 12 = 77 \text{ kg}$$.

### Q4
Find the average of the first 40 natural numbers.
*   **Difficulty:** Easy | **Expected Time:** 20s | **TCS Frequency:** Medium
*   **Solution:**
    *   $$\text{Average} = \frac{n + 1}{2} = \frac{40 + 1}{2} = 20.5$$.

---

### Q5
A car travels at 60 km/h from town A to town B and returns at 90 km/h. Find the average speed.
*   **Difficulty:** Easy | **Expected Time:** 45s | **TCS Frequency:** High
*   **Solution:**
    *   $$v_{\text{avg}} = \frac{2 \times 60 \times 90}{60 + 90} = \frac{10800}{150} = 72 \text{ km/h}$$.

---

### Q6
The average salary of 12 officers is 40,000 Rupees, and the average salary of the rest of the employees is 20,000 Rupees. If the average salary of all employees is 25,000 Rupees, find the total number of employees.
*   **Difficulty:** Medium | **Expected Time:** 90s | **TCS Frequency:** High
*   **Solution:**
    *   Applying Alligation:
        *   Officers (40k) vs Rest (20k), Mean (25k).
        *   Ratio = $(25 - 20) : (40 - 25) = 5 : 15 = 1 : 3$.
    *   Since Officers (1 part) = 12, Rest (3 parts) = 36.
    *   Total employees = $12 + 36 = 48$.

### Q7
The average age of 10 students is 15 years. When 5 new students join, the average age increases by 1 year. Find the average age of the new students.
*   **Difficulty:** Medium | **Expected Time:** 90s | **TCS Frequency:** High
*   **Solution:**
    *   Initial Sum = $10 \times 15 = 150$.
    *   New Sum = $15 \times 16 = 240$.
    *   Sum of 5 new students = $240 - 150 = 90$.
    *   Average of new students = $90 / 5 = 18$ years.

### Q8
The average of 5 consecutive odd numbers is 61. What is the difference between the highest and lowest numbers?
*   **Difficulty:** Easy | **Expected Time:** 30s | **TCS Frequency:** High
*   **Solution:**
    *   For 5 consecutive odd numbers, the difference between largest and smallest is always $2 \times (5 - 1) = 8$.

---

### Q9
Out of 3 numbers, the first is thrice the second and the second is twice the third. If the average is 27, find the largest number.
*   **Difficulty:** Easy | **Expected Time:** 45s | **TCS Frequency:** Medium
*   **Solution:**
    *   Let third = $x$, second = $2x$, first = $6x$.
    *   $$\text{Average} = \frac{6x + 2x + x}{3} = 3x = 27 \implies x = 9$$.
    *   Largest number = $6x = 54$.

### Q10
The average marks of 30 students in a test was 65. Later, it was found that the marks of one student were misread as 85 instead of 55. Find the correct average.
*   **Difficulty:** Easy | **Expected Time:** 45s | **TCS Frequency:** High
*   **Solution:**
    *   Difference in sum = $55 - 85 = -30$.
    *   Change in average = $-30 / 30 = -1$.
    *   Correct average = $65 - 1 = 64$.

### Q11
Find the average of the first 25 multiples of 5.
*   **Difficulty:** Easy | **Expected Time:** 30s | **TCS Frequency:** Medium
*   **Solution:**
    *   Multiples: $5, 10, \dots, 125$.
    *   $$\text{Average} = \frac{5 + 125}{2} = 65$$.

### Q12
If the average of 6 consecutive even numbers is 25, find the largest of these numbers.
*   **Difficulty:** Easy | **Expected Time:** 30s | **TCS Frequency:** High
*   **Solution:**
    *   The middle of 6 numbers is between the 3rd and 4th term.
    *   The numbers are $20, 22, 24, 26, 28, 30$. Largest = 30.

### Q13
A person travels 3 equal distances at speeds of 10, 15, and 30 km/h. Find the average speed.
*   **Difficulty:** Medium | **Expected Time:** 60s | **TCS Frequency:** High
*   **Solution:**
    *   $$v_{\text{avg}} = \frac{3 \times 10 \times 15 \times 30}{10 \times 15 + 15 \times 30 + 30 \times 10} = \frac{13500}{150 + 450 + 300} = \frac{13500}{900} = 15 \text{ km/h}$$.

### Q14
The average weight of 15 students increases by 1.5 kg when a student weighing 40 kg is replaced by a new student. Find the weight of the new student.
*   **Difficulty:** Easy | **Expected Time:** 30s | **TCS Frequency:** High
*   **Solution:**
    *   $$\text{New Weight} = 40 + 15 \times 1.5 = 40 + 22.5 = 62.5 \text{ kg}$$.

### Q15
The average age of 4 members of a family is 32 years. If the age of a guest is included, the average age increases by $12.5\%$. Find the age of the guest.
*   **Difficulty:** Medium | **Expected Time:** 90s | **TCS Frequency:** Medium
*   **Solution:**
    *   Initial average = 32. Increase = $12.5\% = 4$ years.
    *   New Average = 36.
    *   $$\text{Guest's Age} = \text{New Average} + N \times (\text{Average Increase}) = 36 + 4 \times 4 = 52 \text{ years}$$.

### Q17
A batsman has an average of 30 runs in 40 innings. In the next inning, he scores 0 runs. What is his new average?
*   **Difficulty:** Easy | **Expected Time:** 30s | **TCS Frequency:** Medium
*   **Solution:**
    *   Initial sum = $40 \times 30 = 1200$.
    *   New average = $1200 / 41 \approx 29.27$ runs.

### Q18
The average temperature of Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday, and Thursday was 38 °C, and that of Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday, and Friday was 40 °C. If Monday's temperature was 30 °C, find Friday's temperature.
*   **Difficulty:** Medium | **Expected Time:** 60s | **TCS Frequency:** Medium
*   **Solution:**
    *   $F - M = 4 \times (40 - 38) = 8$.
    *   Since $M = 30$, Friday's temperature = $30 + 8 = 38 \text{ °C}$.

---

### Q19
The average of 8 numbers is 12. If each number is multiplied by 3, what is the new average?
*   **Difficulty:** Easy | **Expected Time:** 15s | **TCS Frequency:** High
*   **Solution:**
    *   If each observation is scaled by $k$, the average is also scaled by $k$.
    *   New Average = $12 \times 3 = 36$.

### Q20
If the average of $x$ and $1/x$ is $M$, find the average of $x^2$ and $1/x^2$.
*   **Difficulty:** Hard | **Expected Time:** 120s | **TCS Frequency:** Low
*   **Solution:**
    *   $\frac{x + 1/x}{2} = M \implies x + 1/x = 2M$.
    *   Squaring both sides: $x^2 + 1/x^2 + 2 = 4M^2 \implies x^2 + 1/x^2 = 4M^2 - 2$.
    *   Average of $x^2$ and $1/x^2$ = $\frac{x^2 + 1/x^2}{2} = \frac{4M^2 - 2}{2} = 2M^2 - 1$.
