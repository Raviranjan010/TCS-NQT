### Q1
The average weight of 3 men A, B, and C is 84 kg. Another man D joins the group and the average now becomes 80 kg. If another man E, whose weight is 3 kg more than that of D, replaces A, then the average weight of B, C, D, and E becomes 79 kg. Find the weight of A.

#### Standard Method
1.  Sum of weights of A, B, C = $84 \times 3 = 252$ kg.
2.  Sum of weights of A, B, C, D = $80 \times 4 = 320$ kg.
    *   Weight of D = $320 - 252 = 68$ kg.
3.  Weight of E = $68 + 3 = 71$ kg.
4.  Sum of weights of B, C, D, E = $79 \times 4 = 316$ kg.
5.  Subtract sum of B, C, D, E from A, B, C, D:
    $$(A + B + C + D) - (B + C + D + E) = 320 - 316$$
    $$A - E = 4 \implies A = E + 4 = 71 + 4 = 75 \text{ kg}$$

#### Alternate Fast Method
*   D's weight = $80 \times 4 - 84 \times 3 = 320 - 252 = 68$ kg.
*   E's weight = $68 + 3 = 71$ kg.
*   Comparing the groups $\{A, B, C, D\}$ (average 80) and $\{E, B, C, D\}$ (average 79):
    *   The only difference is A is replaced by E, causing the average of 4 people to decrease by 1 kg.
    *   Total weight decrease = $4 \times 1 = 4$ kg.
    *   Therefore, $A - E = 4 \implies A = 71 + 4 = 75$ kg.

### Q2
A batsman has a certain average of runs for 11 innings. In the 12th inning, he makes a score of 90 runs and thereby decreases his average by 5 runs. Find his average after the 12th inning.

#### Standard Method
1.  Let the average for 11 innings be $A$. Total runs = $11A$.
2.  After 12 innings, new average = $A - 5$.
    $$12(A - 5) = 11A + 90 \implies 12A - 60 = 11A + 90 \implies A = 150$$
3.  Average after 12th inning = $A - 5 = 145$ runs.

#### Alternate Fast Method
*   Since the 12th inning score of 90 runs decreased the average of all 12 innings by 5:
    *   Total deficit = $12 \times 5 = 60$ runs.
    *   Therefore, the previous average must have been $90 + 60 = 150$.
    *   New average = $150 - 5 = 145$ runs.

### Q3
The average salary of all the employees in a firm is 5,000 Rupees. The average salary of male employees is 5,200 Rupees and that of female employees is 4,200 Rupees. Find the percentage of male employees in the firm.

#### Standard Method
1.  Let total males be $M$ and females be $F$.
    $$5200M + 4200F = 5000(M + F)$$
    $$200M = 800F \implies \frac{M}{F} = \frac{4}{1}$$
2.  Male percentage = $\frac{M}{M + F} \times 100 = \frac{4}{5} \times 100 = 80\%$.

#### Alternate Fast Method (Alligation)
*   Males (5200) vs Females (4200), Mean (5000).
*   Ratio of Males to Females = $(5000 - 4200) : (5200 - 5000) = 800 : 200 = 4 : 1$.
*   Male percentage = $\frac{4}{4+1} \times 100 = 80\%$.

### Q4
Of the three numbers, the first is twice the second and is half of the third. If the average of these three numbers is 56, find the numbers.

#### Standard Method
1.  Let the second number be $x$. First number = $2x$. Third number = $4x$.
2.  Average = $\frac{2x + x + 4x}{3} = \frac{7x}{3} = 56 \implies 7x = 168 \implies x = 24$.
3.  Numbers are $48, 24, 96$.

#### Alternate Fast Method
*   Ratio of numbers is $2 : 1 : 4$. Sum of parts = 7.
*   Average of parts = $7 / 3$.
*   Scale factor = $56 / (7/3) = \frac{56 \times 3}{7} = 24$.
*   Numbers are $2 \times 24 = 48$; $1 \times 24 = 24$; $4 \times 24 = 96$.

### Q5
The average age of 8 men is increased by 2 years when two of them whose ages are 21 years and 23 years are replaced by two new men. Find the average age of the two new men.

#### Standard Method
1.  Let sum of ages of new men be $S$.
    $$S - (21 + 23) = 8 \times 2 \implies S - 44 = 16 \implies S = 60 \text{ years}$$
2.  Average age of two new men = $60 / 2 = 30$ years.

#### Alternate Fast Method
*   Average age increase for 8 men is 2 years, meaning total weight increase = 16 years.
*   Average increase per replaced man = $16 / 2 = 8$ years.
*   Average age of replaced men = $\frac{21 + 23}{2} = 22$ years.
*   Average age of new men = $22 + 8 = 30$ years.

### Q6
The average of 50 numbers is 38. If two numbers, namely 45 and 55, are discarded, what is the average of the remaining numbers?

#### Standard Method
1.  Sum of 50 numbers = $50 \times 38 = 1900$.
2.  Sum after discarding 45 and 55 = $1900 - 100 = 1800$.
3.  New average (with 48 numbers) = $\frac{1800}{48} = 37.5$.

#### Alternate Fast Method
*   The sum of the two discarded numbers is $45 + 55 = 100$.
*   Their average is $100 / 2 = 50$, which is 12 units higher than the group average of 38.
*   Total excess removed = $2 \times 12 = 24$ units.
*   This 24 units deficit is distributed across the remaining 48 numbers.
*   New Average = $38 - \frac{24}{48} = 38 - 0.5 = 37.5$.

### Q7
A motorist travels a distance of 10 km at a speed of 30 km/h, another 10 km at 40 km/h, and a final 10 km at 60 km/h. Find the average speed for the entire journey.

#### Standard Method
1.  Total distance = 30 km.
2.  Total time = $\frac{10}{30} + \frac{10}{40} + \frac{10}{60} = \frac{1}{3} + \frac{1}{4} + \frac{1}{6} = \frac{4 + 3 + 2}{12} = \frac{9}{12} = 0.75$ hours.
3.  $$\text{Average Speed} = \frac{30}{0.75} = 40 \text{ km/h}$$

#### Alternate Fast Method (Harmonic Mean)
*   Since segment distances are equal (10 km each), apply 3-variable Harmonic Mean:
    $$v_{\text{avg}} = \frac{3 \times 30 \times 40 \times 60}{30 \times 40 + 40 \times 60 + 60 \times 30} = \frac{216000}{1200 + 2400 + 1800} = \frac{216000}{5400} = 40 \text{ km/h}$$

### Q8
The average of 6 numbers is 30. If the average of the first 4 numbers is 25 and that of the last 3 numbers is 35, find the fourth number.

#### Standard Method
1.  Sum of all 6 numbers = 180.
2.  Sum of first 4 numbers = 100.
3.  Sum of last 3 numbers = 105.
4.  Total sum of segments (which overlaps at 4th number) = $100 + 105 = 205$.
5.  4th number = $205 - 180 = 25$.

#### Alternate Fast Method
*   Deviation of first 4 numbers from mean (30) = $4 \times (25 - 30) = -20$.
*   Deviation of last 3 numbers from mean (30) = $3 \times (35 - 30) = +15$.
*   Net deviation of segments = $-5$.
*   Since the 4th number is counted twice, its value must adjust for the deviation mismatch:
    $$\text{4th Number} = 30 + (-5) = 25$$.

### Q9
The average age of 30 students in a class is 15 years. If the teacher's age is included, the average age becomes 16 years. Find the teacher's age.

#### Standard Method
1.  Initial Sum = $30 \times 15 = 450$.
2.  New Sum (with teacher) = $31 \times 16 = 496$.
3.  Teacher's age = $496 - 450 = 46$ years.

#### Alternate Fast Method
*   Teacher's age = $\text{New Average} + N \times (\text{Average Increase}) = 16 + 30 \times 1 = 46$ years.

### Q10
The average score of a cricketer after 50 innings is 48. His highest score exceeds his lowest score by 120 runs. If these two innings are excluded, the average score of the remaining 48 innings is 47. Find his highest score.

#### Standard Method
1.  Total runs in 50 innings = $50 \times 48 = 2400$.
2.  Total runs in 48 innings (excluding highest and lowest) = $48 \times 47 = 2256$.
3.  Sum of Highest ($H$) and Lowest ($L$) = $2400 - 2256 = 144$ runs.
4.  We are given: $H - L = 120$.
5.  $$2H = 144 + 120 = 264 \implies H = 132 \text{ runs}$$.

#### Alternate Fast Method
*   Sum of H + L = $2 \times 48 + 48 \times 1 = 96 + 48 = 144$.
*   Since $H - L = 120$:
    *   $H = \frac{144 + 120}{2} = 132$ runs.
