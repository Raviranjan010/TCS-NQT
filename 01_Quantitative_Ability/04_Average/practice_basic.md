# Average — Practice Basic (15 Questions)

This practice set contains 15 basic questions testing arithmetic means, weighted averages, and consecutive number series.

*   **Suggested Time:** 15 minutes (60 seconds per question)
*   **Target Accuracy:** 100%

---

### Q1
Find the average of 12, 15, 18, 21, and 24.
*   **Answer:** 18
*   **Step-by-Step Calculation:**
    Since the numbers are in Arithmetic Progression, the average is the middle term: 18.

### Q2
Calculate the average of the first 50 natural numbers.
*   **Answer:** 25.5
*   **Step-by-Step Calculation:**
    $$\text{Average} = \frac{n + 1}{2} = \frac{50 + 1}{2} = 25.5$$

### Q3
What is the average of the first 10 even numbers?
*   **Answer:** 11
*   **Step-by-Step Calculation:**
    $$\text{Average} = n + 1 = 10 + 1 = 11$$

### Q4
Find the average of the first 15 odd numbers.
*   **Answer:** 15
*   **Step-by-Step Calculation:**
    $$\text{Average} = n = 15$$

### Q5
If the sum of 5 numbers is 135, what is their average?
*   **Answer:** 27
*   **Step-by-Step Calculation:**
    $$\text{Average} = \frac{\text{Sum}}{\text{Count}} = \frac{135}{5} = 27$$

### Q6
The average of 3 numbers is 15. If two of them are 12 and 18, find the third.
*   **Answer:** 15
*   **Step-by-Step Calculation:**
    $$\text{Sum} = 3 \times 15 = 45 \implies \text{Third} = 45 - (12 + 18) = 15$$

### Q7
If a car travels 2 hours at 40 km/h and 3 hours at 50 km/h, what is the average speed?
*   **Answer:** 46 km/h
*   **Step-by-Step Calculation:**
    $$\text{Average Speed} = \frac{\text{Total Distance}}{\text{Total Time}} = \frac{2(40) + 3(50)}{2 + 3} = \frac{230}{5} = 46$$

### Q8
Find the average of consecutive numbers from 10 to 20 (inclusive).
*   **Answer:** 15
*   **Step-by-Step Calculation:**
    $$\text{Average} = \frac{10 + 20}{2} = 15$$

### Q9
The average age of 10 boys is 12 years. If 1 boy of 12 years leaves, what is the new average?
*   **Answer:** 12 years
*   **Step-by-Step Calculation:**
    Removing an element equal to the average does not change the average.

### Q10
The average marks of 5 students is 60. If one student scored 100, what is the average of the other 4?
*   **Answer:** 50
*   **Step-by-Step Calculation:**
    $$\text{Sum} = 5 \times 60 = 300 \implies \text{Other 4 Sum} = 300 - 100 = 200 \implies \text{Average} = \frac{200}{4} = 50$$

### Q11
A motorist travels a distance at 20 km/h and returns at 30 km/h. Find the average speed.
*   **Answer:** 24 km/h
*   **Step-by-Step Calculation:**
    $$v_{\text{avg}} = \frac{2(20)(30)}{20 + 30} = \frac{1200}{50} = 24 \text{ km/h}$$

### Q12
What is the average of the squares of the first 3 natural numbers?
*   **Answer:** 4.67
*   **Step-by-Step Calculation:**
    $$\text{Average} = \frac{1^2 + 2^2 + 3^2}{3} = \frac{1 + 4 + 9}{3} = \frac{14}{3} \approx 4.67$$

### Q13
If $x, x+2, x+4$ have an average of 10, find $x$.
*   **Answer:** 8
*   **Step-by-Step Calculation:**
    Middle term $x+2 = 10 \implies x = 8$.

### Q14
The average weight of 8 men increases by 1 kg when a man of 60 kg is replaced. What is the weight of the new man?
*   **Answer:** 68 kg
*   **Step-by-Step Calculation:**
    $$\text{New Weight} = 60 + 8(1) = 68 \text{ kg}$$

### Q15
Find the average of 0, 10, and 20.
*   **Answer:** 10
*   **Step-by-Step Calculation:**
    $$\text{Average} = \frac{0 + 10 + 20}{3} = 10$$
