# Percentage — Revision Notes (Quick Summary)

This document is a one-page summary designed for last-minute revision before the TCS NQT exam.

---

## 1. Fundamentals & Multiplying Factors
Percentages are a way of expressing fractional proportions out of 100.
*   **Scale Factor (Increase):** To increase $X$ by $P\%$, multiply by $\left(1 + \frac{P}{100}\right)$.
*   **Scale Factor (Decrease):** To decrease $X$ by $P\%$, multiply by $\left(1 - \frac{P}{100}\right)$.
*   **Base Rule:** The percentage change is always calculated relative to the original/initial value.

## 2. Key Fraction to Percentage Map
Memorizing these conversions speeds up calculations during the exam:
*   $\frac{1}{2} = 50\%$
*   $\frac{1}{3} = 33.33\%$
*   $\frac{1}{4} = 25\%$
*   $\frac{1}{5} = 20\%$

## 3. Successive Changes Formula
For two successive changes of $A\%$ and $B\%$, the single equivalent percentage change is:
$$\text{Net Change} = A + B + \frac{AB}{100}$$
*   Use positive values for increases and negative values for decreases.

---

## 4. Product Constancy Rules
If $X \times Y = \text{Constant}$:
*   An increase of $X$ by $\frac{1}{n}$ requires a decrease of $Y$ by $\frac{1}{n+1}$.
*   A decrease of $X$ by $\frac{1}{n}$ requires an increase of $Y$ by $\frac{1}{n-1}$.
*   **Applications:** Price and consumption, speed and time (when distance is constant), length and width of a rectangle (when area is constant).
