# Percentage — Exam Shortcuts & Hacks

These shortcuts are designed to save time during the online proctored TCS NQT exam, where speed and accuracy are crucial.

---

## 1. The Scaling Factor (Multiplier) Method

Instead of calculating the percentage value and then adding or subtracting it, multiply by the equivalent decimal multiplier directly.

| Percentage Change | Decimal Multiplier (Scale Factor) | Example Calculation |
| :---: | :---: | :--- |
| **+10%** | $1.10$ | $500 \times 1.1 = 550$ |
| **+25%** | $1.25$ | $800 \times 1.25 = 1000$ |
| **+50%** | $1.50$ | $60 \times 1.5 = 90$ |
| **-10%** | $0.90$ | $400 \times 0.9 = 360$ |
| **-20%** | $0.80$ | $150 \times 0.8 = 120$ |
| **-25%** | $0.75$ | $200 \times 0.75 = 150$ |

## 2. Product Constancy Shortcuts

When the product of two variables is constant ($\text{Price} \times \text{Consumption} = \text{Expenditure}$), a percentage increase in one implies a direct fractional decrease in the other.

*   **Shortcut Rule:** If a value increases by $\frac{1}{x}$, it must decrease by $\frac{1}{x+1}$ to keep the product constant.
*   **Shortcut Rule:** If a value decreases by $\frac{1}{x}$, it must increase by $\frac{1}{x-1}$ to keep the product constant.

### Common Exam Patterns
*   **Price of sugar increases by 25% ($\frac{1}{4}$):**
    *   Consumption must decrease by: $\frac{1}{4+1} = \frac{1}{5} = 20\%$.
*   **Speed of a motorist decreases by 33.33% ($\frac{1}{3}$):**
    *   Time taken must increase by: $\frac{1}{3-1} = \frac{1}{2} = 50\%$.
*   **Length of a rectangle increases by 50% ($\frac{1}{2}$):**
    *   Width must decrease by: $\frac{1}{2+1} = \frac{1}{3} = 33.33\%$.
*   **Price of a commodity decreases by 20% ($\frac{1}{5}$):**
    *   Consumption must increase by: $\frac{1}{5-1} = \frac{1}{4} = 25\%$.

## 3. Successive Percentage Compounding Shortcuts

For standard compounding values, memorize the equivalent decimal rates:

*   **10% for 2 periods:** Net change = $21\%$ (multiplier $1.21$).
*   **10% for 3 periods:** Net change = $33.1\%$ (multiplier $1.331$).
*   **20% for 2 periods:** Net change = $44\%$ (multiplier $1.44$).
*   **20% for 3 periods:** Net change = $72.8\%$ (multiplier $1.728$).
