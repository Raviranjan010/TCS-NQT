### Q1
In an office, $40\%$ of the employees are men, and $60\%$ are women. $75\%$ of the men earn more than 50,000 Rupees per month, while $45\%$ of all employees earn more than 50,000 Rupees per month. What percentage of the women earn 50,000 Rupees or less per month?

#### Standard Method
1.  Let total employees be 100. Men = 40, Women = 60.
2.  Total earning > 50k = 45.
3.  Men earning > 50k = $75\% \text{ of } 40 = 30$.
4.  Women earning > 50k = $45 - 30 = 15$.
5.  Women earning $\le$ 50k = $60 - 15 = 45$.
6.  Percentage of women earning $\le$ 50k = $\frac{45}{60} \times 100 = 75\%$.

#### Alternate Fast Method (Alligation)
*   Let the fraction of men earning > 50k be $75\%$ and women be $W\%$.
*   By weighted average:
    $$0.40(75) + 0.60(W) = 45$$
    $$30 + 0.6W = 45 \rightarrow 0.6W = 15 \rightarrow W = 25\% \text{ earn > 50k}$$
*   Therefore, women earning $\le$ 50k = $100\% - 25\% = 75\%$.

### Q2
A salesman is appointed on a basic salary of 12,000 Rupees per month with a condition that he will get a commission of $6\%$ on the sales exceeding 15,000 Rupees. If his total earning in a month is 18,000 Rupees, what were his total sales?

#### Standard Method
1.  Total earning = Basic Salary + Commission.
2.  Commission = $18,000 - 12,000 = 6,000$ Rupees.
3.  Let sales exceeding 15,000 be $x$.
    $$0.06x = 6000 \rightarrow x = 100,000$$
4.  Total Sales = $15,000 + 100,000 = 115,000$ Rupees.

#### Alternate Fast Method (Scale Ratio)
*   Since commission rate is $6\%$ ($\frac{3}{50}$):
*   Excess Sales = $\text{Commission} \times \frac{50}{3} = 6000 \times \frac{50}{3} = 100,000$.
*   Total Sales = $100,000 + 15,000 = 115,000$.

### Q3
The price of a commodity increases by $30\%$, causing its sales to decrease by $15\%$. What is the net percentage change in revenue?

#### Standard Method
1.  Let initial Price = 10, Sales = 10. Revenue = 100.
2.  New Price = 13, New Sales = 8.5.
3.  New Revenue = $13 \times 8.5 = 110.5$.
4.  Net Change = $10.5\%$.

#### Alternate Fast Method (Successive Formula)
*   Apply $A + B + \frac{AB}{100}$ directly:
    $$30 - 15 - \frac{450}{100} = 15 - 4.5 = 10.5\% \text{ increase}$$

### Q4
In a library, $20\%$ of the books are in Hindi, $50\%$ of the remaining are in English, and $30\%$ of the remaining are in French. If the remaining 6,300 books are in regional languages, what is the total number of books in the library?

#### Standard Method
1.  Let total books be $X$.
2.  Remaining after Hindi = $0.80X$.
3.  Remaining after English = $0.50 \times 0.80X = 0.40X$.
4.  Remaining after French = $0.70 \times 0.40X = 0.28X$.
5.  $$0.28X = 6300 \rightarrow X = \frac{6300}{0.28} = 22,500$$

#### Alternate Fast Method (Fraction Product)
*   Fraction remaining = $\frac{4}{5} \times \frac{1}{2} \times \frac{7}{10} = \frac{14}{50} = \frac{7}{25}$.
*   Total Books = $6300 \times \frac{25}{7} = 900 \times 25 = 22,500$.

### Q5
If A's salary is $20\%$ more than B's, and B's salary is $25\%$ more than C's, by how much percent is A's salary more than C's?

#### Standard Method
1.  Let C's salary = 100.
2.  B's salary = 125.
3.  A's salary = $125 \times 1.20 = 150$.
4.  Percentage difference = $50\%$.

#### Alternate Fast Method (Successive Growth)
*   Apply successive change of $25\%$ and $20\%$ directly:
    $$25 + 20 + \frac{25 \times 20}{100} = 45 + 5 = 50\%$$

### Q6
Due to a $25\%$ increase in the price of apples, a customer can buy 4 fewer apples for 240 Rupees. What is the new price of an apple?

#### Standard Method
1.  Let original price be $P$. Original quantity = $240/P$.
2.  New price = $1.25P$. New quantity = $240 / 1.25P = 192 / P$.
3.  Difference:
    $$\frac{240}{P} - \frac{192}{P} = 4 \rightarrow \frac{48}{P} = 4 \rightarrow P = 12 \text{ Rupees/apple}$$
4.  New Price = $12 \times 1.25 = 15$ Rupees/apple.

#### Alternate Fast Method (Direct Value Allocation)
*   The $25\%$ increase on 240 Rupees represents a value of $240 \times 0.25 = 60$ Rupees.
*   This 60 Rupees is the reason the buyer gets 4 fewer apples.
*   Therefore, the new price of 4 apples must be 60 Rupees.
*   New Price per apple = $60 / 4 = 15$ Rupees.

### Q7
In an exam, $70\%$ of the candidates passed in Quantitative Ability, $80\%$ passed in Reasoning, and $10\%$ failed in both. If 144 candidates passed in both, find the total number of candidates.

#### Standard Method
1.  Failed in QA = $30\%$, Failed in Reasoning = $20\%$. Failed in both = $10\%$.
2.  Total failed in at least one = $30 + 20 - 10 = 40\%$.
3.  Total passed in both = $100 - 40 = 60\%$.
4.  $$0.60X = 144 \rightarrow X = 240$$

#### Alternate Fast Method (Passed Venn Diagram)
*   Passed QA = $70\%$, Passed Reasoning = $80\%$.
*   Since $10\%$ failed in both, $90\%$ passed in at least one.
*   Passed in both = $\text{QA} + \text{Reasoning} - \text{At least one} = 70 + 80 - 90 = 60\%$.
*   Total candidates = $144 / 0.60 = 240$.

### Q8
An alloy of copper and zinc contains $40\%$ copper. Another alloy contains $60\%$ copper. If they are melted in the ratio $2:3$, what is the percentage of copper in the resulting alloy?

#### Standard Method
1.  Let total weight be 5 kg ($2$ kg of Alloy 1 and $3$ kg of Alloy 2).
2.  Copper in Alloy 1 = $2 \times 0.40 = 0.8$ kg.
3.  Copper in Alloy 2 = $3 \times 0.60 = 1.8$ kg.
4.  Total copper = $0.8 + 1.8 = 2.6$ kg.
5.  Percentage = $\frac{2.6}{5} \times 100 = 52\%$.

#### Alternate Fast Method (Weighted Average)
*   $$\text{Copper \%} = \frac{2(40) + 3(60)}{2 + 3} = \frac{80 + 180}{5} = \frac{260}{5} = 52\%$$

### Q9
If the radius of a sphere is increased by $10\%$, what is the percentage increase in its volume?

#### Standard Method
1.  Volume is proportional to $r^3$.
2.  New Volume = $V \times (1.1)^3 = 1.331V$.
3.  Increase = $33.1\%$.

#### Alternate Fast Method (Successive Compound)
*   Apply $10\%$ successively three times:
    *   Step 1 (2 times): $10 + 10 + 1 = 21\%$.
    *   Step 2 (3 times): $21 + 10 + 2.1 = 33.1\%$.

### Q10
A vendor sells $30\%$ of his fruits and throws away $10\%$ of the remaining. Next day he sells $60\%$ of the balance. What percentage of the fruits are left with him?

#### Standard Method
1.  Let total fruits be 100.
2.  Sells 30 $\rightarrow$ remaining = 70.
3.  Throws away $10\%$ of remaining $\rightarrow 70 \times 0.90 = 63$ remaining.
4.  Sells $60\%$ of balance $\rightarrow 63 \times (1 - 0.60) = 63 \times 0.40 = 25.2$ remaining.
5.  Percentage left = $25.2\%$.

#### Alternate Fast Method (Multiplier Chain)
*   $$\text{Final fraction} = 0.70 \times 0.90 \times 0.40 = 0.252 \rightarrow 25.2\%$$
