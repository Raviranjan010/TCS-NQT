### Q1
A candidate scores $35\%$ marks and fails by 40 marks, while another candidate scores $60\%$ marks and gets 35 marks more than the minimum pass marks. Find the pass percentage.

#### Solution
1.  Let the maximum marks be $M$.
2.  Pass marks equation from candidate 1: $0.35M + 40$.
3.  Pass marks equation from candidate 2: $0.60M - 35$.
4.  Equate them:
    $$0.60M - 35 = 0.35M + 40$$
    $$0.25M = 75 \rightarrow M = 300$$
5.  Pass Marks = $0.35(300) + 40 = 105 + 40 = 145$.
6.  Pass Percentage = $\frac{145}{300} \times 100 \approx 48.33\%$.

### Q2
Due to a reduction of $20\%$ in the price of sugar, a man can buy 5 kg more sugar for 320 Rupees. What is the original price of sugar per kg?

#### Solution
1.  Let the original price of sugar be $P$ Rupees/kg.
2.  Original quantity purchased = $\frac{320}{P}$.
3.  Reduced price = $0.80P$.
4.  New quantity purchased = $\frac{320}{0.80P}$.
5.  Set up the difference:
    $$\frac{320}{0.80P} - \frac{320}{P} = 5$$
    $$\frac{400}{P} - \frac{320}{P} = 5 \rightarrow \frac{80}{P} = 5 \rightarrow P = 16 \text{ Rupees/kg}$$

### Q3
The population of a town was 10,000. In a year, the male population increased by $8\%$ and the female population by $10\%$, and the total population became 10,900. Find the number of males originally.

#### Solution
1.  Let the number of males be $M$, so females = $10,000 - M$.
2.  Total population became 10,900, representing a net increase of 900.
3.  Equation for the increase:
    $$0.08M + 0.10(10,000 - M) = 900$$
    $$0.08M + 1000 - 0.10M = 900$$
    $$-0.02M = -100 \rightarrow M = 5000$$

### Q4
In an examination, $35\%$ of the candidates failed in English, $40\%$ failed in Hindi, and $15\%$ failed in both. What percentage of candidates passed in both subjects?

#### Solution
1.  Using set theory: $N(E \cup H) = N(E) + N(H) - N(E \cap H)$.
2.  Total failed in at least one subject = $35\% + 40\% - 15\% = 60\%$.
3.  Percentage who passed in both subjects = $100\% - 60\% = 40\%$.

### Q5
A container has 80 liters of milk. 8 liters of milk is replaced with water. This process is repeated one more time. What is the final volume of milk in the container?

#### Solution
1.  Formula: $\text{Final Milk} = \text{Initial Milk} \times \left(1 - \frac{Y}{X}\right)^n$.
2.  Here, $X = 80$, $Y = 8$, $n = 2$.
3.  $$\text{Final Volume} = 80 \times \left(1 - \frac{8}{80}\right)^2 = 80 \times (0.9)^2 = 80 \times 0.81 = 64.8 \text{ liters}$$

### Q6
The salary of a clerk is first increased by $20\%$ and then decreased by $10\%$. What is the net change in his salary?

#### Solution
1.  Successive change formula: $A + B + \frac{AB}{100}$.
2.  Here, $A = +20$, $B = -10$.
3.  $$\text{Net Change} = 20 - 10 + \frac{20 \times (-10)}{100} = 10 - 2 = 8\% \text{ increase}$$

### Q7
A man spends $75\%$ of his income. If his income increases by $20\%$ and expenditure by $10\%$, what is the percentage increase in his savings?

#### Solution
1.  Let initial Income = 100.
2.  Expenditure = 75, Savings = 25.
3.  New Income = 120 ($20\%$ increase).
4.  New Expenditure = $75 \times 1.10 = 82.5$ ($10\%$ increase).
5.  New Savings = $120 - 82.5 = 37.5$.
6.  Savings Increase = $\frac{37.5 - 25}{25} \times 100 = \frac{12.5}{25} \times 100 = 50\%$.

### Q8
If the numerator of a fraction is increased by $200\%$ and the denominator is increased by $350\%$, the resultant fraction is $\frac{5}{12}$. What is the original fraction?

#### Solution
1.  Let the original fraction be $\frac{x}{y}$.
2.  Numerator increases by $200\% \rightarrow 300\% \text{ of } x$.
3.  Denominator increases by $350\% \rightarrow 450\% \text{ of } y$.
4.  $$\frac{300\% \cdot x}{450\% \cdot y} = \frac{5}{12} \implies \frac{2x}{3y} = \frac{5}{12} \implies \frac{x}{y} = \frac{5 \times 3}{12 \times 2} = \frac{5}{8}$$

### Q9
Two numbers are respectively $20\%$ and $50\%$ more than a third number. What percentage is the first number of the second number?

#### Solution
1.  Let the third number be 100.
2.  First number = 120, Second number = 150.
3.  $$\text{Percentage} = \frac{120}{150} \times 100 = \frac{4}{5} \times 100 = 80\%$$

### Q10
Out of 4000 citizens, $60\%$ vote. If candidate A receives $55\%$ of the votes cast, how many votes did candidate A receive?

#### Solution
1.  Total votes cast = $4000 \times 0.60 = 2400$ votes.
2.  Votes received by A = $2400 \times 0.55 = 1320$ votes.

### Q12
In a mixture of 40 liters of acid and water, acid is $10\%$. How much water should be added to make the acid content $20\%$ in the new mixture?

#### Solution
1.  Wait, the acid is $10\%$ in 40 liters $\rightarrow 4$ liters of acid.
2.  To make the acid content $20\%$, wait, the concentration of acid would increase! This means we need to evaporate water or add acid, not add water.
3.  Let's correct the question: "How much acid should be added to make the acid content $20\%$?"
4.  Let the acid added be $x$ liters.
5.  $$\frac{4 + x}{40 + x} = \frac{20}{100} = \frac{1}{5}$$
6.  $$5(4 + x) = 40 + x \rightarrow 20 + 5x = 40 + x \rightarrow 4x = 20 \rightarrow x = 5 \text{ liters}$$

### Q13
A machine's value depreciates by $10\%$ every year. If its present value is 162,000 Rupees, what was its value 2 years ago?

#### Solution
1.  Let the value 2 years ago be $V$.
2.  $$V \times (0.9)^2 = 162,000$$
3.  $$V \times 0.81 = 162,000 \rightarrow V = \frac{162,000}{0.81} = 200,000 \text{ Rupees}$$

### Q14
What is the net change if a price is first marked up by $30\%$ and then discounted by $30\%$?

#### Solution
1.  Using successive change formula: $30 - 30 - \frac{900}{100} = -9\%$.
2.  Net change is a **9% decrease**.

### Q15
If $x\%$ of $y$ is 100, and $y\%$ of $z$ is 200, find the relation between $x$ and $z$.

#### Solution
1.  $$\frac{x}{100} \cdot y = 100 \implies xy = 10,000$$
2.  $$\frac{y}{100} \cdot z = 200 \implies yz = 20,000$$
3.  Divide the second equation by the first:
    $$\frac{yz}{xy} = \frac{20,000}{10,000} \implies \frac{z}{x} = 2 \implies z = 2x$$
