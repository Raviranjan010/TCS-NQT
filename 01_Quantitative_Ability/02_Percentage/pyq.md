### PYQ1 — Price-Consumption Balance
A dealer increases the price of tea by $15\%$. By what percentage must the consumption be reduced so that expenditure increases by only $5\%$?
*   **Difficulty:** Medium | **Expected Time:** 90s | **TCS Frequency:** High
*   **Standard Solution:**
    *   Let initial Price = 100, Consumption = 100. Expenditure = 10,000.
    *   New Price = 115. New Expenditure = 10,500.
    *   New Consumption = $\frac{10,500}{115} \approx 91.3\%$.
    *   Decrease = $100 - 91.3 = 8.7\%$.
*   **Faster Method:**
    *   $\text{Ratio} = \frac{105}{115} = \frac{21}{23} \rightarrow \text{Decrease} = \frac{2}{23} \approx 8.7\%$.

### PYQ2 — Successive Population Growth
The population of a city is $176,400$. If it increases at the rate of $5\%$ per annum, what will be its population after 2 years?
*   **Difficulty:** Easy | **Expected Time:** 60s | **TCS Frequency:** High
*   **Standard Solution:**
    *   $176400 \times (1.05)^2 = 176400 \times 1.1025 = 194,481$.
*   **Faster Method:**
    *   $176400 \times \frac{21}{20} \times \frac{21}{20} = 441 \times 441 = 194,481$.

### PYQ3 — Election Valid Vote Share
In a college election, $20\%$ of the votes were invalid. Candidate A got $60\%$ of the valid votes. If the total votes polled were 8,000, how many votes did the other candidate get?
*   **Difficulty:** Easy | **Expected Time:** 45s | **TCS Frequency:** High
*   **Standard Solution:**
    *   Valid votes = $8000 \times 0.80 = 6400$.
    *   Other candidate got $40\%$ of valid votes = $6400 \times 0.40 = 2560$.
*   **Faster Method:**
    *   $8000 \times 0.8 \times 0.4 = 8000 \times 0.32 = 2560$.

### PYQ4 — Salary Deductions
A person saves $20\%$ of his income. If his income increases by $20\%$ and he still saves the same amount (in Rupees), what is the percentage increase in his expenditure?
*   **Difficulty:** Medium | **Expected Time:** 90s | **TCS Frequency:** High
*   **Standard Solution:**
    *   Initial: Income = 100, Savings = 20, Expenditure = 80.
    *   New: Income = 120, Savings = 20, Expenditure = 100.
    *   Increase in Expenditure = $\frac{100 - 80}{80} \times 100 = 25\%$.
*   **Faster Method:**
    *   The entire increase in income of 20 Rupees goes to expenditure because savings are constant.
    *   Expenditure increase = $20 / 80 = 25\%$.

### PYQ5 — Successive Compounding
A property value increases by $10\%$ in the first year, $20\%$ in the second year, and $30\%$ in the third year. What is the overall percentage increase in the property value over 3 years?
*   **Difficulty:** Hard | **Expected Time:** 120s | **TCS Frequency:** High
*   **Standard Solution:**
    *   Multiplier = $1.10 \times 1.20 \times 1.30 = 1.32 \times 1.30 = 1.716$.
    *   Increase = $71.6\%$.
*   **Faster Method:**
    *   Step 1: $10 + 20 + 2 = 32\%$.
    *   Step 2: $32 + 30 + 9.6 = 71.6\%$.

### PYQ6 — Student Pass Statistics
In an exam, $35\%$ of students failed in English and $45\%$ failed in Math. If $20\%$ failed in both, find the percentage of students who passed in both.
*   **Difficulty:** Easy | **Expected Time:** 45s | **TCS Frequency:** High
*   **Standard Solution:**
    *   Failed in at least one = $35 + 45 - 20 = 60\%$.
    *   Passed in both = $100 - 60 = 40\%$.
*   **Faster Method:**
    *   Venn Diagram: $100 - (15 + 20 + 25) = 40\%$.

### PYQ7 — Basic Salary Markup
B's income is $20\%$ less than A's. How much percent is A's income more than B's?
*   **Difficulty:** Easy | **Expected Time:** 30s | **TCS Frequency:** High
*   **Standard Solution:**
    *   Let A = 100, B = 80. Difference = 20.
    *   Percentage = $\frac{20}{80} \times 100 = 25\%$.
*   **Faster Method:**
    *   $\frac{1}{5}$ decrease implies $\frac{1}{5-1} = \frac{1}{4} = 25\%$ increase.

### PYQ8 — Two Numbers Comparison
Two numbers are respectively $30\%$ and $40\%$ less than a third number. What percentage is the second number of the first?
*   **Difficulty:** Medium | **Expected Time:** 60s | **TCS Frequency:** Medium
*   **Standard Solution:**
    *   Third = 100. First = 70. Second = 60.
    *   Percentage = $\frac{60}{70} \times 100 \approx 85.71\%$.
*   **Faster Method:**
    *   Ratio = $6 / 7 = 85.71\%$.

### PYQ9 — Machine Value Depreciation
The value of a machine depreciated by $15\%$ in the first year and $10\%$ in the second year. If the current value is 76,500 Rupees, what was the original value?
*   **Difficulty:** Medium | **Expected Time:** 90s | **TCS Frequency:** High
*   **Standard Solution:**
    *   $V \times 0.85 \times 0.90 = 76500 \implies V \times 0.765 = 76500 \implies V = 100,000$ Rupees.
*   **Faster Method:**
    *   $0.765V = 76500 \rightarrow V = 100,000$.

### PYQ10 — Monthly Expenditure Allocation
A man spends $40\%$ of his salary on food, $20\%$ on house rent, $10\%$ on entertainment, and $10\%$ on conveyance. If his savings at the end of the month are 1,500 Rupees, find his monthly salary.
*   **Difficulty:** Easy | **Expected Time:** 45s | **TCS Frequency:** High
*   **Standard Solution:**
    *   Total spent = $40 + 20 + 10 + 10 = 80\%$.
    *   Savings = $20\%$.
    *   $0.20S = 1500 \implies S = 7500$ Rupees.
*   **Faster Method:**
    *   $1/5$ of salary = 1500 $\rightarrow$ Salary = 7500.

### PYQ11 — Number System Multipliers
If $10\%$ of $x$ is the same as $20\%$ of $y$, then $x:y$ is equal to:
*   **Difficulty:** Easy | **Expected Time:** 15s | **TCS Frequency:** Low
*   **Standard Solution:**
    *   $0.10x = 0.20y \implies x/y = 2/1 \implies 2:1$.
*   **Faster Method:**
    *   Inspection shows $x$ must be double of $y$ to balance the coefficients $\rightarrow 2:1$.

### PYQ12 — Exam Candidates Share
In an examination, $80\%$ of boys and $70\%$ of girls cleared the cut-off. If the total candidates were 1500 and $75\%$ cleared the cut-off, find the number of boys.
*   **Difficulty:** Medium | **Expected Time:** 90s | **TCS Frequency:** High
*   **Standard Solution:**
    *   Let boys be $B$.
    *   $0.80B + 0.70(1500 - B) = 0.75 \times 1500 \implies 0.10B + 1050 = 1125 \implies 0.10B = 75 \implies B = 750$.
*   **Faster Method (Alligation):**
    *   Boys (80%) vs Girls (70%), Mean (75%).
    *   Ratio of boys to girls is $(75 - 70) : (80 - 75) = 5:5 = 1:1$.
    *   Boys = $1500 / 2 = 750$.

### PYQ13 — Tax Rates Successive
A city's property tax rate increases by $8\%$, and the property valuation increases by $5\%$. What is the net percentage increase in property tax collected?
*   **Difficulty:** Easy | **Expected Time:** 45s | **TCS Frequency:** Medium
*   **Standard Solution:**
    *   $1.08 \times 1.05 = 1.134 \rightarrow 13.4\%$ increase.
*   **Faster Method:**
    *   $8 + 5 + 0.4 = 13.4\%$.

### PYQ14 — Successive Marked Price
A shopkeeper increases the price of an item by $40\%$ and then offers a discount of $40\%$. Find the net profit or loss percentage.
*   **Difficulty:** Easy | **Expected Time:** 20s | **TCS Frequency:** High
*   **Standard Solution:**
    *   $40 - 40 - \frac{1600}{100} = -16\%$.
*   **Faster Method:**
    *   A successive increase and decrease of $x\%$ always results in a loss of $\frac{x^2}{100}\% = \frac{1600}{100} = 16\%$ loss.

### PYQ15 — Student Mark Bounds
A candidate scored $30\%$ and failed by 5 marks. Another candidate scored $40\%$ and got 10 marks more than the pass marks. What were the passing marks?
*   **Difficulty:** Easy | **Expected Time:** 45s | **TCS Frequency:** High
*   **Standard Solution:**
    *   $0.10M = 15 \implies M = 150$.
    *   Pass marks = $0.30(150) + 5 = 50$.
*   **Faster Method:**
    *   Difference of $10\%$ corresponds to 15 marks. Therefore, $30\%$ corresponds to 45 marks.
    *   Pass marks = $45 + 5 = 50$.
