# Verbal Ability - Cheatsheet

Quick reference sheet of rules and guidelines for Verbal Ability.

---

## ⚡ Verbal Cheatsheet

### 1. Subject-Verb Agreement Gotchas
*   Singular nouns joined by `and` take a plural verb.
*   Nouns joined by `or`/`nor` take a verb matching the closest subject.
*   Collective nouns (family, committee, jury) take a singular verb when acting as a single unit, and a plural verb when members act individually.

### 2. Tenses
*   **Present Perfect**: Action started in the past and continues to the present. Use `since` (point in time) or `for` (duration).
*   **Past Perfect**: Used for the earlier of two past actions.
    *   *Example*: *"The train **had left** (past perfect) before I **reached** (simple past) the station."*

### 3. Conditional Sentences
*   **Type 1**: If + Simple Present $\rightarrow$ Will + Base Verb.
*   **Type 2**: If + Simple Past $\rightarrow$ Would + Base Verb.
*   **Type 3**: If + Past Perfect $\rightarrow$ Would Have + Past Participle.
    *   *Example*: *"If I **had studied**, I **would have passed**."*

### 4. Idioms & Phrasal Verbs
*   "Break down": Stop functioning.
*   "Call off": Cancel.
*   "Put up with": Tolerate.
