## 1. NUM-01: Successive Discounts & Percentage Change

*   **Problem Statement**: An item is marked up by 40% and then sold after two successive discounts of 10% and 20%. Find the net profit or loss percentage.
*   **Derivation**:
    1.  Let cost price $\text{CP} = 100$.
    2.  Marked Price $\text{MP} = 100 \times (1 + 0.40) = 140$.
    3.  Successive discounts are 10% and 20%.
        *   Net selling price $\text{SP} = 140 \times (1 - 0.10) \times (1 - 0.20)$
        *   $\text{SP} = 140 \times 0.90 \times 0.80 = 140 \times 0.72 = 100.80$.
    4.  Net profit percentage:
        $$\text{Profit} = \text{SP} - \text{CP} = 100.80 - 100 = 0.80\%$$
*   **Shortcut**: Net discount of $D_1\%$ and $D_2\% = D_1 + D_2 - \frac{D_1 \cdot D_2}{100}$.
    *   For 10% and 20%: Net discount $= 10 + 20 - \frac{10 \times 20}{100} = 28\%$.
    *   $\text{SP} = 140 \times (1 - 0.28) = 140 \times 0.72 = 100.8$. Profit $= 0.8\%$.

## 2. NUM-02: SI vs CI Difference

*   **Problem Statement**: The difference between Simple Interest (SI) and Compound Interest (CI) on a sum of money for 2 years at 10% per annum is Rs. 150. Find the principal sum.
*   **Derivation**:
    *   $\text{SI for 2 years} = \frac{P \cdot R \cdot 2}{100} = 0.20 \cdot P$.
    *   $\text{CI for 2 years} = P \left(1 + \frac{R}{100}\right)^2 - P = P(1.10)^2 - P = 0.21 \cdot P$.
    *   $\text{Difference} = \text{CI} - \text{SI} = 0.21 \cdot P - 0.20 \cdot P = 0.01 \cdot P$.
    *   Given Difference = 150:
        $$0.01 \cdot P = 150 \implies P = \text{Rs. } 15,000$$
*   **Shortcut**: For 2 years, difference $D = P \left(\frac{R}{100}\right)^2$.
    *   $150 = P \left(\frac{10}{100}\right)^2 \implies 150 = P \times \frac{1}{100} \implies P = 150 \times 100 = 15,000$.

## 3. NUM-03: Time & Work (Work Equivalence)

*   **Problem Statement**: 12 men can complete a work in 8 days. 16 women can complete the same work in 12 days. In how many days will 8 men and 8 women complete the work together?
*   **Derivation**:
    1.  Work is equal: $12 \cdot M \cdot 8 = 16 \cdot W \cdot 12 \implies 96 \cdot M = 192 \cdot W$.
    2.  Work efficiency relationship: $1 \cdot M = 2 \cdot W$.
    3.  Total work in terms of women efficiency:
        $$\text{Work} = 16 \cdot W \cdot 12 = 192 \cdot W \text{ units}$$
    4.  Efficiency of 8 men and 8 women together:
        $$\text{Eff} = 8 \cdot M + 8 \cdot W = 8 \cdot (2 \cdot W) + 8 \cdot W = 24 \cdot W \text{ units/day}$$
    5.  Time taken:
        $$\text{Time} = \frac{\text{Total Work}}{\text{Total Eff}} = \frac{192 \cdot W}{24 \cdot W} = 8 \text{ days}$$

## 4. NUM-04: Average Speed & Relative Speed

*   **Problem Statement**: A train travels from A to B at 60 km/h and returns from B to A at 40 km/h. Find the average speed of the train for the entire journey.
*   **Derivation**:
    *   Let distance between A and B be $d$.
    *   Time to go $= \frac{d}{60}$, Time to return $= \frac{d}{40}$.
    *   $\text{Average Speed} = \frac{\text{Total Distance}}{\text{Total Time}} = \frac{2d}{\frac{d}{60} + \frac{d}{40}} = \frac{2d}{\frac{5d}{120}} = \frac{2 \times 120}{5} = 48 \text{ km/h}$.
*   **Shortcut**: If distance is equal, Average Speed $= \frac{2 \cdot x \cdot y}{x + y}$.
    *   $\text{Avg Speed} = \frac{2 \times 60 \times 40}{60 + 40} = \frac{4800}{100} = 48 \text{ km/h}$.
