## 🚀 1. Topics to Master First (High Weight + Low/Medium Difficulty)
These topics provide the highest mark returns for the least amount of study time. Focus heavily on these if you are short on time:

*   **Verbal Sentence Completion**: 20 questions in the exam. Mostly tests prepositions, conjunctions, and simple vocabulary. High ROI.
*   **Percentages**: The mathematical foundation for all commercial arithmetic. Master fraction conversions and base multipliers.
*   **Seating Arrangements**: 3 questions. Once you understand linear/circular placement constraints, you can solve these with 100% accuracy.
*   **Email Writing**: 1 question worth significant marks. Follow the exact word outline sequence and write in formal English.
*   **Data Interpretation (DI)**: 3 questions. Usually involves simple calculations (averages, percentage increases) based on tables, bar charts, or pie diagrams.

---

## 🟡 2. Secondary Focus Topics (Medium Weight / Medium Difficulty)
Study these topics once you have secured the critical foundations:

*   **Averages & Mixtures**: Standard questions on average age, average marks, and mixtures alligation scaling.
*   **Profit & Loss**: Focus on markup, cost/selling price ratios, and successive discounts.
*   **Array & String Coding**: Practice basic operations (rotation, search, unique counting, string reversal, backtracking permutations).
*   **Time, Speed & Distance**: Master relative speed (trains meeting, boats going upstream/downstream, and average speed).
*   **Time & Work**: Focus on work equivalence, individual efficiencies, and pipes/cisterns.

---

## 🟢 3. Topics to Skip If Time is Extremely Limited
These topics have a high difficulty curve and low question frequency. Skip these if you have less than 48 hours to prepare:

*   **Functions & Logarithms**: Requires extensive algebraic base knowledge and formulas. Average of 0-1 question.
*   **Mensuration & Geometry**: High formula count (areas, volumes, angles). Average of 1 question.
*   **Complex Probability**: Requires deep permutations logic. Focus only on simple coins, cards, and dice problems instead.
*   **Advanced Number System**: (e.g. finding remainder theorems or base system conversions). Skip if you don't know the shortcut patterns.
