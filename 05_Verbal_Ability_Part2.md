**Prep Focus: Passage Recall (90s write) & Email Writing (9 minutes)**

This guide provides strategies, structured templates, and practice exercises with model responses for two of the most critical writing tasks in the Verbal Ability section.

## 🧠 Passage Recall Master Strategy

In the Passage Recall section:
1. A short passage (4-5 sentences, ~80 words) is displayed on screen for **exactly 30 seconds**.
2. The passage disappears.
3. You have **90 seconds** to write a summary/recall of the text.

### ⚡ Active Reading Tactics (The 30-Second Window)
- **Do not read aloud**: Read silently to maximize speed.
- **Identify the Core Triad**:
  * **Who/What**: The main subject (e.g., *Artificial Intelligence*, *Quantum Computing*, *Great Barrier Reef*).
  * **When/Where/How Much**: Numbers, percentages, dates (e.g., *2025*, *70%*, *five countries*).
  * **Why/Result**: The cause-and-effect relation (e.g., *rising temperatures causing coral bleaching*).
- **Keyword Capture**: Mentally repeat the 4-5 core keywords.

### ✍️ Re-writing Rules (The 90-Second Window)
- **Do NOT copy-paste from memory**: TCS uses plagiarism detection. If your text is an exact match of the prompt, it gets flagged.
- **Use Synonyms & Paraphrase**: Rearrange the clause structure (e.g., change active voice to passive voice, or vice versa).
- **Keep it Simple**: Write 2-3 grammatically perfect sentences. A short, grammatically correct paragraph is infinitely better than a long, error-filled one.

### 📝 Passage Recall Practice Sets

#### Practice Set 1
* **Source Passage**:
  > "The global shift towards electric vehicles (EVs) has accelerated dramatically, with sales rising by 60% in 2025. This surge is driven by government subsidies and growing environmental awareness. However, the limited availability of public charging stations remains a major bottleneck in rural areas."
* **Model Recall (Score: 10/10)**:
  > In 2025, electric vehicle sales grew by sixty percent globally, spurred by environmental consciousness and state incentives. Nonetheless, rural regions face a significant obstacle due to the scarce distribution of public charging infrastructure.

## 📧 Email Writing Rules & Strategy

In this section:
1. You have **9 minutes** to write a professional email.
2. You must write **at least 100 words**.
3. You are given a list of outline words/phrases. **You MUST use all of them in the exact order provided**.

### ⚠️ Critical Constraints
* **No Outline Word Swapping**: Do not change the form of the words (e.g., if the outline says "meeting", do not write "meetings" or "met").
* **Keep the Order**: You cannot rearrange the words. The sequence must be 1st word $\to$ 2nd word $\to \dots \to$ Last word.
* **Grammar & Structure**: Include a clear Subject Line, Salutation, Body paragraphs (introducing the context, expanding, and concluding), and a formal Sign-off.

### 📝 Email Writing Practice Sets

#### Practice Set 1
* **Context**: Write an email to your project manager requesting a deadline extension.
* **Outline Words**: `project status` $\to$ `unexpected issues` $\to$ `additional time` $\to$ `resource constraints` $\to$ `new deadline` $\to$ `appreciate understanding`
* **Model Email (122 words)**:
  > **Subject**: Request for Extension of Project Deadline
  >
  > Dear Mr. Sharma,
  >
  > I am writing to provide an update on our current **project status**. During the final integration phase, we encountered several **unexpected issues** with the database connections. 
  >
  > Consequently, our development team requires **additional time** to resolve these bugs. Due to sudden **resource constraints**, as two developers are on medical leave, we are unable to meet the original completion date. We propose a **new deadline** of next Friday, July 3rd, which will allow us to deliver a stable product.
  >
  > I would highly **appreciate understanding** and support regarding this delay. Please let me know if we can schedule a quick call to discuss the revised timeline.
  >
  > Sincerely,  
  > Ravi Ranjan  
  > Software Engineer

#### Practice Set 2
* **Context**: Write an email to a client explaining a system downtime.
* **Outline Words**: `scheduled maintenance` $\to$ `unforeseen technical glitch` $\to$ `temporary disruption` $\to$ `deeply regret` $\to$ `fully restored` $\to$ `support team`
* **Model Email (118 words)**:
  > **Subject**: System Downtime Resolution Update
  >
  > Dear Client,
  >
  > We recently conducted a **scheduled maintenance** cycle on our servers to improve database response times. Unfortunately, we encountered an **unforeseen technical glitch** during the reboot process.
  >
  > This issue caused a **temporary disruption** in all cloud applications for approximately forty minutes. We **deeply regret** any inconvenience this unexpected outage may have caused your daily business operations. 
  >
  > Please be assured that all systems are now **fully restored** and running smoothly. Our **support team** is monitoring the platform closely to prevent similar incidents in the future.
  >
  > Thank you for your patience.
  >
  > Best regards,  
  > Ravi Ranjan  
  > IT Operations Manager
