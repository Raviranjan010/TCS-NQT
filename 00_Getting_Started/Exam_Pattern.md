# TCS NQT Exam Pattern

> ⚠️ **IMPORTANT — READ FIRST**
> TCS NQT's exact structure (section names, number of questions, timing, which modules are mandatory vs. optional, and the Ninja/Digital/Prime band cutoffs) **has changed across recruitment cycles**. Conflicting information is widely circulated online, including on coaching sites that copy each other without cross-checking. The structure below reflects the **most commonly reported pattern** as of recent cycles (2023–2025), but **you must verify every number against your official TCS iON candidate dashboard and the call letter sent to your registered email before exam day**. Do not trust any third-party source — including this document — over official TCS communications.

---

## Overview of the TCS NQT Selection Bands

TCS NQT (National Qualifier Test) is used as a hiring filter for multiple TCS roles. Your score determines which band you qualify for:

| Band | Role Target | What It Requires |
|------|------------|-----------------|
| **Ninja** | TCS Ninja (entry-level) | Clearing the Foundation section |
| **Digital** | TCS Digital (higher package) | Higher score in Foundation + Advanced sections |
| **Prime** [VERIFY] | TCS Prime / specialized roles | High accuracy + strong Coding + Advanced Aptitude |

> The exact score thresholds for each band are **not publicly disclosed** by TCS and vary by year and campus. [VERIFY]

| Sub-section | Topics | Questions (approx.) | Time (approx.) |

## Key Structural Rules (Commonly Reported)

1. **Sectional Time Limits** — Each sub-section has its own timer; you cannot carry over unused time. [VERIFY]
2. **No switching between sections** — Once you move to the next section, you cannot return. [VERIFY]
3. **Negative Marking** — Widely reported as **0.33 marks deducted per wrong answer** in objective questions, but this has varied. [VERIFY with official instructions shown at the start of your exam]
4. **MCQ format** — Foundation and Advanced Aptitude are multiple-choice (4 options, 1 correct).
5. **Coding section** — Evaluated on correctness of output for test cases, not just compilation.
