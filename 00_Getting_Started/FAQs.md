# TCS NQT — Frequently Asked Questions

> Items tagged **[VERIFY]** indicate areas where official TCS documentation is unclear, has changed across cycles, or where community-sourced information conflicts. Cross-check these against your **official call letter, TCS iON portal, and the exam instructions screen** before acting on them.

## Section A — Before the Exam

---

### FAQ 1 — Is there negative marking in TCS NQT?

**Answer:** Yes, negative marking is **widely reported** for the objective (MCQ) sections of TCS NQT. The most commonly cited deduction is **−1/3 mark (approximately −0.33)** per wrong answer. [VERIFY — the exact penalty can differ by section and cycle]

**Implication:** Do not guess randomly. If you can eliminate 2 of 4 options, the expected value of guessing from the remaining 2 is positive (+0.17 net). If you cannot eliminate any option, skipping is safer.

---

### FAQ 2 — What programming languages are allowed in the Coding section?

**Answer:** TCS NQT commonly supports: **C, C++, Java, Python (2.x and 3.x)** [VERIFY for your specific cycle — language options appear on-screen at the start of the Coding section]. Some cycles have also included Perl and Ruby. [VERIFY]

**Recommendation:** Use whichever language you are most comfortable writing and debugging quickly. Python is recommended for speed of coding; C++ for performance on large inputs; Java if that's your strongest language.

---

### FAQ 3 — Can I switch between sections during the exam?

**Answer:** No — TCS NQT has **separate timers per section**. Once a section's timer ends, you are automatically moved forward. You **cannot go back** to a previous section. [VERIFY — this is the widely reported format but exam instructions should be read carefully]

---

### FAQ 4 — Is a calculator allowed?

**Answer:** An **on-screen basic calculator** may be available on the TCS iON platform. [VERIFY — it has been reported in some cycles but not others] Physical calculators are NOT allowed. Practice doing approximate calculations mentally so you are not dependent on a calculator.

---

### FAQ 5 — How many times can I take the TCS NQT?

**Answer:** You can appear for TCS NQT **once per recruitment cycle**. Multiple attempts within the same cycle are not allowed. You may appear again in a future cycle if eligible. [VERIFY eligibility criteria — year of graduation, CGPA cutoffs, and attempt limits vary by cycle and by whether you are a fresher or have already been placed]

### FAQ 6 — What is the eligibility criteria for TCS NQT?

**Answer (commonly reported criteria):** [VERIFY ALL against current TCS NQT announcement]
- Full-time B.E./B.Tech/M.E./M.Tech/MCA/M.Sc. (CS/IT streams eligible; some non-CS streams allowed) [VERIFY]
- Minimum 60% or 6.0 CGPA throughout 10th, 12th, and graduation [VERIFY — some cycles use 60%, some 65%]
- No active backlog at time of appearing [VERIFY]
- Graduation year within the specified window (e.g., 2024/2025 passouts) [VERIFY for your cycle]
- Not more than 2 years gap in academics (in some cycles) [VERIFY]

---

### FAQ 7 — What CGPA / percentage is required?

**Answer:** The most commonly reported cutoff is **60% (or 6.0 CGPA on a 10-point scale)** in 10th, 12th, and college aggregate. Some cycles have required 65%. [VERIFY against your specific cycle's eligibility notification on the TCS NQT registration portal]

---

### FAQ 8 — Do I need to register separately for Ninja, Digital, and Prime?

**Answer:** No. You take **one exam**; your performance determines which band you qualify for. However, you may need to indicate your preference during registration. [VERIFY — some cycles require you to opt-in for Advanced/Prime sections at registration time; others allow all candidates to attempt all sections]

---

### FAQ 9 — Is the Coding section mandatory?

**Answer:** The Coding section is **mandatory for Digital/Prime band qualification** but may not affect Ninja band eligibility (which depends primarily on the Foundation section). [VERIFY — this has been reported inconsistently; some cycles count partial Coding towards Foundation band too]

---

### FAQ 10 — Is there an Email Writing / Essay section?

**Answer:** An Email Writing task has been reported in several TCS NQT cycles — typically a scenario where you must write a formal email within a word limit. Its presence, scoring weight, and whether it is part of the Foundation or a separate section has **varied across cycles**. [VERIFY against your call letter] Prepare for it regardless — it takes only 10–15 minutes of practice and costs you nothing to be ready.

## Section B — On Exam Day

---

### FAQ 11 — What documents do I need to carry to the exam centre?

**Answer (commonly required):** [VERIFY against your specific call letter]
- Printed copy of your Hall Ticket / Admit Card (downloaded from TCS iON portal)
- Any one government-issued photo ID: Aadhaar card, PAN card, Passport, or Voter ID
- College ID card (sometimes required) [VERIFY]
- Passport-size photograph (sometimes required) [VERIFY]

---

### FAQ 12 — How early should I arrive at the test centre?

**Answer:** Official reporting time is stated on your call letter — typically **60–90 minutes before the scheduled slot**. [VERIFY exact time from your specific call letter] Arrive early enough to complete biometric registration, seat allocation, and test login without rushing.

---

### FAQ 13 — What happens if I fail TCS NQT — can I retake it?

**Answer:** If you do not clear TCS NQT in one cycle, you can attempt it in the **next cycle** if you are still within the eligibility window (graduation year, CGPA, etc.). There is no permanent ban. However, if you have already been selected through a previous NQT, you may not be eligible to re-appear. [VERIFY the exact retake policy for your graduation year on the TCS NQT portal]

---

### FAQ 14 — Does TCS NQT score expire?

**Answer:** TCS NQT scores are typically **valid for one recruitment cycle** and are not carried forward indefinitely. [VERIFY — TCS has sometimes allowed recent scores to be used for multiple drives within the same academic year, but this varies] Do not assume your score from a previous year is valid for a new cycle.

---

### FAQ 15 — Will I get my score / result immediately after the exam?

**Answer:** No. Results are not shown immediately on screen after submission. TCS releases results (typically pass/fail or band qualification) via **email to your registered address and on the TCS iON portal**, usually within a few days to a few weeks of the exam. [VERIFY — timelines have varied widely across cycles]

The specific numerical score breakdown may or may not be shared with candidates depending on the cycle. [VERIFY]
