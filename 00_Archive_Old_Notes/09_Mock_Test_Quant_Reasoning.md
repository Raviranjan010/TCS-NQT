## 🔢 SECTION 1: NUMERICAL ABILITY (Q1 – Q20)

### Q1. (MCQ)
If $A$'s income is $25\%$ more than $B$'s income, by what percentage is $B$'s income less than $A$'s income?
* (A) $20\%$
* (B) $25\%$
* (C) $16.67\%$
* (D) $33.33\%$
* **Hint**: Use the base adjustment formula: $\frac{x}{100+x} \times 100\%$.
* **Solution**: 
  * *Pattern ID*: Num-Mock-01
  * *Approach*: Let B's income $= 100$. Then A's income $= 125$. The difference $= 25$. Percentage less $= \frac{25}{125} \times 100\% = 20\%$.
  * *Shortcut*: $25\% = 1/4$ increase $\implies$ decrease is $1/(4+1) = 1/5 = 20\%$.
  * *Trap*: Assuming B's income is 25% less than A's directly.

### Q2. (Non-MCQ)
The population of a city grows by $20\%$ in the first year and by $15\%$ in the second year. If the population at the end of the second year is 13,800, find the original population at the start of the first year.  
* **Hint**: Let the original population be $P$. Apply successive multipliers: $P \times 1.20 \times 1.15 = 13,800$.
* **Solution**:
  * *Pattern ID*: Num-Mock-02
  * *Approach*: Set up the equation: $P \times 1.38 = 13,800 \implies P = 10,000$.
  * *Shortcut*: Net growth $= 20 + 15 + \frac{20 \times 15}{100} = 38\%$. Thus, $138\%$ of $P = 13,800 \implies P = 10,000$.
  * *Trap*: Simply adding the percentages to get $35\%$ growth.

## 🧩 SECTION 2: REASONING ABILITY (Q21 – Q40)

### Q21. (MCQ)
Find the missing term in the sequence: **5, 11, 23, 47, 95, ?**
* (A) 190
* (B) 191
* (C) 192
* (D) 193
* **Hint**: Each term is related to the double of the previous term.
* **Solution**:
  * *Pattern ID*: Reas-Mock-01
  * *Approach*: Test the multiplier rule: $T_n = 2 T_{n-1} + 1$.
  * *Solution*: $95 \times 2 + 1 = 191$.
  * *Shortcut*: Constant second difference table also yields 191.
  * *Trap*: Calculating difference as constant odd numbers.
